## "Truth or Drink: Gen Z Edition"

---

## Demographics & Energy

| **Attribute** | **Specification** |
|---|---|
| **Age Range** | 20–25 (born 1999–2004) |
| **Personality Types** | Emotionally intelligent oversharer, anxious-but-self-aware introvert, chaotic-good extrovert, quietly competitive thinker |
| **Diversity** | Non-negotiable across race, ethnicity, gender expression, sexuality, body type, and socioeconomic background. We want people who *look like the group chat*, not a stock photo. |
| **Energy Baseline** | "Third hangout" energy — past small talk, not yet fully comfortable. That's where the gold is. |

We're casting for **micro-tension**, not performance. The best Truth or Drink moments come from the half-second *before* someone decides to answer — the eye dart, the nervous laugh, the hand reaching for the glass. We need people whose faces *broadcast* internal conflict.

---

## Character Archetypes Needed

We don't script archetypes — we **cast for latent archetypes** that emerge under pressure. Here's what we're scouting:

- **The Hero** — Not the loudest person. The one who answers the hardest question honestly and makes the other person feel safe enough to do the same. Emotional courage > bravado.
- **The Villain** — Reframed: the person who asks the follow-up question the audience is screaming. Not cruel — *relentless*. They don't let you off the hook with a vague answer.
- **The Underdog** — Seems quiet in the first 90 seconds. By minute four, they've said the most memorable thing in the video. Slow-burn presence.
- **The Overconfident** — Walks in saying "I'm an open book." Gets humbled by question three. The *crack* in their armor is the content.
- **The Wildcard** — Genuinely unpredictable. Not "random for the sake of random" — they just process the world differently. Their answers make you pause and rewatch.
- **The Super Fun** — Pure joy energy. Laughs *with* people, not at them. Their comfort is contagious and gives the other player permission to be messy.

**Ideal pairing:** One person who leans Overconfident/Villain + one who leans Underdog/Hero. The asymmetry creates narrative arc. [[H01:asymmetric archetype pairing drives higher completion rates|We hypothesize that mismatched confidence levels generate stronger viewer retention than two equally extroverted players]].

---

## What We Value (In Order)

1. **Comfort on camera under pressure** — Can they sit in an awkward silence without performing? Do they fidget authentically or freeze up? We test this in pre-interviews by asking one unexpected personal question and watching the *recovery*, not the answer.
2. **Experience with vulnerability formats** — Have they ever been on a podcast, filmed a "get ready with me" where they actually talked about something real, gone live during an emotional moment? We don't need polished — we need *practiced at being human on camera*.
3. **Social native fluency** — They understand the grammar of short-form content intuitively. They know what a "moment" feels like because they've consumed thousands of them. They won't over-explain a joke or under-sell a revelation.
4. **Group chemistry instinct** — In a two-person format, can they *volley*? Do they listen to respond or listen to connect? We pair-test finalists over a 10-minute Zoom with zero prompts and watch the conversational rhythm.

---

## Red Flags to Avoid

- **Follower count as a vanity metric** — 500K followers means nothing if their comments are all bot emojis. We'd rather cast someone with 2,000 followers whose audience actually *talks back to them*.
- **Traditional acting credits** — Trained actors default to "performance mode" under pressure. We need people who default to *honesty mode*. Theater kids are fine — but only if they've unlearned the instinct to project.
- **Brand deal history** — Anyone whose first instinct is "how will this affect my brand?" will self-censor at exactly the wrong moment. We need people who forget the camera is there by question two.
- **"I'm so crazy" energy in the audition** — If someone is trying to be a character in the casting call, they'll be exhausting on set. Authenticity isn't a volume setting.
- **Inability to say "I don't know"** — If a person fills every silence with noise, they'll flatten the emotional texture of the video.

---

## Gender Mix & Regional Voice

| **Element** | **Approach** |
|---|---|
| **Gender Mix** | Fluid. We're not defaulting to M/F pairings. Non-binary, trans, and gender-nonconforming talent are actively sought. The pairing should feel *unexpected* to the audience — not a dating show setup. |
| **Regional Voice** | Prioritize non-coastal voices. Gen Z in Atlanta, Detroit, Albuquerque, and rural Oregon have stories that don't get platformed. Regional slang, accents, and cultural reference points add texture and broaden audience identification. |
| **Language** | Bilingual moments are welcome and should not be edited out. Code-switching is authentic Gen Z behavior. |

[[H02:non-coastal casting broadens geographic engagement|We hypothesize that featuring talent from underrepresented regions increases share rates in those DMAs by 30%+]].

---

## Casting North Star

> **We are looking for two strangers who are brave enough to be ordinary on camera.** Not influencers, not performers — people whose real lives are interesting because they've actually *lived* them. The ideal cast makes the audience feel like they're eavesdropping on a conversation they weren't supposed to hear, and they can't look away. When one player tears up or bursts out laughing, the viewer should feel it in their chest. **That's the cast.**

[[H03:stranger pairing outperforms acquaintance pairing for Gen Z|We hypothesize that strangers generate 2x the emotional surprise moments compared to friends, because there's no shared context to hide behind]].

# TRUTH OR DRINK: GEN Z EDITION — FULL VIDEO SCRIPT

**Target Duration:** 5–7 minutes (edited)
**Talent:** Two strangers, ages 20–25 (referred to as **PLAYER A** and **PLAYER B**)
**Set:** CUT Analytics signature clean backdrop — matte black, single warm overhead pendant light, minimalist white table, two chairs, bottle of mid-shelf tequila (or non-alcoholic alternative clearly visible), two shot glasses, card deck center-frame.

## COLD OPEN (0:00–0:25)

*No music. No title card yet. We open MID-CONVERSATION — the cameras have already been rolling during the "get to know you" small talk. We cut in at the exact moment it gets real.*

**PLAYER A:**
*(mid-sentence, laughing nervously)*
—wait, so we're just gonna *do* this? With a stranger?

**PLAYER B:**
*(grinning, adjusting in their chair)*
I mean… I told my roommate I was doing this and she said, "You're gonna cry on the internet."

**PLAYER A:**
That's so specific.

**PLAYER B:**
She knows me.

*SMASH CUT TO:*

**TITLE CARD** — clean white text on black:

### **TRUTH OR DRINK**
*Gen Z Edition*

*One-second beat of silence. Then:*

**PLAYER B:**
*(to camera)*

## ROUND 1 — WARM-UP: "THE EASY ONES" (0:55–2:00)

*On-screen text: ROUND 1 — WARM-UP*

*Player A draws a card. Reads aloud.*

**PLAYER A:**
*(reading)*
"What's the most embarrassing thing on your screen time report?"

**PLAYER B:**
*(immediately)*
Oh no.

**PLAYER A:**
You can drink!

**PLAYER B:**
No, no, I'll say it. It's… LinkedIn. I spend like two hours a day on LinkedIn.

**PLAYER A:**
*LinkedIn?!*

**PLAYER B:**
I'm not even applying to jobs! I just like the drama in the comments!

*(Both laugh. The ice cracks.)*

---

*Player B draws.*

**PLAYER B:**
*(reading)*
"What's a lie you've told this week that you're not proud of?"

**PLAYER A:**
*(long pause — this is the first real silence. Hold it. Don't cut.)*
…I told my mom I was doing fine.

**PLAYER B:**
*(softly)*
Yeah.

**PLAYER A:**
I'm not, like, *not* fine. But I'm not… the version of fine she needs me to be.

*(Beat. Player B nods. No joke. No deflection. This is the moment the video finds its emotional register.)*

## ROUND 2 — "IT GETS PERSONAL" (2:00–3:45)

*On-screen text: ROUND 2 — OKAY, IT'S GETTING REAL*

*Player A draws.*

**PLAYER A:**
*(reading, then wincing)*
"What's something you pretend to not care about that actually keeps you up at night?"

**PLAYER B:**
*(reaches for the bottle, hesitates, pulls hand back)*
…Whether people actually like me or just tolerate me.

**PLAYER A:**
*(quietly)*
I feel that so hard.

**PLAYER B:**
Like, I *know* logically — but at 2 a.m.? Logic is not home.

**PLAYER A:**
Logic left the building. Logic is on vacation.

*(Small laugh. Relief. This is the rhythm — vulnerability, then levity, then back again.)*

---

*Player B draws.*

**PLAYER B:**
*(reading)*
"Have you ever been in love with someone who didn't love you back? What did you do about it?"

**PLAYER A:**
*(immediately pours a shot)*

**PLAYER B:**
Oh WOW.

**PLAYER A:**
*(takes the shot, grimaces)*
I will say — it was recent. And I *did not* handle it well.

**PLAYER B:**
How not-well are we talking?

**PLAYER A:**
I wrote a letter. A *physical* letter. On *stationery*.

**PLAYER B:**
*(hand over mouth)*
Did you *send* it?

**PLAYER A:**
*(dead-eyed into camera)*
I hand-delivered it.

**PLAYER B:**
NO.

**PLAYER A:**
To their *workplace*.

*(Player B collapses forward laughing. Player A is half-laughing, half-mortified. This is the clip. This is the one that goes everywhere.)*

## ROUND 3 — "THE DEEP END" (3:45–5:30)

*On-screen text: ROUND 3 — THE DEEP END 🫠*

*The energy shifts. Both players are leaning in now. The bottle is less of a prop and more of a safety net neither wants to use.*

*Player A draws.*

**PLAYER A:**
*(reading slowly)*
"What's something about your generation that you think we actually got wrong?"

*(Long pause from Player B.)*

**PLAYER B:**
I think we… confused being aware of our trauma with actually healing from it. Like, I can name all my attachment styles, I can tell you exactly why I self-sabotage — but I still *do it*. Knowing the vocabulary isn't the same as doing the work.

**PLAYER A:**
*(nodding)*
We therapize each other but we don't actually go to therapy.

**PLAYER B:**
Or we go to therapy and then post about it instead of sitting with it.

*(Silence. Let it breathe. This is the comment section moment — the one that starts a thousand "this changed me" replies.)*

---

*Player B draws the final card.*

**PLAYER B:**
*(reading)*
"What's one thing you wish you could tell your younger self — but you know they wouldn't listen?"

**PLAYER A:**
*(eyes immediately glassy — they didn't expect this one)*
…That the people who leave aren't always the ones who are wrong.

*(Beat.)*

**PLAYER A:**
Sometimes you're the reason. And that's — that's okay. But you have to look at it.

**PLAYER B:**
*(quietly)*
Can I steal that answer? Because… yeah. Same.

*(They look at each other. Two strangers who, five minutes ago, didn't know each other's names. Something real just happened.)*

I was literally about to ask. I feel like you just saw my whole soul and you didn't even flinch.

**PLAYER B:**
That's because your soul is *chaotic* and I respect it.

*(Both laugh. Real laugh. The kind that makes your shoulders drop.)*

## POST-SCRIPT: PRODUCTION NOTES

- **Music:** Minimal. Lo-fi ambient *only* under Round 1. Rounds 2–3 should be **score-free** — silence and room tone are the emotional architecture.
- **Editing philosophy:** Long holds on faces during pauses. Never cut away from a genuine reaction to get a "better angle." The front-facing camera is the confessional. The side angle is the safety net.
- **Alternate content cuts:** Isolate the stationery/letter story as a **60-second vertical clip**. Isolate the "therapy language vs. actual healing" exchange as a **90-second standalone**. Both are high-share, high-comment candidates.
- **Accessibility:** Full captions (styled, not auto-generated). Audio descriptions available. Non-alcoholic alternative *always* visible and *never* treated as lesser.
