## Shooting Setup & Location

**Overall Feel:** Intimate, moody, confessional — like a late-night conversation that got too real. The set should feel like a liminal space between a therapy office and a house party. [[H01: minimal set design increases perceived authenticity|We hypothesize that stripping the set back forces viewer attention onto facial micro-expressions, boosting emotional engagement]].

**Set Design Table:**

| Element | Specification | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Backdrop | Matte black seamless paper (Savage #20) or painted cyc wall | Eliminates distraction, creates "void" intimacy |
| Table | Minimalist white round table, 30" diameter, matte finish | Clean contrast against dark backdrop; round shape removes "sides" |
| Chairs | Two matching mid-century stools, matte black, no armrests | Keeps body language open and readable |
| Hero Bottle | Premium clear glass bottle (vodka or tequila), centered | Visual anchor and tension object |
| Shot Glasses | Two matching clear shot glasses, slightly oversized | Visible liquid level for audience to track consumption |
| Pendant Light | Single warm-tone overhead pendant (2700K–3000K), hung ~4ft above table | Creates pool-of-light effect, suggests interrogation/confession |
| Floor | Black felt or black painted concrete | Sound dampening + visual continuity |
| Background Accent | Subtle warm Edison bulb string ~15ft behind talent (heavily bokeh'd) | Adds depth without distraction |

**Lighting Table:**

| Light | Unit | Position | Intensity | Color Temp | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Key Light | Aputure 300d III w/ Lantern 90 softbox | Overhead, slightly front, centered on table | 60% | 3200K | Soft, warm, flattering top-down wash |
| Fill Light (A) | Aputure Amaran 200d w/ dome diffusion | 45° camera-left, 5ft from talent | 25% | 3200K | Gentle fill on Player A's face |
| Fill Light (B) | Aputure Amaran 200d w/ dome diffusion | 45° camera-right, 5ft from talent | 25% | 3200K | Gentle fill on Player B's face |
| Hair/Rim Light | 2× Aputure MC Pro (RGB pocket) | Behind each talent, low, angled up | 15% | 3000K warm amber | Subtle edge separation from black bg |
| Practical | Edison pendant (seen in frame) | Directly above table center | Dimmed to 40% | 2700K | Motivated source; mood-setting |
| Background | 1× Aputure MC Pro | On floor behind talent, aimed at string lights | 10% | 2700K | Bokeh depth texture |

**Lighting Ratio:** Approximately 3:1 key-to-fill. Shadows should be present but not harsh — we want "confessional," not "interrogation."

## Pacing & Editing Notes

### Act-by-Act Structure Table

| Act | Timestamp (Edited) | Content | Energy Level | Card Difficulty | Editing Style |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Cold Open** | 0:00–0:15 | Best reaction moment from later in shoot (flash-forward hook) | 🔥 High | N/A | Jump cut, smash to title card |
| **ACT 1 — Meet & Warm-Up** | 0:15–1:30 | Introductions, first impressions, rules explained; 2–3 easy questions | 😊 Light/Playful | Level 1 (surface) | Wider shots, natural pacing, let chemistry breathe; light text overlays for names/ages |
| **ACT 2 — The Middle Ground** | 1:30–3:30 | Questions about dating, social media habits, money; first real drinks taken | 😬 Rising Tension | Level 2 (personal) | Tighter shots start mixing in; reaction cuts increase; music bed subtly shifts |
| **ACT 3 — The Deep End** | 3:30–5:30 | Mental health, family, identity, regret; most vulnerable answers | 😢🔥 Peak Emotional | Level 3 (vulnerable) | Mostly close-ups; slow-mo on key reactions; longer holds on silence; music drops out for biggest moments |
| **ACT 4 — Cooldown & Close** | 5:30–6:30 | Final fun question to release tension; players reflect on experience; goodbye | 😄 Warm/Reflective | Level 1 (light) | Return to wide shot; warm color push; genuine laughter moments |
| **End Card** | 6:30–6:45 | CUT Analytics logo, subscribe CTA, "Comment which question you'd drink on" | — | — | Branded template; engagement hook |

### Editing Principles

- **Jump cuts are the language** — use them liberally within answers to maintain pace and remove dead air, but **never** cut away during a genuine emotional moment. Let those breathe.
- **Silence is a weapon** — hold on a face 1–2 seconds longer than comfortable when someone is deciding whether to drink or answer. [[H02: extended silence before a drink decision increases watch-through rate|We believe the 2-second "decision hold" is the single highest-retention editing beat in the format]].
- **Sound design > music** — use a minimal lo-fi/ambient music bed (think: Trent Reznor-lite) at low levels. Pull music OUT entirely for the most vulnerable moments. The sound of the bottle pouring and glass hitting the table should be crisp and ASMR-satisfying.
- **Text overlays** — Gen Z-native lower thirds: player name, age, pronouns (if offered). Occasional pop-up text for especially quotable lines (TikTok-clip-ready). Use a clean sans-serif (Inter or Satoshi), white with subtle drop shadow.
- **Color grade** — Warm, slightly desaturated, filmic. Skin tones are sacred — never sacrifice them for a "look." Subtle vignette to reinforce the pool-of-light set design.
- **Vertical cuts** — Editor produces 3–5 standalone 30–60 second vertical clips (9:16) for TikTok/Reels/Shorts simultaneously. Each clip should be a self-contained moment with hook → tension → payoff.
- **Pacing target** — Average shot length of 2.5–4 seconds in Acts 1–2, extending to 4–8 seconds in Act 3. Overall edit should feel like it's *slightly* faster than the viewer expects — always leave them wanting one more question.

## Key Risks & Contingencies

| Risk | Impact | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Talent over-intoxication** | Unusable footage, liability, ethical concern | Medium | Cap at 4–5 shots per person max; have water/snacks on set; PA monitors intake; offer non-alcoholic option at any time; have rideshare vouchers for post-shoot |
| **Low chemistry between strangers** | Flat, unengaging content; poor retention | Medium-High | Cast 3 pairs, shoot 2, publish the best 1; casting brief emphasizes energy matching; Director warms talent up with 10-min off-camera conversation before rolling |
| **Talent refuses to answer AND drink (dead card)** | Pacing stalls, breaks format tension | Medium | Have 30 cards prepared for 12–15 used; Director can swap in a replacement question seamlessly; "wildcard" cards let the OTHER player write a question |
| **Audio issues (lav rustle, HVAC noise)** | Unusable audio on key moments | Low-Medium | Boom mic as redundant backup; sound check before every take; kill all HVAC during recording; PAs enforce set silence |
| **Talent discomfort / emotional distress** | Ethical concern, potential bad PR, talent walks | Low | Director briefs talent on safe word ("pause") to stop at any time; no question is mandatory; post-shoot debrief offered; mental health resource card provided |
| **Set/lighting malfunction** | Delays, continuity breaks | Low | Backup bulbs for practical pendant; spare Aputure MC units; gaffer on standby throughout shoot |
| **Footage doesn't protect 9:16 crop** | Vertical social cuts are unusable | Medium | DP frames all shots with 9:16 safe area marked on monitors; A-Cam center-framed at all times |
| **Legal/release issues** | Cannot publish content | Low | All releases signed BEFORE talent enters set; PA double-checks paperwork; legal template pre-approved by counsel |

[[H03: casting three pairs and selecting the best reduces reshoot probability by ~70%|Overcasting is our primary insurance policy against flat content]].

# Truth or Drink: Gen Z Edition — 4-Scene Storyboard

**Concept:** Two strangers (ages 20–25) sit across from each other at a minimalist table, asking increasingly personal questions about dating, mental health, money, and identity. They can answer honestly — or take a shot. The tension between vulnerability and avoidance is the engine of the video.

---

### SCENE 1 — THE HOOK & HELLO (0:00–1:30)

**Visual:** Cold open on a TIGHT CLOSE-UP — Player B mid-laugh, hand over mouth, eyes wide. We hear Player A's voice off-screen: *"Wait, you actually did that?!"* SMASH CUT to black. Title card: **TRUTH OR DRINK: GEN Z EDITION**. Beat. WIDE SHOT fades in — two strangers sitting across the table, bottle between them, warm light overhead. They introduce themselves.

| Shot | Camera | Framing | Action | Audio |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1A | B-Cam | ECU Player B face | Flash-forward reaction — genuine shock/laughter | Natural audio, no music |
| 1B | — | Black frame | Title card animates in (clean, bold, white on black) | Low bass hit + silence |
| 1C | A-Cam | Wide two-shot | Players see each other for the first time; introductions | Soft ambient music bed fades in |
| 1D | B-Cam / C-Cam | Alternating MCUs | "What's your name? How old are you? First impression of me?" | Lavs + room tone |
| 1E | A-Cam | Wide | Director (off-screen) explains rules; first card is drawn | Director VO; music dips |

**Mood:** Nervous energy, first-date awkwardness, giggly. Light.

---

### SCENE 2 — WARMING UP / FIRST DRINKS (1:30–3:30)

**Visual:** The questions get more personal. We see the first hesitation — a player picks up the card, reads it, and their face changes. The camera SLOWLY PUSHES IN. They reach for the bottle. The sound of liquid pouring is crisp and deliberate. The other player reacts.

| Shot | Camera | Framing | Action | Audio |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2A | C-Cam | MCU Player A | Reads a dating question aloud; smirks nervously | Lav audio; music bed at low level |
| 2B | B-Cam | MCU Player B | Processes the question; eyes dart to the bottle | Silence hold (2 sec) |
| 2C | A-Cam | Wide (slow push) | Player B reaches for bottle, pours, drinks | Pour SFX emphasized; glass-on-table thud |
| 2D | C-Cam | MCU Player A | Reaction — "Oh, so it's like THAT" | Natural laughter; music swells slightly |
| 2E | B-Cam | MCU Player B | Answers the NEXT question honestly instead — surprising vulnerability | Music drops; voice isolated |

**Mood:** Playful tension building. The drinks start flowing. Laughter mixes with genuine surprise.

---

### SCENE 3 — THE DEEP END (3:30–5:30)

**Visual:** The energy shifts. A question about mental health or family lands. One player goes quiet. The wide shot holds. Then they speak — and it's real. The other player listens. No jokes. The camera is TIGHT on faces. We see eyes glisten. This is the emotional core of the video.

| Shot | Camera | Framing | Action | Audio |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 3A | B-Cam | Tight CU Player A | Reads the hardest question of the set — voice softens | Voice only; music fully out |
| 3B | C-Cam | Tight CU Player B | Long pause. Looks down. Looks back up. Begins to answer honestly. | Room tone + voice; 3-second silence hold before answer |
| 3C | A-Cam | Wide (static) | Both players in frame; the space between them feels smaller now | Ambient only |
| 3D | B-Cam | Tight CU Player A | Listening — nodding, empathetic; maybe tears up | No music; natural audio |
| 3E | C-Cam (60fps) | Slow-mo CU Player B | The moment after the answer — exhale, small smile, relief | Slow-mo audio design; soft tone fades in |
| 3F | A-Cam | Wide | Player A: "Can I give you a hug?" — they reach across the table | Natural audio; music returns gently |

**Mood:** Vulnerable, raw, human. This is the clip that goes viral. This is why people watch.

---

### SCENE 4 — THE RELEASE & GOODBYE (5:30–6:45)

**Visual:** The final card is light — a palate cleanser. Laughter returns. They cheers. They reflect on the experience. Wide shot holds as they stand, hug, and walk off set in opposite directions. The bottle and two empty glasses remain on the table. Hold on the empty set for 3 seconds. End card.

| Shot | Camera | Framing | Action | Audio |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 4A | A-Cam | Wide | Final fun question — both laugh, energy lifts | Music bed returns (warmer, major key) |
| 4B | B-Cam / C-Cam | Alternating MCUs | "What was the hardest question for you?" — genuine reflection | Natural audio |
| 4C | A-Cam | Wide | They cheers, clink glasses, drink together | Glass clink SFX; laughter |
| 4D | A-Cam | Wide | They stand, hug, say goodbye | Music swells to gentle peak |
| 4E | A-Cam | Wide (static hold) | Empty set — table, bottle, two glasses, warm light | Music fades; 3-sec silence; end card |

**Mood:** Warm, cathartic, hopeful. The viewer feels like they just witnessed something real.

# TRUTH OR DRINK: GEN Z EDITION — FULL VIDEO SCRIPT

**Target Duration:** 5–7 minutes (edited)
**Talent:** Two strangers, ages 20–25 (referred to as **PLAYER A** and **PLAYER B**)
**Set:** CUT Analytics signature clean backdrop — matte black, single warm overhead pendant light, minimalist white table, two chairs, bottle centered.

---

### PRE-ROLL / COLD OPEN (0:00–0:15)

> *[TIGHT CU — Player B's face. Mid-reaction. Eyes wide, mouth open, hand reaching for shot glass.]*

**PLAYER A (O.S.):**
"Wait — you're telling me you actually sent that text?!"

> *[Player B breaks into shocked laughter, grabs the shot glass, throws it back.]*

> *[SMASH CUT TO BLACK]*

> *[TITLE CARD fades in: **TRUTH OR DRINK: GEN Z EDITION** — clean white text on black. Bass hit.]*

---

### ACT 1 — THE MEET (0:15–1:30)

> *[WIDE SHOT fades in. Two strangers sit across from each other. Bottle between them. Warm overhead light. They've never met.]*

**DIRECTOR (O.S.):**
"Alright — introduce yourselves."

**PLAYER A:**
"Hi! I'm [Name], I'm [age], and I'm... honestly kind of nervous right now."

**PLAYER B:**
"I'm [Name], [age], and same — my hands are literally shaking. *[laughs]* Hi."

**DIRECTOR (O.S.):**
"Okay, here's how this works. You're going to take turns drawing a card. You read the question out loud. The other person either answers honestly... or takes a shot. Simple. Ready?"

**BOTH:**
"Ready." / "Let's go." / "No." *[laughter]*

---

**CARD 1 — Level 1 (Warm-Up)**

> *[Player A draws a card. Reads it.]*

**PLAYER A:**
*[Reading]* "What was your first impression of me when you sat down?"

**PLAYER B:**
"Oh, okay, starting there — um... honestly? I thought you were way too attractive for me to be honest with. *[laughs]* Like, I immediately got nervous."

**PLAYER A:**
"Stop! *[laughing]* That's so sweet. I thought you looked like someone who's about to absolutely destroy me with these questions."

---

**CARD 2 — Level 1**

> *[Player B draws.]*

**PLAYER B:**
*[Reading]* "What's the most embarrassing thing on your phone's screen time report?"

**PLAYER A:**
"...I'm drinking. *[reaches for bottle]*"

**PLAYER B:**
"NO! It's the second question!"

**PLAYER A:**
*[Pouring]* "And I'm already drinking. That's how bad it is."

> *[Drinks. Winces. Both laugh.]*

---

### ACT 2 — GETTING REAL (1:30–3:30)

**CARD 3 — Level 2 (Personal)**

> *[Player A draws.]*

**PLAYER A:**
*[Reading, smile fading slightly]* "Have you ever stayed in a relationship longer than you should have? Why?"

> *[Beat. Player B looks at the bottle. Looks back at Player A. Decides to answer.]*

**PLAYER B:**
"Yeah. My last relationship — I

stayed way too long because I was terrified of being alone. Like, I knew by month four it wasn't right, but I convinced myself that 'good enough' was the same as 'good.' It took me almost a year to leave."

**PLAYER A:**
"...Damn. I felt that."

**PLAYER B:**
"Yeah. I think a lot of us do that — settle because the alternative is sitting with yourself, and that's scarier."

> *[Genuine moment. Brief silence. Camera holds.]*

---

**CARD 4 — Level 2**

> *[Player B draws.]*

**PLAYER B:**
*[Reading]* "What's something you pretend to have figured out but absolutely don't?"

**PLAYER A:**
"Money. One hundred percent money. I have a budget spreadsheet that I made at 2 AM once and I've literally never opened it again. I just Venmo people and pray."

**PLAYER B:**
*[Laughing]* "The 'close your eyes and tap Apple Pay' method."

**PLAYER A:**
"It's a lifestyle. A bad one."

---

**CARD 5 — Level 2**

> *[Player A draws.]*

**PLAYER A:**
*[Reading]* "Have you ever posted something online purely for validation — and then deleted it when it didn't perform?"

**PLAYER B:**
"...Okay, I'm drinking on this one."

**PLAYER A:**
"That IS your answer! The drink IS the answer!"

> *[Player B laughs, pours, drinks. Shakes head.]*

**PLAYER B:**
"We're all performing. Every single one of us. Next card."

---

### ACT 3 — THE DEEP END (3:30–5:30)

**CARD 6 — Level 3 (Vulnerable)**

> *[Player B draws. Reads silently first. Looks up.]*

**PLAYER B:**
*[Reading slowly]* "What's something you haven't forgiven yourself for?"

> *[Long pause. Player A stares at the bottle. Stares at Player B. The room is quiet.]*

**PLAYER A:**
"I, um... I wasn't there when my grandma passed. I was supposed to visit that weekend and I just — I didn't go. I was hungover and I said I'd go the next week. And there wasn't a next week."

> *[Voice cracks slightly. Player B reaches across the table and briefly touches Player A's hand.]*

**PLAYER B:**
"Thank you for telling me that."

**PLAYER A:**
*[Wiping eye, laughing softly]* "Cool, so I'm crying on the internet now. That's great."

---

**CARD 7 — Level 3**

> *[Player A draws.]*

**PLAYER A:**
*[Reading]* "Do you think your parents are proud of you — honestly?"

**PLAYER B:**
*[Immediately]* "My mom, yes. My dad... I don't know. I think he's proud of a version of me that doesn't fully exist. Like, he's proud of the degree and the job title, but if he knew the full picture — the anxiety, the therapy, the fact that I don't want the life he planned for me — I don't know if 'proud' is the word."

**PLAYER A:**
"That's the most Gen Z sentence ever spoken and it's also painfully real."

**PLAYER B:**
"We contain multitudes. And anxiety disorders."

> *[Both laugh — the kind of laugh that releases tension.]*

---

**CARD 8 — Level 3**

> *[Player B draws. Final deep card.]*

**PLAYER B:**
*[Reading]* "What's the loneliest you've ever felt, even when you were surrounded by people?"

> *[Player A looks at the bottle. Looks at the camera. Looks at Player B.]*

**PLAYER A:**
"My 21st birthday. I had like thirty people there. Everyone was singing. And I remember standing in the middle of it thinking, 'Not a single person here knows what's actually going on with me.' I smiled the whole night. Went home and cried in the shower."

**PLAYER B:**
"I think that's the thing nobody talks about — you can be the life of the party and completely invisible at the same time."

> *[Beat. Both sit with it.]*

---

### ACT 4 — THE CLOSER (5:30–6:30)

**DIRECTOR (O.S.):**
"Last card. This one's a little different. Read it together."

> *[Both lean in to read a single card placed on the table.]*

**BOTH:**
*[Reading]* "Based on this conversation — would you be friends in real life?"

**PLAYER A:**
"Absolutely. Are you kidding me? You just held my hand while I cried about my grandma."

**PLAYER B:**
"I literally told you my deepest daddy issues. We're bonded for life now."

> *[Both laugh. Genuine warmth.]*

**PLAYER A:**
"I think this is what's missing, honestly. Like, we don't do this. We don't just... sit with a stranger and be real."

**PLAYER B:**
"Yeah. This was terrifying and also maybe the most honest conversation I've had in months."

> *[They clink their glasses — one full, one mostly empty.]*

**BOTH:**
"To strangers."

> *[They drink. Smile. Hold eye contact. Cut to black.]*

---

### END CARD / OUTRO

> *[Text on screen, clean white on black:]*

**"They exchanged numbers after filming."**

> *[Beat.]*

**"They've hung out twice since."**

> *[CUT Analytics logo. Subscribe CTA. End screen with related videos.]*

## Pacing & Editing Notes

### Act-by-Act Pacing Table

| **Act** | **Timecode (Edited)** | **# of Cards** | **Emotional Register** | **Edit Pace** | **Key Edit Notes** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Cold Open** | 0:00–0:15 | 0 | Curiosity / Nervous energy | Fast | Jump-cut montage of best moments (no context); text overlay: "Two strangers. One bottle. No filter." Hook must land in 3 seconds. |
| **Act 1: Warm-Up** | 0:15–1:30 | 2 | Playful / Awkward / Flirty | Medium-fast | Quick cuts between reactions; let the first drink moment breathe for comedy; establish rhythm of card-read → answer/drink → reaction |
| **Act 2: Getting Real** | 1:30–3:30 | 3 | Reflective / Honest / Surprised | Medium | Longer holds on answers; fewer cuts; let silences exist (2-3 seconds); use Camera C inserts of hands/fidgeting to add texture without cutting away from emotion |
| **Act 3: The Deep End** | 3:30–5:30 | 3 | Vulnerable / Raw / Emotional | Slow | Minimal cuts; let Camera B close-ups dominate; hold on faces during pauses; if tears happen, DO NOT cut away — stay on the moment; subtle music bed fades in underneath |
| **Act 4: The Closer** | 5:30–6:30 | 1 (final card) | Warm / Connected / Hopeful | Medium → Slow fade | Final card read together; genuine reactions; end on the clink and eye contact; slow fade to black; text cards land with deliberate pacing |
| **Post-Credits Tag** | 6:30–6:50 | 0 | Light / Relief / BTS charm | Fast / Casual | Behind-the-scenes energy; shaky handheld okay; feels like a reward for watching to the end; boosts comments ("stay till the end!") |

### Editing Principles

- **Silence is content.** Gen Z audiences on YouTube respond to authentic pauses. Do not fill every gap with music or cuts. A 3-second silence before a vulnerable answer is more powerful than any edit.
- **Jump cuts for comedy, long takes for emotion.** Acts 1–2 can use CUT's signature jump-cut style. Acts 3–4 should slow down dramatically — the contrast IS the experience.
- **Reaction shots are the show.** The person *listening* is often more compelling than the person *talking*. Camera B's reaction coverage is the most valuable footage in the edit.
- **Music bed philosophy:** No music in Acts 1–2 (room tone + natural laughter is the soundtrack). Introduce a minimal lo-fi/ambient bed at the start of Act 3 — something warm and piano-driven, never overpowering dialogue. Music swells slightly under the final card and text cards. [[H03:test music vs no-music versions on engagement|A/B test a fully music-free version against the scored version to measure impact on average view duration]]
- **Text overlays:** Minimal. Use only for the cold open hook line, the end-card text reveals ("They exchanged numbers after filming"), and the subscribe CTA. No subtitles burned in — use YouTube's CC system.
- **Vertical recuts:** Shoot with 9:16 safe area in mind. Camera A's two-shot should have both players within center-frame vertical crop. Camera B close-ups are natively vertical-friendly. Plan for 3–5 short-form clips (60–90 sec each) pulled from the best moments for TikTok, Reels, and Shorts.
- **Thumbnail frame:** During the shoot, have the director call for a "thumbnail moment" — both players reacting to a question with exaggerated expressions, shot on Camera A. Capture 2–3 options. Thumbnail should feature both faces, the question card text overlaid, and a bold emotion (shock, laughter, tears).
- **Target final runtime:** 5:30–6:50 edited. Anything under 5 minutes feels rushed for the emotional arc. Anything over 7 minutes risks drop-off before the payoff in Act 4.

## Key Risks & Contingencies

| **Risk** | **Impact** | **Likelihood** | **Mitigation** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Talent clams up / low energy | Dead footage; no usable emotional beats; video feels flat | Medium | Cast 3 pairs, shoot 2, publish 1. Director uses warm-up exercises pre-shoot (10 min off-camera conversation). Have backup "ice-breaker" cards that aren't in the main deck. |
| Talent gets too intoxicated | Slurred speech; liability issues; unusable footage; brand risk | Low-Medium | Use diluted spirits (half-strength) or low-ABV alternatives. PA tracks pour count — hard cap at 4 shots per person. Water and snacks available between acts. |
| Emotional distress / talent becomes upset | Talent shuts down or wants to stop; ethical and duty-of-care concerns | Medium | Director briefs talent pre-shoot: "You can pass on ANY question, no judgment." Safe word established to pause filming. PA has contact info for a counselor if needed. Signed release includes emotional content acknowledgment. |
| Audio issues (lav failure, HVAC noise) | Unusable audio on key moments; costly ADR or lost content | Low | Redundant audio: 2 lavs + boom + camera scratch audio. Sound check before every act. HVAC turned off during shooting (coordinate with facilities). |
| Lighting continuity breaks | Visual inconsistency between acts; distracting in edit | Low | Gaffer takes reference photos at top of each act. Pendant light on dimmer lock. No windows on set (controlled environment). |
| Low chemistry between strangers | Conversation feels forced; no natural connection; video underperforms | Medium-High | Casting process screens for complementary energy types (see casting brief). Pre-shoot "green room" time where both players chat without cameras. Shoot two pairings and select the stronger one in edit. |
| Talent backs out day-of | Scrambled schedule; wasted crew day | Low-Medium | Confirm talent 72 hrs, 24 hrs, and 2 hrs before call time. Have 1 standby player on call who can arrive within 1 hour. |
| Footage storage failure | Catastrophic data loss | Very Low | Dual-slot recording on all cameras. DIT or PA performs on-set backup to portable SSD after each act. No card is reformatted until backup is verified. |
| Video underperforms algorithmically | Low impressions; poor CTR; below-average view duration | Medium | Prepare 3 thumbnail options + 2 title variants for A/B testing. Front-load the cold open with the single most shocking/emotional moment. Publish short-form clips 24–48 hrs before the full video to build anticipation. |
| Talent requests removal post-publish | Legal/PR issue; video must be pulled; lost investment | Low | Comprehensive talent release signed pre-shoot covering perpetual usage rights across all platforms. Talent reviews final edit before publish (48-hr approval window). Transparent communication about what "going viral" might mean. |

### Truth or Drink: Gen Z Edition — 4-Scene Storyboard

**Concept:** Two strangers (ages 20–25) sit across from each other at a minimalist table, asking increasingly personal questions about dating, mental health, money, and identity. They can answer honestly — or take a shot. The tension between vulnerability and avoidance is the engine of every scene. [[H01:vulnerability escalation drives watch-time|Each scene escalates emotional stakes to test whether deeper honesty correlates with higher retention]].

---

**SCENE 1 — "THE MEET" (0:00–1:00)**

| **Element** | **Description** |
|---|---|
| Visual | Wide shot: empty set, pendant light glowing. PLAYER A enters frame left, PLAYER B enters frame right. They see each other for the first time. |
| Audio | Ambient room tone; natural laughter/nervousness. No music. |
| Action | Players introduce themselves. Brief small talk. Producer (off-camera) explains the rules. A bottle and two shot glasses are placed on the table. |
| Mood | Nervous energy, curiosity, sizing each other up. First impressions are forming in real time. |

**SCENE 2 — "WARMING UP" (1:00–2:30)**

| **Element** | **Description** |
|---|---|
| Visual | Medium two-shot, both players visible. Intercut with over-the-shoulder singles. |
| Audio | Natural dialogue. Light ambient music bed fades in (lo-fi, barely perceptible). |
| Action | Round 1 questions — lighter but still pointed: "What's the most unhinged thing in your screen time report?" / "Have you ever lied on a dating app? About what?" Players begin choosing truth or drink. |
| Mood | Playful, competitive, testing boundaries. First drinks are taken with bravado. |
| Key Shot | Reaction shot when someone gives an unexpectedly honest answer — the other player's face is the content. |
| Editing Note | Establish rhythm: question card → reader's face → answerer deliberating → answer OR drink → reaction. This pattern becomes the visual grammar of the video. |

---

**SCENE 3 — "THE DEEP END" (2:30–5:00)**

| **Element** | **Description** |
|---|---|
| Visual | Tighter framing — camera pushes in slightly compared to Scene 2. More close-ups. Shallow depth of field isolates faces. |
| Audio | Music drops out entirely during the most vulnerable answers. Room tone becomes intimate. |
| Action | Round 2 & 3 questions escalate: "What's something you pretend doesn't bother you but actually keeps you up at night?" / "Have you ever been ashamed of how much money your family has — or doesn't have?" / "Do you think you're hard to love?" Players are now genuinely wrestling with whether to answer. Drinks become an emotional tell — choosing to drink *is* an answer. |
| Mood | Vulnerable, surprising, occasionally heavy — punctuated by nervous laughter that breaks tension. This is the emotional core. [[H02:drink-as-avoidance signals emotional peaks|Testing whether moments where players choose to drink instead of answer generate higher comment engagement than honest answers]]. |
| Key Shot | A sustained 8–12 second unbroken close-up of a player processing a question before responding. No cuts. Let silence breathe. |
| Editing Note | Slow the pace. Longer holds on faces. If someone tears up, stay on them — don't cut away. The audience needs permission to feel. Use a subtle sound design choice (room tone shift, barely audible low hum) to signal emotional gear-change. |

---

**SCENE 4 — "THE LAST QUESTION & GOODBYE" (5:00–6:30)**

| **Element** | **Description** |
|---|---|
| Visual | Return to the wide two-shot from Scene 1 — visual bookend. Final question is shot in a single unbroken take. |
| Audio | No music. Pure dialogue and room tone. Music returns only in the final 15 seconds as players stand. |
| Action | Final question — each player asks the other one question of their own (not from the card deck). This is unscripted and unrehearsed. After answering, they reflect: "What surprised you about this?" They stand, hug or shake hands, and exit. |
| Mood | Cathartic, warm, unexpectedly connected. Two strangers who now know each other in ways most friends don't. |
| Key Shot | The hug/handshake — hold the wide shot and let them linger. If they exchange numbers, capture it. |
| Editing Note | End card overlays final shot. Include a CTA: "What question would YOU ask? Comment below." Fade to black. Subscribe animation. Total edited runtime target: **5:30–6:30**. |

## Production Notes for Script Execution

| **Note** | **Detail** |
|---|---|
| **Improvisation** | The script above is a **structural guide, not a verbatim script**. Real dialogue will emerge from casting and chemistry. Question cards are real; answers are never pre-planned. |

| **Session Length** | Plan to shoot 45–60 minutes of raw footage to yield 5:30–6:30 of edited content. The best moments are often in minute 30+, once guards are fully down. |
| **Pickup Lines** | After the main session, record 2–3 minutes of "exit interview" with each player separately: "What surprised you?" / "What will you think about tonight?" These become potential social clips or DVD-extra style bonus content. |

# Truth or Drink: Gen Z Edition — 4-Scene Storyboard

## Storyboard Overview

| **Scene** | **Title** | **Duration (Edited)** | **Emotional Arc** | **Key Visual** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | The Meet | 0:00–1:15 | Nervous energy, humor, curiosity | Wide two-shot — strangers sizing each other up, first laughs |
| 2 | The Warm-Up | 1:15–2:45 | Playful tension, light vulnerability | Medium singles — eyebrow raises, smirks, first drink taken |
| 3 | The Deep End | 2:45–5:00 | Raw honesty, emotional weight, surprise | Tight close-ups — eye contact, pauses, genuine reactions |
| 4 | The Cool-Down | 5:00–6:30 | Warmth, reflection, connection | Wide pull-back — laughter, hug or handshake, final toast |

---

### Scene 1: The Meet (0:00–1:15)

**Setup:** PLAYER A is already seated. PLAYER B walks in. They've never met.

**Shots:**

| **Shot #** | **Camera** | **Framing** | **Action** | **Audio Note** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1A | A (Wide) | Full two-shot, table centered | B walks in, sits down. They introduce themselves. Awkward smiles. | Natural room tone, no music |
| 1B | B (Medium) | Over-the-shoulder on A | A says first impression of B | Lav mic captures whispered aside |
| 1C | C (Detail) | Tabletop — bottle, shot glasses, card stack | Hands reach for first card | Foley emphasis on card slide |
| 1D | A (Wide) | Two-shot reframe | Both react to the first question being revealed | Capture the "oh no" beat |

**Mood Board Cues:** Think first-date jitters. Warm amber lighting. Slight lens flare from the pendant. Negative space around subjects emphasizes isolation before connection.

**Director Notes:**
- Let the silence breathe — don't rush introductions
- Encourage talent to verbalize first impressions ("You look like someone who…")
- Camera C should catch the bottle label and the ritual of pouring — this is visual foreshadowing

---

### Scene 2: The Warm-Up (1:15–2:45)

**Setup:** Light questions. The rules become clear through play. First drinks happen here.

**Shots:**

| **Shot #** | **Camera** | **Framing** | **Action** | **Audio Note** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2A | B (Medium) | Single on Player A | A reads a question, debates answering, answers honestly | Capture vocal hesitation |
| 2B | A (Medium) | Single on Player B | B reads a question, laughs, takes the drink instead | Glass clink, swallow, exhale |
| 2C | C (Detail) | Close-up on hands | Card flip, shot pour, drink lift | ASMR-adjacent sound design |
| 2D | A (Wide) | Two-shot | Both laughing after a surprising answer | Natural overlap dialogue |

**Sample Questions for This Beat:**
- "What's the most unhinged thing in your screen time report?"
- "Have you ever posted a thirst trap and then deleted it? Why?"
- "What's the biggest lie on your dating profile?"

**Director Notes:**
- This is where [[H01:vulnerability escalation drives watch-time|the escalation pattern begins]] — questions should feel fun but slightly uncomfortable
- First drink is a KEY moment — Camera C must be rolling; this becomes the thumbnail candidate
- If both players answer everything honestly, gently prompt: "You sure you don't want to drink on that one?"

---

### Scene 3: The Deep End (2:45–5:00)

**Setup:** Questions shift to mental health, money, identity, and real emotional territory. This is the core of the video.

**Shots:**

| **Shot #** | **Camera** | **Framing** | **Action** | **Audio Note** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 3A | B (Tight) | Close-up on Player A's face | A receives a deeply personal question — long pause before answering | Room tone only — no score |
| 3B | A (Tight) | Close-up on Player B's face | B's genuine reaction — empathy, surprise, recognition | Capture micro-expressions |
| 3C | C (Detail) | Hands, table surface | A player's hands fidgeting, gripping the glass, setting it down untouched (chose truth) | Amplify tactile sounds |
| 3D | A (Wide) | Two-shot, slightly longer lens | A moment of shared silence or unexpected emotional connection | Let it run 10+ seconds uncut |
| 3E | B (Medium) | Single on whoever is speaking | The "confession" moment — the answer that surprises even the speaker | Isolate voice, reduce room noise |

**Sample Questions for This Beat:**
- "What's something you've never told anyone because you're afraid of how it sounds?"
- "Do you think you're financially behind where you should be? Does it scare you?"
- "Have you ever stayed in a relationship because you were afraid of being alone?"
- "What's the thing about your generation that you actually think is broken?"
- "If your therapist could say one sentence to you right now, what would it be?"

**Director Notes:**
- **This is where the video lives or dies.** Protect this space.
- No crew talking, no interruptions. Minimal direction. Let talent lead.
- [[H02:drink-as-avoidance signals emotional peaks|When someone reaches for the bottle instead of answering, hold the shot]] — that avoidance IS the content
- If a player gets emotional, do NOT cut. Camera operators hold steady. The director gives a small nod to continue only if talent seems okay.
- Safe word protocol is active — if anyone says "pause," cameras keep rolling but the director steps in for a private check-in
- This section will likely run 8–12 minutes raw; editorial will sculpt it to ~2:15 of the most potent material

---

### Scene 4: The Cool-Down (5:00–6:30)

**Setup:** Final question, reflection, and farewell. Emotional release.

**Shots:**

| **Shot #** | **Camera** | **Framing** | **Action** | **Audio Note** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 4A | A (Wide) | Two-shot, original framing | Final question is read — both players decide together whether to answer or drink | Full ambient capture |
| 4B | B (Medium) | Single on Player A | A shares what surprised them about B | Warm, intimate vocal tone |
| 4C | A (Medium) | Single on Player B | B shares what surprised them about A | Mirror the intimacy |
| 4D | C (Detail) | Two glasses being raised | Final toast — clink, drink, set down | Crisp glass-on-glass foley |
| 4E | A (Wide) | Full two-shot, slow push-in | They stand, hug or handshake, genuine goodbye | Fade up subtle score underneath |
| 4F | A (Wide) | Hold on empty table | Both walk off frame. Bottle and cards remain. Beat. Cut to black. | Room tone fades to silence |

**Final Question (always the same — becomes a series signature):**
- "What's one thing you wish someone would ask you — that nobody ever does?"

**Director Notes:**
- [[H03:final-round retention cliff test|This scene tests whether emotional payoff retains viewers through the end]] — it must feel earned, not forced
- The final toast should feel ritualistic — pour slowly, make eye contact, drink together
- Shot 4F (empty table) is critical for the end card overlay and subscribe CTA
- If the hug/handshake happens organically, beautiful. If not, don't manufacture it. An awkward wave can be just as powerful.

## Script Notes for Production

| **Element** | **Note** |
|---|---|
| **Dialogue flexibility** | Script is a blueprint — not a teleprompter read. Talent should use their own words. The questions are fixed; the answers must be organic. |
| **Improvisation windows** | Acts 2 and 3 should have 60–70% improvised dialogue. Only the cold open, final question, and toast are semi-scripted. |
| **Director prompts** | Director may gently redirect if energy stalls: "Tell me more about that," "What does that feel like?" — never leading or editorial. |
| **Sensitivity language** | Avoid prompting with "That must be hard" or "I'm sorry." Let talent validate each other. Director stays neutral. |
| **Safe word** | If either player says **"bookmark"**, production pauses. No questions asked. Check-in happens off-camera. Talent decides whether to continue. |
| **Alcohol management** | Maximum 4 shots per player across the full shoot. Non-alcoholic alternative (matching shot glasses, identical liquid color) available at all times — no one should feel pressured to drink. |
| **Run time expectation** | Raw footage will be 25–40 minutes. Edited cut targets 5:30–6:30. Editor should prioritize emotional turning points and authentic laughter over completeness. |
