# TRUTH OR DRINK: GEN Z EDITION — FULL VIDEO SCRIPT

**Target Duration:** 5–7 minutes (edited)
**Talent:** Two strangers, ages 20–25 (referred to as **PLAYER A** and **PLAYER B**)
**Set:** CUT Analytics signature clean backdrop — matte black, single warm overhead pendant light, minimalist white table, two chairs, bottle of vodka, two shot glasses, and a stack of question cards.

---

## ACT I — COLD OPEN HOOK (0:00–0:15)

*[COLD OPEN — No music. Tight close-up on a shot glass being filled. The pour is loud. Cut to a JUMP-CUT montage of the spiciest moments from later in the video — reactions, gasps, someone mid-drink, someone burying their face in their hands. Audio is choppy, stacked:]*

**PLAYER A (V.O.):** "Wait — you *actually* did that?"

**PLAYER B (V.O.):** "I'm not answering that. Absolutely not."

*[Sound of a shot glass slamming down.]*

**PLAYER A (V.O.):** "Bro, that's like… really sad actually."

*[SMASH CUT TO BLACK. Beat of silence. Title card fades in: **TRUTH OR DRINK: GEN Z EDITION**. A single bass note hums underneath.]*

---

## ACT II, SCENE 1 — "SO… THIS IS AWKWARD" (0:15–1:30)

*[Wide two-shot on a 50mm lens. Centered symmetry. PLAYER A (she/her, 22, oversized vintage tee, silver rings) and PLAYER B (he/him, 24, buzz cut, gold chain, hoodie) sit across from each other. The bottle and glasses sit between them like a dare. They've never met. They fidget.]*

**PLAYER A:** *[small laugh, looking around]* "Okay so… hi."

**PLAYER B:** *[nodding slowly]* "Hey. This is weird."

**PLAYER A:** "It's very weird. I don't know anything about you."

**PLAYER B:** "Same. I know your name and that's literally it."

*[A PRODUCER's hand enters frame and places the stack of question cards on the table. Both players look down at it.]*

**PLAYER A:** "Oh, that's a thick stack."

**PLAYER B:** *[exhales through teeth]* "Yeah, I already regret this."

*[Both laugh. The tension cracks slightly. PLAYER A picks up the first card.]*

**PLAYER A:** "Okay, rules — I ask you, you ask me, and if you don't wanna answer…"

**PLAYER B:** *[gestures to the bottle]* "Liquid courage."

**PLAYER A:** "Or liquid avoidance."

**PLAYER B:** "Same thing."

*[Beat. They lock eyes.]*

**PLAYER A:** "Alright. Let's go."

---

## ACT II, SCENE 2 — ROUND 1: DATING & SITUATIONSHIPS (1:30–3:00)

*[Lower-third text fades in: **ROUND 1: DATING & SITUATIONSHIPS**. A subtle lo-fi beat fades in underneath.]*

### Question 1 — Player A asks Player B

**PLAYER A:** *[reading card, already smirking]* "What's the worst thing you've done to someone you were 'talking to'?"

**PLAYER B:** *[immediately looks away]* "Oh, we're starting HERE?"

**PLAYER A:** "We're starting here."

**PLAYER B:** *[long pause, rubbing his jaw]* "Okay… I once told a girl I deleted Hinge for her. I did not delete Hinge."

**PLAYER A:** *[gasps]* "You are the problem!"

**PLAYER B:** "I KNOW. I know. I was twenty-one. I was awful."

**PLAYER A:** "Did she find out?"

**PLAYER B:** "She saw the notification on my phone at brunch."

**PLAYER A:** *[hand over mouth]* "At BRUNCH?!"

**PLAYER B:** *[nodding, ashamed]* "At brunch."

*[Both burst out laughing. PLAYER B shakes his head.]*

---

### Question 2 — Player B asks Player A

**PLAYER B:** *[picks up next card, reads it, and immediately looks up]* "Oh this is crazy. Okay — [[H12:test vulnerability pacing|'Have you ever gone back to someone you publicly said you were done with?']]"

**PLAYER A:** *[dead stare into the camera]*

**PLAYER B:** "The silence is telling me everything."

**PLAYER A:** *[reaches for the bottle, then pulls her hand back]* "No. No, I'll answer. Yes. Twice. The same person."

**PLAYER B:** "TWICE?!"

**PLAYER A:** "He had a truck and a dog. I'm not made of stone."

**PLAYER B:** *[laughing hard]* "A truck and a DOG — that's your bar?!"

**PLAYER A:** "Don't judge me, Hinge Boy."

*[PLAYER B raises his hands in surrender.]*

---

### Question 3 — Player A asks Player B

**PLAYER A:** *[reading card]* "What's your body count, and do you add or subtract when you tell people?"

**PLAYER B:** *[instant reach for the bottle]* "Nope."

**PLAYER A:** "DRINK! Drink drink drink."

*[PLAYER B pours a shot, takes it clean, winces.]*

**PLAYER B:** "Some things die with me."

**PLAYER A:** "The drink told us everything we needed to know."

*[Cut to:]*

---

### CONFESSIONAL BEAT #1 (Interstitial)

*[Single talking-head shot. PLAYER B alone, different angle, tighter frame. He speaks directly into the lens.]*

**PLAYER B:** "I didn't think I'd be this nervous? Like, it's easy to be honest with your friends because they already know you're trash. A stranger? That's a mirror, bro. That's different."

*[Cut back to the table.]*

---

## ACT II, SCENE 3 — ROUND 2: MENTAL HEALTH & REAL LIFE (3:00–4:45)

*[Lower-third text: **ROUND 2: MENTAL HEALTH & THE REAL STUFF**. The lo-fi beat shifts to something slightly more muted, atmospheric. The lighting warms one degree — barely perceptible but felt.]*

### Question 4 — Player B asks Player A

**PLAYER B:** *[reading card, voice softening]* "Have you ever lied about being okay when someone checked in on you? Why?"

*[Pause. The energy shifts. PLAYER A's smile fades slowly.]*

**PLAYER A:** "Yeah. Like… all the time, honestly."

**PLAYER B:** *[quiet]* "Yeah?"

**PLAYER A:** "I think I got really good at performing 'fine' during COVID. Like, everyone was struggling, so I felt like I didn't have permission to also be struggling. So I just… wasn't. Publicly. But I was."

*[Beat. PLAYER B nods slowly.]*

**PLAYER B:** "That's real."

**PLAYER A:** *[lighter]* "Sorry, that got deep fast."

**PLAYER B:** "No — that's the point, right?"

*[They share a look. It lands.]*

---

### Question 5 — Player A asks Player B

**PLAYER A:** *[reading card]* "[[H07:test emotional pivot reaction|What's something you haven't forgiven yourself for?]]"

*[PLAYER B exhales. Long beat. He looks at the bottle. Looks back at PLAYER A.]*

**PLAYER B:** "I, uh… I didn't visit my grandma enough before she passed. I was like an hour away at school and I just… kept saying next weekend. And then there wasn't a next weekend."

*[Silence. PLAYER A reaches across the table and briefly touches his hand.]*

**PLAYER A:** "Thank you for saying that."

**PLAYER B:** *[clears throat, half-laughs]* "Damn. Okay. Moving on before I cry on the internet."

*[They both laugh, but it's warm. Genuine.]*

---

### Question 6 — Player B asks Player A

**PLAYER B:** *[reading card]* "Do you think your generation is more emotionally intelligent or just more emotionally performative?"

**PLAYER A:** *[eyebrows shoot up]* "Oh, this card came for NECKS."

**PLAYER B:** "Right?!"

**PLAYER A:** "Honestly? Both. I think we have the language now — like, 'boundaries' and 'attachment styles' — but sometimes we use the language to avoid actually doing the work. Like, I've definitely said 'I'm protecting my peace' when I was actually just being avoidant."

**PLAYER B:** "Wow. Okay, self-aware queen."

**PLAYER A:** "Therapy is expensive but the vocabulary is free."

*[Both crack up.]*

---

### CONFESSIONAL BEAT #2 (Interstitial)

*[Single talking-head. PLAYER A alone.]*

**PLAYER A:** "It's weird — I feel like I trust him? And I met him twenty minutes ago. I think there's something about both of you being equally exposed that just… levels the ground. Like, we're both embarrassed. That's bonding."

*[Cut back to the table.]*

---

## ACT II, SCENE 4 — ROUND 3: MONEY & IDENTITY (4:45–6:15)

*[Lower-third text: **ROUND 3: MONEY, AMBITION & IDENTITY**. Beat shifts again — slightly more energy, a subtle percussive loop.]*

### Question 7 — Player A asks Player B

**PLAYER A:** *[reading card]* "How much money is in your bank account right now?"

**PLAYER B:** *[laughs immediately]* "Why is this harder than the grandma question?!"

**PLAYER A:** "Because capitalism is our deepest shame."

**PLAYER B:** *[sighing]* "Like… checking or savings?"

**PLAYER A:** "Total. Everything."

**PLAYER B:** *[wincing]* "Probably like… $1,400?"

**PLAYER A:** "That's not bad!"

**PLAYER B:** "Rent is $1,200."

**PLAYER A:** *[slow nod]* "Oh. So you have two hundred dollars."

**PLAYER B:** "I have two hundred dollars and a dream."

*[PLAYER A pours herself a solidarity shot. They clink and drink together.]*

---

### Question 8 — Player B asks Player A

**PLAYER B:** *[reading card]* "Do you think you'll be more or less successful than your parents?"

**PLAYER A:** *[pause]* "Differently successful? Like, my mom owned a house at my age. That's literally mythological to me. But I think I'll be happier. I hope I'll be happier. She wasn't."

**PLAYER B:** *[quietly]* "That's a good answer."

**PLAYER A:** "I think our generation redefined success because we had to. The old definition wasn't available anymore."

---

### Question 9 — Player A asks Player B

**PLAYER A:** *[reading card, grinning]* "[[H19:test identity vulnerability as climax|What's something you pretend to like because it's part of your identity now and you're in too deep?]]"

**PLAYER B:** *[immediately]* "Jazz."

**PLAYER A:** *[SCREAMING]* "JAZZ?!"

**PLAYER B:** "I said I liked jazz on a first date THREE YEARS AGO and now everyone gets me jazz vinyl for my birthday. I have eleven jazz records. I don't like jazz."

**PLAYER A:** *[doubled over]* "You are TRAPPED!"

**PLAYER B:** "I'm in too deep! I can't come out now! I went to a jazz bar last month! I clapped after solos!"

**PLAYER A:** "You clapped after SOLOS?!"

**PLAYER B:** "I didn't know when the songs ended! I was just clapping when other people clapped!"

*[Both are in tears laughing. PLAYER A is slapping the table.]*

---

### Question 10 — Final Question: Player B asks Player A

**PLAYER B:** *[pulling the last card, reads it silently, then looks up]* "Okay. Last one. Heavy. 'What's the version of yourself you're most afraid other people see?'"

*[Long pause. The laughter from the jazz moment still lingers in the air, which makes the shift hit harder.]*

**PLAYER A:** *[thinking]* "The version that's trying too hard. I think I'm really scared that people can tell how badly I want to be liked. And that wanting to be liked that badly is, like… inherently unlikable."

*[Beat.]*

**PLAYER B:** "For what it's worth — from a stranger — I don't see that at all. I just see someone who's honest."

**PLAYER A:** *[eyes glistening, laughing it off]* "Okay, stop, you're gonna make me ruin my makeup."

**PLAYER B:** "You're not wearing makeup."

**PLAYER A:** "EXACTLY. I have nothing to protect me right now!"

*[They both laugh. It's the kind of laugh that only happens after real vulnerability.]*

---

## ACT III — THE CLOSE (6:15–6:50)

*[Wide two-shot again. The bottle is noticeably lower. The card stack is scattered.]*

**PLAYER A:** "That was… way more than I expected."

**PLAYER B:** "Yeah. I feel like I just went through a whole relationship in twenty minutes."

**PLAYER A:** "Speed-run emotional intimacy."

**PLAYER B:** *[raising his glass]* "To strangers who don't stay strangers."

*[They clink glasses. Drink. Set them down.]*

**PLAYER A:** "Also, I'm going to tell everyone you don't like jazz."

**PLAYER B:** "Please don't. I will find you."

*[They shake hands across the table. It turns into a genuine, slightly awkward hug over the table. The bottle wobbles. They catch it. Laugh.]*

---

## CLOSING CONFESSIONAL BEATS

*[Rapid intercut between individual talking heads.]*

**PLAYER B:** "I think Gen Z gets called 'too sensitive' a lot. But like — I just told a stranger about my grandma and I feel lighter? That's not sensitivity. That's just being human."

**PLAYER A:** "I'd do this again. Maybe with harder liquor though, because some of those questions needed tequila, not vodka."
