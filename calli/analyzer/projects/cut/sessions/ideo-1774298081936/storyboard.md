## "SIPS & SECRETS" — Truth or Drink: Gen Z Edition
### Duration: 5–7 min | Format: Single-camera studio shoot

## SCENE 1: "THE SETUP"

**Visual Description:**
Wide establishing shot on a 50mm lens, slowly dollying in to a medium two-shot. Two strangers (ages 20–24) sit across from each other at a small round table against a desaturated lavender backdrop — CUT's signature minimal aesthetic. Soft, diffused key light from camera-left with a subtle warm backlight creating a halo separation. The table holds a bottle of tequila, two shot glasses, and a branded card deck. The mood is bright, slightly nervous, anticipatory.

**On-Screen Action & Key Dialogue:**
Both participants introduce themselves with name/age/one fun fact (lower-third text pops in with a Gen Z-coded font and pastel color). Host voice (off-camera) explains the rules: *"You flip a card, read the question out loud. You either answer honestly… or you drink. No in-betweens."* Both laugh nervously; one says, *"I'm already scared."*

**Mood:** 😬 — Nervous excitement; the calm before the chaos.

---

## SCENE 2: "THE WARM-UP ROUND"

**Visual Description:**
Cut between tight singles (85mm, shallow depth of field) as each person reads a card. Lighting unchanged but we add a subtle [[H14:test whether adding a colored LED accent light shift between rounds improves viewer retention|pink-to-amber LED accent shift]] on the backdrop to visually signal escalation. Jump cuts are punchy — 1–2 second pauses trimmed to keep energy high. Table-level insert shots capture fingers flipping cards, liquid pouring into glasses.

**On-Screen Action & Key Dialogue:**
Questions start relatively safe: *"What's your most embarrassing dating app moment?"* Participant A answers with a cringe story about accidentally super-liking their professor. Participant B flips the next card — *"What's the pettiest reason you've ghosted someone?"* — pauses, winces, and reaches for the tequila instead. The drink is the punchline. On-screen text animates: **"SHE CHOSE VIOLENCE 🍹"**

**Mood:** 😂 — Playful, icebreaker energy; laughs are easy and genuine.

---

## SCENE 3: "THE DEEP CUT" ⭐ *(THE CLIP — see Director's Note)*

**Visual Description:**
Camera pushes to an extreme close-up (100mm macro) on eyes and micro-expressions — this is the emotional pivot. Lighting subtly dims; the backlight intensifies to create a more intimate, almost confessional feel. [[H07:test if a 2-second silence hold before the answer outperforms immediate responses for shareability|A deliberate 2-second silence hold]] stretches the tension. Background music (lo-fi, low-mix) drops out entirely when the question is read.

**On-Screen Action & Key Dialogue:**
The card reads: *"Have you ever pretended to be okay when you were falling apart — and who didn't notice?"* Long beat. Participant B's expression shifts — they set the shot glass down, untouched. *"Yeah… my best friend. I was having the worst semester of my life and I just kept sending memes like everything was fine. She still doesn't know."* Participant A reaches across the table and quietly says, *"She'd want to know."* No text overlay — the moment speaks for itself.

**Mood:** 🥺 — Raw vulnerability; the scroll-stopping emotional gut-punch.

---

## SCENE 4: "THE CLOSER"

**Visual Description:**
Pull back to the original wide two-shot, mirroring Scene 1's framing to create visual bookending. Lighting returns to its warm, bright baseline. [[H22:test whether a post-game debrief segment where participants react to each other's answers increases average watch-through rate|A brief "debrief" segment]] plays where both participants share one takeaway. Confetti or a subtle party popper gag breaks any residual heaviness.

**On-Screen Action & Key Dialogue:**
Host asks: *"Last question — no card. Would you do this again with someone you actually know?"* Both pause, then laugh. Participant A: *"Absolutely not. They'd learn too much."* End card fades in with CTA: **"Who would YOU play with? Tag them."** Final shot: both clinking glasses and drinking together — not because they dodged a question, but because they earned it.

**Mood:** 🤝 — Warm resolution; friendship-forged-in-fire energy.

## 🎬 DIRECTOR'S NOTE

**Pacing:** The video follows a classic **tension curve** — light/light/heavy/release. Scenes 1–2 should move *fast* (jump cuts, 2–3 sec per beat, total ~90 sec). Scene 3 is where we **breathe** — let silences live, hold on faces, stretch to ~90–120 sec. Scene 4 is a quick 30–45 sec exhale. Total runtime target: **5:30**.

**The Clip:** Scene 3 is the hero clip for social. The 45-second segment from card-read through *"She'd want to know"* is engineered for TikTok/Reels. The emotional pivot from comedy to vulnerability is the mechanic that drives shares — it subverts the "drinking game" expectation. Trim it as a standalone with a cold open on the question card.

**Hypothesis Testing Priority:** H07 (silence hold) is the highest-signal test — it's a zero-cost editorial choice that directly impacts the clip's virality. Run an A/B on the cut with and without the hold in the first 48 hours of posting.
