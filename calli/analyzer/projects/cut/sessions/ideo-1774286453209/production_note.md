Concept
Two exes who haven't spoken since their breakup sit across from each other with a bottle of tequila and a stack of questions they've never had the courage to ask — but the questions escalate from "why did you unfollow me?" to "did you ever actually love me?" and they only get one skip, which they've already used by minute four. The format weaponizes unfinished emotional business: every question is a grenade with the pin half-pulled, and drinking is a public confession of cowardice.
The specific wound: Jalen was going to propose. He had the ring. Mira found it in his jacket pocket three days before she ended things — and she never told him she knew. He still doesn't know she saw it. That detail is the bomb under the table for the entire video.
The Hook: Two people who used to share a bed now can't make eye contact. The cold open drops one line that changes everything — Mira, direct to camera, pre-game confessional: "He doesn't know I found the ring." Then we cut to the table. The first card reads: "What's the real reason you ended it?" — and neither one drinks. That's when you know this video is going to hurt.
The WTF Moment: At 5:42, Jalen reads the card aloud — "Did you cheat?" — and Mira doesn't drink, doesn't answer, just looks at him for six full seconds. Then she says: "Not physically." Jalen's hand shakes putting the card down. This moment lands at the 60% mark, right where algorithmic retention needs a spike.

Cast
Jalen — The Wounded Stoic. 26, graphic designer, broke up with Mira eight months ago. Presents as calm and over it, but his jaw clenches every time she laughs. He came to prove he's moved on; he'll leave realizing he hasn't. He was three days from proposing when she ended it — returned the ring the following week, told no one. Reveal: "I still have your playlist on my phone. I listen to it when I can't sleep."
Mira — The Deflector. 25, grad student, uses humor as armor. She's the one who ended things but carries guilt she's never named — specifically, she found an engagement ring in his jacket, panicked about losing herself inside someone else's life plan, and broke up with him before he could ask. She has never told him she saw it. Quick-witted, occasionally cruel, then suddenly devastated. Reveal: "I left because I was scared I'd stay forever and lose myself." Secondary reveal (the detonation): "I found the ring, Jalen. I found it three days before."
Deshawn — The Honest Mess. 28, bartender, got dumped two weeks ago. Still has his ex's toothbrush at his apartment. Zero filter, will say the thing everyone's thinking. Reveal: "I texted her 'I miss you' at 2am last night. She hearted it. What does that mean?"
Priya — The Over-It Queen. 24, content creator, dumped her ex a year ago and genuinely doesn't care — or so she performs. Her composure cracks exactly once, and it's devastating. Reveal: "I don't miss him. I miss who I was when I believed him."
Chemistry & Group Dynamic: These aren't strangers — they're people haunted by the same ghosts. The paired-exes format creates immediate dramatic irony: the audience knows about the ring before Jalen does, which transforms every exchange between them into a countdown. When Mira deflects with humor, the audience isn't just watching avoidance — they're watching someone sit on a secret that could break the person across from her. Jalen and Mira's tension is the engine; Deshawn's rawness gives permission for vulnerability; Priya's composure gives the audience someone to root against cracking. The energy is a dinner party where everyone's pretending the earthquake isn't happening — except the audience can already see the cracks in the foundation.
Archetypes: Jalen = Hero/Underdog. Mira = Chaotic Wildcard. Deshawn = Super Fun / Heartwarming. Priya = Overconfident Player (whose armor breaks).

The Loop
Core Loop: Question read aloud → decision (truth or drink) → answer lands (or dodge reveals) → group/partner reacts → emotional consequence shifts the room
Loop Family: Truth/Reveal + Pressure
Why This Loop Fits: Truth or Drink is the purest reveal loop in CUT's arsenal — every round is a binary choice with social cost either way. Drinking IS an answer; it tells the audience "this truth is too dangerous to say out loud," which is more compelling than most truths. The ring secret adds a second layer to the loop: every answer Mira gives, the audience is also asking "is this the moment she tells him?"
Secondary Loops: Eye-contact micro-loop (read card → look at ex → decide whether to hold gaze → audience reads the silence). Drink-count loop (each drink raises the stakes: "she's had four, she's hiding something big"). Ring-reveal countdown (audience knows the secret → every question about the relationship's end brings it closer to the surface → when will it detonate?).
Currency of tension: Emotional exposure. Every truth costs dignity; every drink costs credibility.
Ramp: Staircase — questions escalate in three tiers (surface → wound → core), with each tier clearly signaled by card color. The ring revelation is designed to land in the red-card tier, but the dramatic irony runs from second one.
Payoff: Did they actually love each other? And does saying it out loud change anything? — amplified by the specific: he was going to ask her to marry him, and she ran.

Psychological Drivers
Primary Drivers:

Vulnerability — Every question forces someone to choose between emotional exposure and visible avoidance; viewers feel both the terror and the relief.
Prediction tension — Will they answer or drink? Audiences lock in because they think they know, and they need to find out. The ring secret adds a second prediction layer: *when* will Mira tell him?
Rejection anxiety — Watching someone ask "did you love me?" and waiting for the answer activates every viewer's own fear of the same question. The ring raises this to existential stakes — he was ready for forever, and she chose to leave.
Social risk — These answers exist forever on YouTube; the stakes are real and irreversible.

Secondary Drivers:

Dramatic irony — The audience knows about the ring before Jalen does. This is the most powerful retention tool in storytelling: superior knowledge creates unbearable anticipation. Every time Jalen says something about the breakup, viewers are screaming at the screen.
Second-hand embarrassment — When Deshawn admits he texted his ex at 2am, viewers cringe-laugh because they've done it too.
Self-projection — Every viewer mentally answers the questions themselves; the format is a mirror.
Authenticity hunger — Gen Z detects performance instantly; the moments where composure breaks feel like truth in a feed full of curation.
Closure fantasy — Most people never get to ask their ex these questions; this is vicarious emotional resolution.
Attraction uncertainty — The lingering chemistry between Jalen and Mira makes the audience wonder: will they get back together? The ring makes this question unbearable — they were *that close*.


Story Structure
Act 1 — The Pretense (0:00–2:30)
Story intent: Establish characters as "over it," plant the lie the video will dismantle. Seed the ring for the audience only.

Cold open: Mira, solo confessional, black background: "He doesn't know I found the ring." Hard cut to: close-up of tequila being poured. Mira's voice at the table: "I'm totally fine with this."
Introductions — each person states who their ex is and how long ago it ended
First two questions: light ("What's the first thing you noticed about me?" / "Rate our relationship 1–10")
Split-screen reactions on the rating question — Jalen says 7, Mira flinches. The audience, armed with the ring knowledge, reads her flinch differently than he does.

Act 2 — The Cracks (2:30–5:30)
Story intent: Escalate from surface to wound. First drinks happen. First silence. The ring secret starts to press against every answer Mira gives.

Questions hit the breakup directly: "What did I do that hurt you most?"
Deshawn's unfiltered honesty gives the room permission to go deeper
Priya drinks for the first time — the audience gasps
Jalen and Mira's exchange about the real reason gets heated — Jalen says "I thought we were solid. I thought we were going somewhere." Mira's face tells the audience everything. She almost says it. She doesn't.

Act 3 — The Core (5:30–8:00)
Story intent: The questions no one wants to answer. Maximum vulnerability. The ring detonates.

"Did you cheat?" / "Do you still love me?" / "What would you say if I asked for another chance?"
The WTF moment lands here — Mira's "not physically" answer
Deshawn cries. It's real. This is where retention either holds or collapses.
The ring reveal: On the card "What's something you never told me while we were together?" — Mira doesn't drink. She looks at him for a long time. "I found the ring, Jalen. I found it three days before." His face. The room goes silent. Deshawn puts his hand over his mouth. This is the moment the video becomes unshareable — meaning everyone will share it.

Act 4 — The Landing (8:00–9:30)
Story intent: Emotional resolution without false closure. The ring revelation has changed the air in the room.

Final question: "What do you wish you'd said before it ended?"
Jalen's playlist reveal — now recontextualized by the ring. This wasn't casual attachment. This was a man who was ready.
Mira's "lose myself" line — now the audience understands the full weight: she didn't leave because she didn't love him. She left because she did, and it terrified her.
No hug unless it's real. End on faces, not words.

Cold Open (25 seconds, shot-by-shot):
0–3s: Black screen. Mira's voice, solo confessional: "He doesn't know I found the ring." 3–6s: Extreme close-up, tequila poured into shot glass, sound of liquid. 6–10s: Wide shot, two people sitting across from each other, tension visible. 10–15s: Mira at the table: "I haven't talked to him in eight months." Cut to Jalen: "I'm fine." 15–20s: Card flipped: "What's the real reason you broke up?" 20–23s: Both faces, neither speaks. 23–25s: Title card slam: TRUTH OR DRINK: EX FILES.
First Three Minutes: The format is established in 45 seconds — you see the table, the bottle, the cards, the exes. But the audience already has the ring secret from the cold open, so every second is charged with dramatic irony. Stakes crystallize when Jalen says "I didn't want to do this" and Mira says "Then why did you say yes?" The first conflict is the rating question — Jalen's 7 vs Mira's 5. The disagreement hits before 90 seconds. The 1-minute escalation comes from Deshawn's zero-filter answer about his ex's toothbrush, which makes the audience laugh and lean in simultaneously.

Emotional Design
Tone: Warm but unflinching. This isn't therapy and it isn't a roast — it's the conversation you have at 1am when the defenses come down.
Dominant Emotions: Tension, tenderness, cringe, catharsis.
Emotional Arc: Bravado → discomfort → crack → vulnerability → detonation → quiet devastation → unexpected warmth.
Tension Source: The gap between what people perform and what they feel, made visible by a binary choice — and the specific secret the audience holds that one participant doesn't.
Humor: Deshawn is the relief valve. Priya's dry commentary. The absurdity of pouring another shot instead of saying "yes, I loved you." Levity-to-intensity ratio targets 0.35.
Peak Moments:

0:02 — Gut Punch / Dramatic Irony — "He doesn't know I found the ring." The audience is locked in before the title card.
1:20 — Surprise/Delight — Jalen rates the relationship 7, Mira says 5, his face falls
3:45 — Cringe/Empathy — Deshawn admits the 2am text, buries his face in his hands
5:42 — Shock/Devastation — Mira's "not physically" answer to the cheating question
6:30 — Detonation — "I found the ring, Jalen. I found it three days before." The silence that follows is the most powerful moment in the video.
7:15 — Catharsis — Deshawn cries and says "I just wanted to be enough"
8:50 — Bittersweet/Warm — Jalen's playlist reveal, recontextualized by the ring; Mira's eyes fill but she smiles


Production Design
Set: Minimal — black background, small wooden table, two chairs, one bottle, shot glasses, and a stack of colored cards (white → yellow → red for escalation tiers); low-key lighting with a single warm overhead.
Camera: Three cameras — two tight on faces (85mm, shallow DOF), one wide for reactions and the pour; handheld micro-adjustments for intimacy. NOTE: During the ring reveal, hold on Jalen's face for the full reaction — do not cut away. Let the audience sit in it.
Graphics: Card text appears on screen as lower-third when read aloud; drink counter in corner; minimal — let faces do the work.
Music & Sound: No music during answers — only room tone and the clink of glasses; subtle piano underscore during transitions between question tiers; silence is the most powerful production choice here. During the ring reveal: absolute silence. No score. No sound design. Just two people and the truth.
Testimonials: 15-second solo confessionals between acts — direct to camera, black background, one sentence each ("I didn't think this would be hard"). Mira's pre-game confessional about the ring plays ONLY in the cold open — it is not repeated. The audience carries it.

Performance Notes
What Works: The binary choice (truth or drink) is the simplest, most legible loop on YouTube — zero explanation needed. Exes guarantee pre-loaded emotional stakes. The ring secret gives the audience a reason to stay for the entire video — they're not just watching questions, they're waiting for a detonation. The escalation tier system (white → yellow → red) gives the editor clean act breaks and the algorithm clear retention architecture. High segment_count from rapid question cycling. Traffic_algorithmic_pct should be strong given the clear loop visibility, emotional peaks, and the dramatic irony hook in the cold open.
What Doesn't: If the exes are genuinely over each other, there's no tension — casting is everything. If questions don't escalate fast enough, the middle sags. Too many drinks without answers becomes repetitive (the drink IS an answer, but only if the edit treats it that way). The ring reveal must feel earned, not manufactured — if it lands too early, the back half deflates; if it's saved for the last minute, it feels withheld.
