## 1. SHOOTING SETUP & LOCATION

### Set Design
**Core Set Piece:** One small round table (30" diameter, matte black or raw birch plywood — nothing "produced-looking"). Two simple chairs — mismatched on purpose (one mid-century modern stool, one vintage diner chair). This signals *casual authenticity*, not a talk show.

**Background:** Seamless black or deep charcoal backdrop (CUT's signature). We deviate slightly for Gen Z by adding one subtle textural element — a single practical neon accent light (warm amber or soft pink, NOT the oversaturated RGB "gaming" look) placed low and behind participants to create a gentle rim/halo separation. [[H14: warm neon accent vs. pure black void on Gen Z watch-time|This ambient accent light is a direct test of whether environmental warmth increases retention for younger audiences]].

**Table Dressing:** The bottle(s) of alcohol centered on the table. A small stack of question cards face-down. Two shot glasses. Nothing else. Negative space is the aesthetic.

### Lighting Mood & Feel
- **Key Light:** Soft, large-source overhead — a 4x4 or Chimera butterfly with grid, angled slightly forward. Goal: flattering, even skin tones with gentle shadows under cheekbones. Not flat, not dramatic. Think "well-lit FaceTime call at golden hour."
- **Fill:** Minimal. Negative fill on the outside edges of each participant to preserve some shape. Ratio roughly 2:1 key-to-fill.
- **Rim/Separation:** The practical neon element plus a dedicated hair/rim light per participant (small LED panels with CTS gel, dialed low).
- **Overall Mood:** Intimate, slightly warm (3800K–4200K range), inviting. Should feel like 1AM at a good bar — not a studio.

### Location
Shoot in-studio (CUT's standing studio or rented black-box stage). Controlled sound is non-negotiable for a dialogue-heavy format. Minimum 20x20 ft shooting area; 12 ft ceiling clearance preferred.

## 2. PROPS & WARDROBE

### Props
| Item | Spec / Notes |
|---|---|
| **Alcohol** | One bottle of a recognizable-but-cool spirit. Tequila (Espolòn or Casamigos) reads Gen Z. Backup: vodka (Tito's). NO beer — shots are funnier and higher stakes. Must verify participants are 21+. |
| **Shot Glasses** | 2 clear, simple, slightly oversized (2 oz) — reads well on camera. |
| **Question Cards** | Custom-printed on thick matte black card stock, white sans-serif type (CUT branding). Rounded corners. They should look tactile and "unboxing-worthy." |
| **Water Glasses** | Off-camera but within reach. Hydration = duty of care. |
| **Backup Bottle** | Second full bottle standing by. If energy is high and participants are willing, we want to keep rolling. |
| **Napkins/Tissues** | Hidden below table line. Emotional moments happen. |

### Wardrobe
**Direction to Participants (sent 48 hrs pre-shoot):**
- "Wear what you'd wear to a friend's house party — your *real* fit, not a costume."
- **Avoid:** Logos, all-white, all-black, busy patterns, thin stripes (moiré on camera).
- **Encourage:** Color. Texture. Jewelry. Accessories that express personality.
- **Art Dept Check:** If both participants arrive in similar palettes, have a small rack of 4–5 neutral swap-out tops (oversized tees, flannels, simple knits) in varied tones.

**Why this matters:** Gen Z viewers read wardrobe as identity signal in the first 2 seconds. Authenticity here drives [[H09: "real clothes" vs. styled wardrobe on perceived authenticity score|our hypothesis that self-selected outfits outperform styled ones on comment sentiment]].

## 4. CAMERA STRATEGY

### Three-Camera Setup

```
        [CAM B - CU]          [CAM C - CU]
             \                    /
              \                  /
         [Participant A] ——TABLE—— [Participant B]
                       |
                    [CAM A]
                  (Hero Wide/Mid)
```

| Camera | Coverage | Lens | Mount | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **A — Hero** | Wide two-shot, slow push-in available | 35mm or 50mm (full frame) | Dolly or slider on track (short 4ft run) | The "establishing" camera. Holds wide during questions, pushes in slightly during escalation moments. Also captures table action (pouring, drinking, card flips). |
| **B — Participant A CU** | Tight close-up, face only | 85mm (full frame) | Fluid head tripod, operator standing by for micro-adjustments | This is the *reaction* camera. Stays tight. Catches micro-expressions: the lip bite, the eye dart, the suppressed laugh. Operator has freedom to slightly loosen to a mid-CU if participant gestures big. |
| **C — Participant B CU** | Tight close-up, face only | 85mm (full frame) | Same as B | Mirror of B. Symmetry in coverage means the editor has equal material from both participants and can play "tennis match" cuts. |

### Technical Specs
- **Resolution:** 4K minimum (allows punch-in during edit for "fake" extreme close-ups without quality loss — critical for reaction shots).
- **Frame Rate:** 24fps base. Cameras B & C also roll select moments at 60fps for slow-motion reaction shots (the "spit-take," the "jaw drop"). Director calls "speed" to ops when they sense a big moment coming.
- **Color:** Shoot flat/log profile (S-Log3 or C-Log3). Grade in post for the warm, slightly desaturated-but-rich look that CUT is known for.
- **Audio Sync:** Timecode-synced across all three cameras + audio recorder via Tentacle Sync or similar.

## 5. PACING & EDITING NOTES

### Structure & Timing

| Segment | Duration | Pacing Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Cold Open** (Scene 1) | 0:00–0:15 | NO intro. NO title card yet. Drop the viewer into a Round 4+ moment — a gasp, a "WAIT WHAT?!", a pour. This is the **hook**. Audio hits before video (black screen → voice → cut to face). Mimics the "overheard conversation" scroll-stop on TikTok/Reels. |
| **Title Card + Reset** | 0:15–0:30 | Quick CUT-branded title slate. Then a SMASH CUT back to Round 1 — the "beginning." Participants introduce themselves in 1–2 sentences MAX. No life stories. Names, ages, one surprising fact. |
| **Rounds 1–3 (Warm-Up)** | 0:30–2:00 | Lighter questions. Faster cuts. Establish rhythm: question → reaction → answer OR drink. Average shot length: 2–3 sec. Music bed is present but low — lo-fi or light electronic. |
| **Rounds 4–6 (Escalation)** | 2:00–4:00 | Questions get personal. Cuts slow down — we *hold* on faces longer (4–6 sec shots). Music dips out entirely during vulnerable answers. Let silence breathe. This is where the video earns its emotional core. |
| **Rounds 7–8 (Peak)** | 4:00–5:30 | Hardest questions. This is the "viral clip" zone. Use slow-motion selectively (ONE moment max — overuse kills impact). If a participant chooses to drink instead of answer, hold on the other person's face — their reaction IS the content. |
| **Closing / Debrief** | 5:30–6:30 | Director asks a soft closing question: "What surprised you about this person?" or "Would you hang out again?" Let the answer breathe. End on a genuine human moment, not a punchline. |
| **End Card** | 6:30–6:45 | CUT branding, subscribe CTA, link to next video. |

### Editing Philosophy
- **Cut on reaction, not action.** The moment someone finishes a sentence, CUT TO the other person's face. The reaction is always more interesting than the statement.
- **Jump cuts are fine** — this is not cinema. Gen Z expects and enjoys the energy of jump cuts. Use them to compress dead air, not to manufacture false energy.
- **Text overlays:** Minimal. Use ONLY for: participant names (first appearance), a question that's hard to hear, or a single "😭" / "💀" style emoji overlay at the ONE most unhinged moment (test sparingly — [[H22: emoji text overlays on Gen Z engagement rate|we're hypothesizing that one well-placed overlay boosts shares but more than two feels try-hard and tanks authenticity perception]]).
- **Sound design:** Subtle. A bass "thud" on hard cuts. A soft "whoosh" on card reveals. The clinking of glass should be ASMR-crisp — mix it forward. No laugh tracks. No sound effects on reactions. Trust the content.
- **Thumbnail & Hook Variants:** Editor produces 3 alternate cold opens and 3 thumbnail frames for A/B testing across platforms.
