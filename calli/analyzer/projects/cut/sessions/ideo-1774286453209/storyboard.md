**Format:** Truth or Drink · **Target:** Gen Z (18–25) · **Duration:** 5–7 min final cut · **Participants:** 2 exes who haven't spoken since their breakup, both Gen Z

## Scene 1: "The Setup"

**Visual Description:**
~~Camera opens with a slow dolly-in on two strangers seated across a small round table~~ **Cold open drops us mid-conversation — we catch a half-sentence, something raw and unfinished ("—and you never even told me that") before SMASH CUT TO BLACK, then the title card: TRUTH OR DRINK: EX FILES. We rewind. Now:** camera opens with a slow dolly-in on two exes seated across a small round table against CUT's signature stark white cyclorama. Lighting is flat and bright — classic CUT key light with minimal shadows — establishing the clean, almost clinical aesthetic the channel is known for. Two shot glasses and a bottle of tequila sit between them. A subtle rack focus shifts from the bottle to the participants' faces as they **clock each other for the first time in months — the recognition is immediate and loaded, a flicker of something neither can mask.**

**On-Screen Action & Key Dialogue:**
Both participants introduce themselves with name and a one-liner ("I'm Mika, 22, and I have zero filter"). **A beat. Then Mika glances at the person across from them: "...We dated for eight months." The other: "Nine." The correction lands like a grenade. Silence.** The off-screen producer voice explains the rules: "You'll take turns asking each other questions. If you don't want to answer — you drink. **You each get one skip. Use it wisely.**" One participant nervously laughs and says, "I'm already scared," while the other cracks their knuckles and grins — **but the grin doesn't quite reach their eyes.**

**Mood:** 😬 — Nervous anticipation; **not the awkward-electric energy of strangers, but something heavier — the tension of two people with actual unfinished business who are about to lose the luxury of avoidance.** The audience feels the asymmetry immediately: one of them clearly isn't over it.

## Scene 2: "The Warm-Up Round"

**Visual Description:**
Standard two-shot with occasional cut to tight singles (85mm lens, shallow depth of field) capturing micro-expressions. Lighting remains bright and even. The color temperature stays neutral-cool at 5600K to keep the sterile, nowhere-to-hide feeling. A small card deck with questions is visible on the table — participants physically draw cards, adding tactile texture to the frame.

**On-Screen Action & Key Dialogue:**
Questions escalate from **deceptively casual to pointed**: **"When did you unfollow me?"** (one claims they don't remember; the other's face says they *absolutely* know the exact date). Then: **"What's the most unhinged thing you still have in your phone from our relationship?"** One participant reads an embarrassing unsent draft text aloud — **it's clearly written the night of the breakup, and the room shifts**; the other chooses to drink instead, prompting a ~~howl of~~ quiet, loaded: "It's THAT bad?!" from their ex. **The producer's voice, off-screen, gently reminds them: "Remember — you only get one skip each." One of them mutters, "Yeah, I'm saving that." They have no idea how soon they'll burn it.** The unanswered question hangs in the air — **and the audience already knows: by minute four, the skips will be gone, and there will be nowhere left to hide.**

**Mood:** The warmth of recognition keeps flickering against the chill of everything unsaid. **This isn't banter between strangers finding chemistry — it's two people who already know exactly where each other's soft spots are, circling them.**
