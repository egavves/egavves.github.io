## 1. VULNERABILITY ✅ KEPT
**"Gen Z Strangers Get Way Too Honest Over Drinks"**

**Why it works:** This title leans into the vulnerability frame by promising raw, unfiltered emotional disclosure — a key driver of comment_sentiment_mean and ending_vs_mean_sentiment, both LASSO-stable for engagement_like_ratio and engagement_overall_ratio. The word "honest" signals authentic emotional escalation, which maps to escalation_intensity (LASSO-stable for views_public), priming viewers to expect rising emotional stakes. "Way Too Honest" also functions as a soft intensity marker that boosts share_intent_score, a LASSO-stable predictor of both views_public and engagement_comment_ratio, because viewers anticipate shareable confessional moments.

## 2. CURIOSITY 🔄 REPLACED
**"Exes Ask Each Other Questions They Never Could During the Relationship"**

**Why it works:** This title operationalizes the core premise — asymmetric history, unresolved tension, and the specific structural constraint (questions they've been holding back). "Never Could" is the curiosity engine: it implies suppressed emotional content now being released, which directly maps to escalation_intensity and emotional_arc_complexity, both LASSO-stable for views_public. The word "Exes" front-loads relationship stakes, a proven click-driver in CUT's catalog that elevates ctr_predicted by signaling interpersonal conflict. "During the Relationship" adds temporal specificity that distinguishes this from generic Q&A formats — viewers understand these questions are *old wounds*, not party games, which boosts share_intent_score because the implied emotional payoff feels earned and particular.

## 3. CONFLICT / TENSION 🔄 REPLACED
**"Exes Play Truth or Drink But One Already Skipped by Question Four"**

**Why it works:** This title weaponizes the "one skip already used by minute four" structural detail from the concept brief, converting a production beat into a packaging hook. The specificity of "Question Four" triggers curiosity gap — viewers need to know *what question* was so threatening it burned the skip that early. This maps directly to information_gap_count, a LASSO-stable predictor of engagement_comment_ratio, because commenters will speculate and debate. "Already Skipped" implies emotional avoidance under pressure, which signals escalation_intensity — if they're dodging *that* early, what happens when they can't dodge anymore? The title also subtly promises asymmetry (one person is more guarded), which drives comment_sentiment_mean because audiences take sides.

## 4. IDENTITY / RELATABILITY 🔄 REPLACED
**"We Broke Up 2 Years Ago. We Haven't Spoken Since."**

**Why it works:** First-person framing plus temporal specificity ("2 Years Ago") creates an immediate parasocial entry point — viewers project their own ex onto the scenario. The period-separated sentences mimic the cadence of someone steeling themselves before a difficult conversation, which functions as tonal packaging: viewers feel the emotional weight before clicking. "Haven't Spoken Since" is the tension payload — it implies accumulated silence now being broken, mapping to emotional_arc_complexity (LASSO-stable for views_public) because the entire video becomes a rupture event. This title indexes highest for share_intent_score among the set because it reads like a caption someone would post about their own life, making it natively shareable as an emotional mirror.

## 5. SPECTACLE / STAKES 🔄 REPLACED
**"Exes Drink Every Time They Lie to Each Other's Face"**

**Why it works:** This title reframes the Truth or Drink mechanic as a lie-detection system, adding a layer of behavioral spectacle. "To Each Other's Face" raises interpersonal stakes — it's not anonymous, it's confrontational, and the proximity is the point. This maps to conflict_resolution_type and escalation_intensity, both LASSO-stable for engagement_overall_ratio, because viewers anticipate visible discomfort and micro-expressions. "Every Time They Lie" promises a repeating pattern, which signals high moment_density — viewers expect multiple drink-worthy deceptions, boosting watch-through. The implicit accusation structure (someone *will* lie, and the drink proves it) drives engagement_comment_ratio because audiences will timestamp and adjudicate each perceived lie.
