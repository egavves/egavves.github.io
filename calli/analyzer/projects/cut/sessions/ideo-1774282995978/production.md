## 1. SHOOTING SETUP & LOCATION

### Location Recommendation: **Controlled Studio Dressed as a Real Living Room**
> *Not a soundstage. Not a living room. The uncanny valley between both — familiar enough to feel real, clean enough to shoot fast.*

**Why not an actual apartment?**
The casting brief explicitly flags that Gen Z trusts *unpolished authenticity over performance* — but authenticity in production terms means **consistent audio, reliable lighting, and no neighbor noise at hour 6 of a shoot.** A dressed studio gives you the vibe without the chaos tax.

---

### Set Design Specs

| Element | Spec | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **Furniture** | Low-to-ground sectional or floor cushions arranged in a loose circle/diamond | Removes hierarchy, encourages physical lean-ins and reactions |
| **Table** | Small coffee table, center — holds drinks, cards, phone props | Functional AND creates a natural focal point for wide shot |
| **Background** | Warm-toned bookshelf OR tapestry/LED light panel — NOT a blank wall | Depth for cameras 2/3; avoids "deposition" energy |
| **Color palette** | Terracotta, sage, warm cream, muted mustard | Reads warm on camera; avoids the cold "corporate YouTube" look |
| **Practical lights** | 2–3 visible lamps (string lights optional) | These are *on camera* — they sell "real space" to the viewer |
| **Floor texture** | Layered rugs | Softens acoustics slightly; looks lived-in |

---

### Lighting Mood

| Zone | Approach |
|---|---|
| **Key light** | Soft, warm LED panels (5600K dialed to ~4200K) slightly off-axis per seat position |
| **Fill** | Bounce cards or low-power fill panels — NO harsh shadows |
| **Practical integration** | Lamps on set are real and on — they contribute warm fill naturally |
| **Overall mood** | **"Late afternoon at a friend's place"** — golden, slightly intimate, never clinical |
| **What to avoid** | Overhead hard light (ages faces, kills vibe), flat TV-news lighting, anything that reads "studio" |

> **[[H01:test whether warm practical-integrated lighting increases comment sentiment scores vs. neutral studio lighting|Lighting mood is a hypothesis worth testing]]** — warmer sets may directly correlate with higher audience retention in the first 90 seconds.

## 2. PROPS & WARDROBE

### Props Master List

| Prop | Quantity | Specific Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Shot glasses** | 8 (2 per player) | Clear glass preferred — liquid visible on camera; have backups for breakage |
| **Drink options** | 3 types minimum | Water (for non-drinkers/safety), a light mixer (seltzer/juice), and one "real" option — label all clearly for consent documentation |
| **Question cards** | 60–80 cards pre-written | Color-coded by escalation tier (Tier 1 = warm, Tier 2 = personal, Tier 3 = spicy) — matches storyboard escalation spine |
| **Card holder/box** | 1 | Aesthetically neutral — wooden box or plain matte black; keeps cards organized on camera |
| **Phone props** | 4 (cast's own preferred) | Used for "show us your screen" questions — confirm consent to film screens in pre-shoot briefing |
| **Throw blanket** | 1–2 | On sectional/couch — adds texture, cast may naturally grab it (authentic comfort behavior) |
| **Snack bowl** | 1 (chips/pretzels) | Idle-hands prop; people eat when nervous — great reaction texture |
| **Water pitcher** | 1 | Practical + visual reset prop between rounds |

### Wardrobe Direction

**Philosophy:** *Styled to look unstyled.* Cast should look like they dressed themselves — because they did, within guardrails.

| Cast Member | Wardrobe Notes |
|---|---|
| **Maya** (chaotic energy, oversharer) | Layered — oversized graphic tee under open button-down, or a fun pattern. Something she could have grabbed off her floor. Avoid anything too "put together." |
| **Dev** (dry humor, tries to seem unbothered) | Neutral-cool: plain crewneck, dark jeans, maybe a cap he takes off in scene 2. The "I didn't try but I did" look. |
| **Priya** (therapist-brained) | Elevated casual — linen or soft knit, earth tones. She looks like she shops at a farmers market. Intentional but not flashy. |
| **4th Player** (TBD per casting) | Coordinate with casting brief energy — contrast against the other three for visual variety |
| **Color rule** | No two players in the same color family. No all-black. No white (blows out under lights). |
| **Wardrobe fitting** | 30-min virtual fitting call + day-of check on arrival — no surprises on set |
| **Jewelry/accessories** | Cast's own, pre-approved — small earrings, rings fine. Nothing that catches light and flickers on camera. |
