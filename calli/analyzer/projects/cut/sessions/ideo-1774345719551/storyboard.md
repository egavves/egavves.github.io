# My Ex Rated My New Partner — Then I Walked In: 4-Scene Storyboard

---

## Scene 1: The Setup — "So… This Is Gonna Be Messy"

**Visual Description:**
Two exes (ages 20–25) sit across from each other at a minimalist round table against CUT's signature plain white backdrop. Camera opens on a medium two-shot, slightly wider than typical to emphasize the empty space and tension between them. Lighting is flat and bright — classic CUT house style — with a subtle warm grade to feel inviting rather than clinical. A row of shot glasses and a single bottle of tequila sit between them like a dare. A small placard between them reads "RATE YOUR EX'S NEW PARTNER" in bold sans-serif — barely visible but enough to make the audience lean in.

**On-Screen Action & Key Dialogue:**
Each participant introduces themselves with their name, age, and their relationship to each other ("I'm Maya, 22, and this is my ex Jaylen — we dated for two years and I still have his hoodie"). The host (off-camera) explains the rules: "You're going to answer brutally honest questions about each other's new partners. Looks. Personality. Compatibility. The things you'd never say out loud. If you don't want to answer — you drink. But just know… there's a twist coming." Both participants nervously laugh, size each other up, and clink empty glasses. We open with a 3-second pre-roll montage of the spiciest reaction from the final twist to hook viewers before the intro plays.

**Mood:** 😬 Nervous anticipation — electric, awkward, giddy energy. The vibe is running-into-your-ex-at-a-party butterflies meets "what did I sign up for." There's an undercurrent of unresolved history crackling beneath every glance.

---

## Scene 2: The Warm-Up Round — "Okay, That's Actually Diabolical"

**Visual Description:**
Camera shifts between tight close-ups on each participant's face (capturing micro-expressions) and a locked-off wide two-shot. The lighting remains unchanged, but the energy in the room noticeably loosens. A lower-third graphic pops on screen showing each question in a bold, Gen Z–friendly sans-serif font (think: Instagram Story text). When ratings are given, a large animated number (1–10) slams onto screen with a satisfying thud sound effect. The set stays minimal — the focus is entirely on faces and reactions.

**On-Screen Action & Key Dialogue:**
Questions escalate from playful to personal: "On a scale of 1–10, how attractive is your ex's new partner?" transitions into "What's one thing you genuinely think you did better for them than their new partner does?" One participant answers honestly — rating their ex's new partner a 6 with a guilty wince — and the other, caught off guard by the specificity, reaches for a shot instead of answering. The host pushes: "That's a drink, which means the truth is worse than tequila." Nervous laughter fills the silence. A question like "Do you think their new partner knows about the thing you two used to do?" sends one participant into a full hands-over-face meltdown. The energy is loose but every answer carries weight — these people have real history and it shows.

**Mood:** 🫣 Chaotic vulnerability — the sweet spot where honesty becomes unhinged. Laughter is a defense mechanism and everyone in the room knows it.

---

## Scene 3: The Deep Round — "You Were NOT Supposed to Say That"

**Visual Description:**
The camera work gets more intimate — slow push-ins during long pauses, a handheld close-up that catches a participant mid-swallow before answering. The shot glasses are noticeably emptier. One participant's body language has shifted — leaning back, arms crossed or leaning forward conspiratorially. The lower-third questions now appear in a slightly larger, bolder font — visually signaling the escalation. Quick-cut reaction shots are edited tighter, punchier.

**On-Screen Action & Key Dialogue:**
The questions turn diabolical: "Rate your ex's new partner's personality honestly — and explain the number." "What's one thing about their new partner you'd never say to their face?" "Do you think your ex downgraded, sidegraded, or upgraded?" One participant gives a rating and then can't stop themselves from elaborating — "She's pretty, she's like a 7, but I just know she doesn't get his humor the way I did, and that was like… our whole thing." The other fires back with something equally devastating delivered with a smile. Someone says "I'm going to hell for this" and takes a shot AFTER answering, not instead of. The host teases: "Remember — there's still a twist." Both participants freeze. The unhinged energy now has a ticking-clock tension underneath it.

**Mood:** 🔥💀 Peak chaos — the air is thick with things-you-can't-unsay. It's messy, it's honest, it's the content people will screen-record and send to group chats. Every answer feels like pulling a pin.

---

## Scene 4: The Twist — "Wait… They Were HERE?!"

**Visual Description:**
The camera pulls to its widest shot yet — revealing the full set for the first time with extra space on both sides of the table. The host announces the twist. Two new figures emerge from off-camera (or from behind a partition — production's call on maximum dramatic staging). The new partners pull up chairs and sit beside their respective person. The table suddenly feels crowded. Camera captures the exes' real-time panic in split-screen close-ups before cutting to the new partners' faces — some stone-cold, some already shaking their heads, some laughing in disbelief. A graphic slams on screen: **"THEY WATCHED THE LAST 3 ANSWERS."**

**On-Screen Action & Key Dialogue:**
The new partners sit down. Silence. Then one of them says something like "A six? You gave me a SIX?" or "So I 'don't get his humor'? Interesting." The exes scramble — backpedaling, laughing, trying to explain. The host asks the new partners: "Having seen what you just saw — do you have a question for your partner's ex?" The new partner asks something pointed and the whole table erupts. One ex tries to charm their way out; the other owns it completely ("I said what I said"). The final moment is a cooldown — the host asks everyone: "Any last words?" Someone delivers a line that's either deeply chaotic or unexpectedly gracious. The group clinks glasses — all four of them now — in a messy, reluctant toast. End card with CUT branding and a prompt: **"Should we do this with YOUR ex? Comment below."**

**Mood:** 😱➡️😂➡️🫶 Shock into chaos into a weird, cathartic release. The twist reframes everything the audience just watched — every rating, every "honest" answer now has consequences sitting right there at the table. It's the moment that makes people rewind the whole video.
