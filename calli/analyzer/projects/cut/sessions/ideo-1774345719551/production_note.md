## Concept
Two exes sit across from each other at a table loaded with shots — but instead of rehashing their own relationship, they're forced to answer brutally honest questions about each other's new partners, rating them on looks, personality, compatibility, and the one thing they'd never say out loud. But the twist is that in the final round, the new partners walk in and watch the last three answers back on a monitor. The core tension isn't jealousy — it's the collision between performative indifference ("I'm so over you") and the raw truth that surfaces when alcohol and pointed questions strip away the act.
**The Hook:**  Two exes who claim they're "totally cool now" are about to rate each other's new partners on camera — and the new partners are watching from the next room. You already know someone's lying about being over it, and you need to see the moment the mask cracks.
**The WTF Moment:**  In Round 3, Dani is asked "What's one thing your ex's new partner does better than you in bed?" She reaches for the drink, hesitates, puts it down, and says: "Honestly? Nothing. But he seems happier, and I think that's because she's less afraid to be boring with him." Marcus stares at her for a full four seconds. The room is silent. Then he drinks anyway.

## Cast
Dani — The Composed One Who Isn't
28, graphic designer, speaks in measured sentences and maintains eye contact like a therapist. She broke up with Marcus but clearly hasn't processed it. Her composure is the shell the format is designed to crack.
**Reveal:**  "I didn't leave because I stopped loving you. I left because you made me feel like loving you was my whole personality."
Marcus — The Charming Deflector
30, bartender and part-time DJ, uses humor as armor. He'll turn every painful question into a bit — until he can't. His new girlfriend is watching, and his inability to fully dismiss Dani will be the episode's central tension.
**Reveal:**  "I told myself I was over it the day I changed your contact name back from the emoji to your real name. That was last week."
Priya — Marcus's New Partner (Late Entry)
26, data analyst, sharp and self-possessed. She walks in during the final round and her micro-expressions — the raised eyebrow, the slight jaw clench — become the episode's most rewatchable moments.
**Reveal:**  "I knew he wasn't over her. I just thought I could be enough to make that not matter."
Jordan — Dani's New Partner (Late Entry)
29, high school teacher, genuinely kind, slightly nervous on camera. His presence reframes everything Dani said — when he watches her answers, his quiet nod of understanding is the emotional payoff.
**Reveal:**  "She told me about this before she agreed to do it. That's how I knew she was different with me."
**Chemistry** & Group Dynamic: The first two-thirds are a two-hander — Dani and Marcus have the electric, unfinished energy of people who broke up mid-sentence. They know each other's rhythms, finish each other's deflections, and the familiarity is both comforting and dangerous. When Priya and Jordan enter, the dynamic fractures into four corners: jealousy, protectiveness, curiosity, and unexpected empathy. The new partners aren't villains — they're mirrors.
Character Archetypes:

**Hero:**  Dani — audience roots for her honesty even when it's uncomfortable
**Villain:**  Marcus (soft villain) — his charm reads as avoidance, and the audience wants him pinned down
Underdog: Jordan — quiet, kind, overshadowed by the ex-drama, but delivers the emotional landing
Chaotic Wildcard: Marcus doubles as this — his humor is unpredictable and sometimes lands in genuinely shocking territory
**Super Fun:**  Marcus's one-liners and Dani's deadpan reactions drive entertainment
**Heartwarming** (in ending): Jordan and Priya — their grace under pressure reframes the whole episode as a story about choosing forward


## The Loop
**Core Loop:**  Question posed → ex must answer honestly about new partner OR drink → answer triggers visible emotional reaction → next question escalates the stakes
**Loop Family:**  Truth/Reveal + Pressure
**Why** This Loop Fits: Truth or Drink is the purest Pressure/Reveal hybrid — the drink itself is a visible, countable currency of avoidance, and every answer is a micro-reveal that recontextualizes the relationship. The "rating the new partner" angle adds a Compatibility sub-loop where the audience is constantly evaluating: are they really over each other?
Secondary Loops:

Drink count → mounting suspicion ("what are they hiding?") → eventual confession
New partner reaction shots → audience prediction ("how will they take it?") → delayed emotional payoff

**Currency of tension:**  Emotional exposure. Every honest answer costs social capital with the new partner; every drink costs credibility ("you're clearly not over it").
**Ramp:**  Staircase with a cliff — Rounds 1-2 escalate steadily (surface → personal → sexual → emotional), then the new partners walking in at Round 3 is a vertical spike that resets the stakes entirely.
**Payoff:**  Are they actually over each other — and can the new relationships survive what's said?

## Psychological Drivers
**Primary Drivers:** 

Vulnerability — Every answer requires choosing between emotional exposure and the visible cowardice of drinking. The audience feels the weight of each decision.
Rejection anxiety — The new partners are watching. Every word could end a current relationship, and the audience is bracing for that moment.
Prediction tension — Viewers are constantly guessing: will they drink or answer? What will the new partner's face do? This fires every 30-45 seconds.
Social comparison — The format forces explicit comparison between old and new partners, activating the audience's own relationship scorecards.

**Secondary Drivers:** 

Second-hand embarrassment — Marcus's attempts at humor when a question cuts too close create cringe-comedy tension the audience can't look away from.
Authenticity detection — Gen Z audiences are hypertuned to performative vs. genuine emotion; the drink mechanic is a built-in lie detector.
Attraction uncertainty — The lingering chemistry between exes makes the audience question whether the new relationships are stable, activating parasocial investment.
Moral judgment — Viewers will take sides (Team Dani vs. Team Marcus), fueling comment section debate.
Self-projection — "What would I say?" fires on nearly every question, driving active viewer participation.


## Story Structure
Act 1: The Warm Lie (0:00–2:00)
Story intent: Establish the premise, introduce the exes, let the audience calibrate their "over it" claims against visible body language.

**Cold open:**  mid-answer moment (Dani saying "Nothing" — pulled from Round 3)
Smash to title card
Dani and Marcus sit down, introduce themselves, explain how long they dated (3 years) and how long ago they broke up (8 months)
First two icebreaker questions — light, funny, both answer easily
Marcus makes a joke; Dani's laugh is a half-second too long

Act 2: The Cracks (2:00–5:30)
Story intent: Escalate from surface to personal to sexual to emotional. Drink count rises. The audience starts keeping score.

Questions shift to rating new partners on specific traits
First drink taken (Marcus, on "What's one thing your ex's new partner is better at than you?")
Dani answers a question Marcus drinks on — power shift
The sexual round: both visibly uncomfortable, timing of the sexual escalation matters
The emotional round: "Do you think your ex settled?" — longest pause of the episode

Act 3: The Collision (5:30–8:00)
Story intent: New partners enter. Everything said is now accountable. The format transforms from confessional to confrontation.

Host announces the twist: "Your new partners have been watching from the next room"
Door opens. Priya and Jordan walk in.
Monitor replays the last three answers
Reaction shots: Priya's jaw clench, Jordan's quiet nod
Each new partner gets to ask ONE question to their partner's ex
Priya to Dani: "Do you still love him?" — Dani drinks. The audience loses it.

Act 4: The Landing (8:00–9:30)
Story intent: Emotional resolution. Not neat, not fake — honest.

All four at the table. Host asks: "What do you want for your ex that you couldn't give them?"
Marcus, for the first time without humor: "Stability. I want him to give her stability."
Jordan reaches across and squeezes Dani's hand
Final shot: all four walking out separately, but Priya and Marcus are holding hands — she chose to stay
End card with drink count graphic

**Cold Open** (25 seconds, shot-by-shot):
0:00–0:03: Black screen. Audio of Dani: "Honestly? Nothing."
0:03–0:06: Cut to Marcus's face — eyes widening, jaw tightening. Handheld, shallow depth of field.
0:06–0:09: Wide shot of the table — bottles, shot glasses, two people leaning in.
0:09–0:12: Dani, mid-sentence: "But he seems happier, and I think that's because she's less afraid to be boring with him."
0:12–0:16: Marcus reaches for the glass. Holds it. The drink or the hesitation — which is more magnetic?
0:16–0:20: Smash cut to title card with bass drop.
0:20–0:25: Quick montage: both laughing, both uncomfortable, the door opening, Priya's face.
**First Three Minutes:** 

0:00–0:25: Cold open as above — drops viewer into peak emotional tension
0:25–1:00: Context & format: "These two dated for 3 years. They broke up 8 months ago. Today they're rating each other's new partners — and the new partners are watching." This is delivered via text cards intercut with Dani and Marcus sitting down, the energy still light.
1:00–1:30: Stakes & cast: Quick intros. Marcus: "We're cool. We're so cool." Dani: "We're fine." The audience immediately detects the lie.
1:30–2:00: First question (icebreaker): "On a scale of 1-10, how attractive is your ex's new partner?" Both answer — Marcus says "solid 8" with a grin, Dani says "he's very handsome" without a number. The asymmetry is the first crack.
2:00–3:00: First escalation: "What's one thing your ex's new partner has that you don't?" Marcus drinks. First drink of the episode. The audience locks in — what is he hiding? This is the early spike driver.


## Emotional Design
**Tone:**  Warm but unflinching — the set says "safe space," the questions say "nowhere to hide." Think therapy session meets house party.
**Dominant Emotions:**  Tension, nostalgia, second-hand embarrassment, unexpected tenderness, catharsis.
**Emotional Arc:**  Playful bravado → creeping discomfort → raw honesty → shock (new partners enter) → grace under pressure → bittersweet resolution.
**Tension Source:**  The gap between what exes perform ("I'm over it") and what the drink mechanic reveals (they're not). Amplified 10x when new partners enter.
**Humor:**  Character-driven — Marcus's deflection humor, Dani's deadpan, the absurdity of rating someone's new partner to their face. Never scripted, always reactive.

## Production Design
**Set:**  Intimate table for two (expanding to four), warm amber lighting, exposed brick background, bottles and shot glasses arranged like a still life — lighting tone signals this isn't a game show, it's a confessional.
**Camera:**  Three-camera setup — two tight singles (shallow DOF, 85mm equivalent) and one wide; handheld B-roll camera for reaction shots when new partners enter; slow push-ins during emotional peaks.
**Graphics:**  Minimal — drink counter in bottom corner (running tally), question cards that slide in from the left with a soft chime, relationship timeline graphic in the intro (dated 3 years, broke up 8 months ago).
**Music** & Sound: Lo-fi ambient bed that drops out completely during emotional peaks — silence is the loudest production choice. Subtle bass hit on each question card. No laugh track, no reaction sound effects.
