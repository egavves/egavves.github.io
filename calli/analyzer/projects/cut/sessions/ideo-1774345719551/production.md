**Format:** Two strangers, one bottle, zero filter | **Target Runtime:** 8–12 min (final cut) / 12–14 min (raw shoot) | **Platform Priority:** YouTube (long-form), TikTok/Reels (clips)

## Shooting Setup & Location

### Set Design

| Element | Specification | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Backdrop** | CUT signature seamless white cyclorama wall | Clean, distraction-free — lets personalities pop. No busy set dressing. |
| **Table** | Minimalist round bistro table, 30" diameter, matte black or natural wood | Small enough to create intimacy; round shape removes "sides" power dynamic |
| **Seating** | Two matching stools, backless, medium height | Backless forces open body language; matching stools = visual equality |
| **Floor** | Polished concrete or white seamless paper carried to floor | Reflects subtle fill light; easy cleanup for spills |
| **Accent Dressing** | Single small succulent or candle (unlit) center-table; nothing else | [[H01:minimal set vs. styled set engagement test|Hypothesis: stripped-back set outperforms decorated sets for Gen Z authenticity perception]] |
| **Bottle Display** | One premium-looking tequila bottle + two shot glasses, pre-poured lineup of 6 shots each on a small tray | Visual "ammo count" — audience tracks how many drinks are left, builds tension |
| **Card Stack** | Branded "Truth or Drink" card deck, face down, center table | Physical prop drives pacing; participants draw cards on camera |

### Lighting Design

| Light | Type | Position | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Key Light** | 2× Aputure 600d w/ large softbox (4×4) | 45° camera-left, elevated 7ft | Soft, even, flattering — Gen Z expects "good skin" lighting |
| **Fill Light** | 1× Aputure 300d w/ diffusion frame | Camera-right, lower intensity (2:1 ratio) | Gentle fill to reduce harsh shadows without killing dimension |
| **Hair/Rim Light** | 2× Aputure 300x (one per participant) | Behind & above each participant | Separates subjects from white backdrop; adds cinematic depth |
| **Background Light** | 2× LED panels w/ diffusion | Aimed at cyc wall | Even white wash — no hotspots, no gray zones |
| **Practical/Accent** | Small LED strip under table edge (warm, dim) | Hidden beneath table lip | Subtle warm glow on the bottle/glasses — makes drinks look inviting on camera |

**Overall Feel:** Bright, clean, and intimate. Think "therapy session meets house party." The white void focuses 100% of attention on facial expressions and body language. Lighting should be flattering enough that participants feel confident but not so polished it feels corporate. [[H02:warm vs. cool color temperature on Gen Z watch time|We hypothesize slightly warm white (4300K) outperforms cool daylight (5600K) for perceived authenticity.]]

## Pacing & Editing Notes

### Act-by-Act Structure

| Act | Timecode (Final Cut) | Content | Energy Level | Editing Style |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Cold Open Hook** | 0:00–0:15 | Hard cut mid-action — shot glass slam, tequila splash, participant mid-laugh or mid-shock. No intro slate. | 🔥🔥🔥 (10/10 — instant grab) | Smash cut, punchy sound design, bass drop or record scratch. Text overlay: "TRUTH OR DRINK: GEN Z EDITION" |
| **ACT I — Meet & Vibe Check** | 0:15–1:30 | Participants introduce themselves, first impressions, light banter. "What's your red flag?" opener. | 😄 (6/10 — warm, curious) | Natural pacing, let the chemistry breathe. Jump cuts to trim dead air. Lower thirds with names/ages/zodiac signs (Gen Z catnip). |
| **ACT II — Light Questions** | 1:30–3:30 | Rounds 1–3. Icebreaker-tier questions: "What's the most unhinged thing on your phone?" / "Read your last DM out loud." | 😂 (7/10 — building) | Rhythmic card-flip transitions. Pop-up text of the question on screen. Reaction zoom-ins when someone hesitates. |
| **ACT III — Escalation** | 3:30–6:30 | Rounds 4–7. Mid-tier vulnerability: "What's a lie you're currently living?" / "Who's someone you owe an apology?" Drinks start flowing. | 😬🔥 (8/10 — tension + laughs) | Slower cuts during genuine moments. Hold on faces longer. Use silence intentionally. Music dips out during real answers. Drink counter graphic appears. |
| **ACT IV — The Deep End** | 6:30–9:00 | Rounds 8–10. High-vulnerability: "What are you most afraid people will find out about you?" / "Have you ever been in love with someone who didn't love you back?" | 😢❤️ (9/10 — raw emotion) | Minimal cuts. Let the camera linger. If someone tears up, HOLD the shot — don't cut away. Subtle piano or ambient tone underneath. This is the viral moment. |
| **ACT V — Cooldown & Close** | 9:00–10:30 | Final question is always wholesome: "What's something you admire about me based on tonight?" Participants exchange contacts/hugs. | 🥹 (7/10 — warm resolution) | Gentle pacing. Wide shot for the hug/handshake. Fade to black. End card with CUT branding + subscribe CTA. |
| **Post-Credits Tag** | 10:30–10:45 | Outtake or the single funniest unused moment — the "wait, go back" clip for social sharing. | 😂 (10/10 — send-off) | Quick, punchy, ends on a laugh. |

### Editing Principles

- **Jump cuts are your best friend** — Gen Z expects them. Trim every pause over 1.5 seconds unless the silence is *doing something.*
- **Text on screen** — Every question should appear as a pop-up graphic when the card is read. Use a bold, clean sans-serif (e.g., Druk Wide or similar).
- **Reaction zooms** — When someone's face says it all, punch in 120–150% on the 4K footage. This is CUT's signature move.
- **Sound design** — Subtle but present. Glass clinks, liquid pours, card flips should all be crisp and slightly boosted. Use a light vinyl crackle or lo-fi ambient bed under Acts I–II, strip it away for Acts III–IV.
- **Music** — Licensed lo-fi/bedroom pop for light sections; pull music entirely during vulnerable moments. Music re-enters gently for the cooldown.
- **Drink counter** — Optional on-screen tally ("Shots taken: 3 / Truths told: 5") to gamify the experience. [[H03:drink counter overlay vs. no counter on engagement metrics|Test whether the gamified counter increases average view duration or feels gimmicky.]]
- **Clip extraction** — Editor flags 5–8 moments during the main edit that can be cut as standalone 15–60 sec vertical clips for TikTok/Reels/Shorts. These should be pulled *before* the full video publishes to seed anticipation.
- **Color grade** — Warm, slightly lifted blacks, gentle skin-tone-friendly grade. Not flat, not cinematic — "elevated natural."
- **Thumbnail frame** — Editor pulls 3–5 candidate freeze frames (mid-reaction faces, mid-drink moments) for the thumbnail designer.

## Key Risks & Contingencies

| Risk | Impact | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Participant gets too intoxicated** | Ethical/legal liability; unusable footage; brand damage | Medium | Duty of Care monitor on set with authority to pause/stop. Pre-set max of 6 shots per person. Shots are half-pours (0.75 oz, not 1.5 oz). Water and snacks available. Rideshare arranged for all participants post-shoot. |
| **Low chemistry between strangers** | Flat, unengaging content; video underperforms | Medium-High | Cast 3 pairs per shoot day; use the best pairing. Director warms them up with 10 min off-camera conversation before rolling. Have backup "chaos agent" questions if energy dips. |
| **Participant refuses to answer AND drink (stalls the game)** | Pacing dies; segment is unusable | Medium | Director has "lifeline" mechanic ready: participant can call a friend on speaker, or swap the question for a dare. Builds this into the rules at the top. |
| **Alcohol spill on equipment** | Gear damage; downtime | Low | All cables routed away from table. Lens cloths on standby. C-cam uses rain cover as precaution. PAs have towels within arm's reach. |
| **Legal/consent issue post-shoot** | Participant wants footage pulled after signing release | Low | Thorough consent process: written release signed pre-shoot, verbal on-camera consent at start, 48-hour "cooling off" clause in contract where participant can request removal of specific answers (not full video). |
| **Content too edgy / brand risk** | Sponsor pullback; demonetization; audience backlash | Medium | Question deck is pre-approved by producer. Three tiers clearly labeled (Light / Medium / Deep). No questions about minors, illegal activity, or non-consensual situations. Editor flags anything questionable for producer review before final cut. |
| **Audio failure (lav mic drops)** | Unusable dialogue | Low | Redundant boom mic recording full session. Sound mixer monitors live and calls for reset immediately if a lav cuts out. Backup lav packs on standby. |
| **Shoot runs long / talent fatigue** | Diminishing energy; overtime costs | Medium | Hard 90-minute shoot window per pair (including setup/reset). Director has a shot list with "must-get" moments prioritized. If energy peaks early, pivot to closing questions rather than grinding through all cards. |

# STORYBOARD

## Truth or Drink: Gen Z Edition — 4-Scene Storyboard

---

### Scene 1: The Setup — "So… This Is Gonna Be Messy"

**Visual Description:**
Two strangers (ages 20–25) sit across from each other at the minimalist round table against CUT's signature plain white backdrop. Camera opens on a **medium two-shot** (CAM A), slightly wider than we'll hold for the rest of the video — we see the full table, the tequila bottle, the lined-up shots, and the face-down card deck between them. Both participants are mid-nervous-laugh, hands in their laps or fidgeting with jewelry. The energy is "first day of school but make it spicy."

**Camera Coverage:**
- **CAM A:** Wide two-shot, locked off, establishes geography
- **CAM B:** Alternating medium close-ups on each face as they introduce themselves
- **CAM C:** Tabletop insert — slow pan across the shot lineup, lingering on the card deck

**Audio/Music:** Light lo-fi beat fades in under the introductions. Natural room tone. Lav mics pick up every nervous giggle.

**On-Screen Graphics:**
- Title card smash: **"TRUTH OR DRINK: GEN Z EDITION"** (bold, white text, slight shake animation)
- Lower thirds appear as each person speaks: *"JAYLEN, 22, Scorpio ♏"* / *"MAYA, 24, Gemini ♊"*

**Key Dialogue Beat:**
> **JAYLEN:** "Okay so I've never done this before and I'm already scared."
> **MAYA:** "Same. I literally almost didn't come today."
> **DIRECTOR (off-camera):** "That's exactly why you're perfect. Okay — rules are simple. Draw a card. Read it out loud. Answer honestly… or take the shot. Ready?"
> **BOTH:** *(looking at each other, then at the bottle)* "…No." *(laughing)*

**Edit Notes:** Quick jump cuts through the small talk. Don't linger — audience wants to get to the game. Total screen time for this scene: **45–75 seconds.**

---

### Scene 2: The Warm-Up — "Wait, You Did WHAT?"

**Visual Description:**
First 2–3 question rounds. MAYA draws the first card, holds it up, reads it aloud. The question appears as a **bold text pop-up** on screen. The energy shifts from nervous to playful as the ice breaks. First drink is poured and taken — CAM C captures the pour in **slow motion (60fps)**, liquid catching the light.

**Camera Coverage:**
- **CAM A:** Two-shot, begins a barely perceptible slow push-in over the course of the scene
- **CAM B:** Tight on the listener's face during each question — this is where the gold is (the *reaction* to the question before the answer)
- **CAM C:** Card flip close-up, pour shots, hand reactions (gripping the table, covering mouth)

**Sample Questions (on-screen text):**
- *"Read your last thirst trap DM out loud."*
- *"What's the most embarrassing thing in your camera roll right now?"*
- *"What's a lie you put on your dating profile?"*

**Key Dialogue Beat:**
> **JAYLEN:** *(reads card, immediately puts it face-down)* "Nope. Absolutely not. Pour me up."
> **MAYA:** "WAIT — you have to at least let me see it!" *(grabs card, reads it, SCREAMS)*
> **JAYLEN:** *(already drinking)* "I said what I said."

**Edit Notes:** This is the **rhythm-establishing** section. Quick cuts between question → reaction → answer/drink. Every card flip gets a subtle **whoosh** sound effect. When someone chooses to drink instead of answer, a small **"DRINK 🥃"** graphic pops on screen. When they answer, **"TRUTH 💀"** appears. Running tally optional per H03. Screen time: **2–3 minutes.**

---

### Scene 3: The Escalation — "I Wasn't Ready for That"

**Visual Description:**
Rounds 5–8. The energy has shifted. The table has fewer full shot glasses (visual proof of vulnerability). Body language has changed — participants are leaning in, making more eye contact, less fidgeting. One or both have loosened up (physically and emotionally). CAM A has pushed in noticeably — the two-shot is tighter now, more intimate. CAM B holds on faces **longer** — we see the micro-expressions, the swallows, the eye-breaks.

**Camera Coverage:**
- **CAM A:** Tighter two-shot now; fluid head operator ready for slow reframes
- **CAM B:** Extreme close-ups — eyes, hands, the moment before someone speaks
- **CAM C:** Near-empty shot tray, a single remaining glass, the discard pile of question cards

**Sample Questions (on-screen text):**
- *"What's something you pretend to be okay with but you're not?"*
- *"Have you ever loved someone who didn't love you back?"*
- *"What would your ex say is the reason it didn't work?"*

**Key Dialogue Beat:**
> **MAYA:** *(long pause after reading the card, looks down, then up)* "Okay… yeah, I'll answer this one."
> *(Beat. The lo-fi music fades completely out. Room tone only.)*
> **MAYA:** "I think I stay in situations way longer than I should because I'm afraid that if I leave, no one else will want me. Like… I know that's not true, but it *feels* true. You know?"
> *(JAYLEN nods slowly. Doesn't interrupt. Doesn't joke.)*
> **JAYLEN:** "…Yeah. I know exactly what you mean."

**Edit Notes:** This is the **emotional core** — the clip that goes viral. **Do NOT cut away during vulnerable moments.** Hold the wide shot or the close-up and let the silence breathe. No music. No graphics. No text pop-ups during the answer itself. The question can appear when the card is drawn, but once someone starts being real, the edit should become invisible. If a participant tears up, the camera stays. Screen time: **3–4 minutes.**

---

### Scene 4: The Close — "I'm Really Glad I Met You"

**Visual Description:**
Final round. The bottle is noticeably emptier. The card deck is nearly gone. Both participants look different than they did in Scene 1 — more relaxed, more open, sitting closer. The last question is always the **wholesome pivot** — designed to end on warmth, not wreckage.

**Camera Coverage:**
- **CAM A:** Pulls back out to the original wide two-shot — bookending the visual journey
- **CAM B:** Medium shot, capturing both body language and facial expression
- **CAM C:** Final detail shots — the empty glasses, the scattered cards, the bottle

**Final Question (on-screen text):**
- *"Based on tonight… what do you genuinely admire about me?"*

**Key Dialogue Beat:**
> **JAYLEN:** "Honestly? You're way braver than you think you are. Like, some of the stuff you said tonight — I don't think I could've said that

to a stranger."
> *(PRIYA looks down, genuinely moved. Presses her lips together.)*
> **PRIYA:** "Okay, don't make me cry again." *(laughs, wipes under her eye)* "You… you actually listen. Like, not performatively. You made me feel like what I was saying mattered. That's rare."
> *(Beat. They both smile — not for the camera, for each other.)*
> **JAYLEN:** "So… one more drink? Just because?"
> **PRIYA:** "Just because."
> *(They both pour. Clink glasses. Drink together. Laughter.)*

**Final Visual:**
Camera A holds the wide two-shot as they set their glasses down and lean back. Natural laughter fades. **SMASH CUT TO BLACK.**

**End Card (on black):**
- White text fades in: *"They exchanged Instagrams after filming."*
- Beat.
- Second line: *"Neither has posted the other yet. It's complicated."*
- CUT logo. Subscribe CTA. End screen with related videos.

**Edit Notes:** The close should feel **earned, not manufactured**. The wholesome pivot only works because Scenes 2 and 3 built genuine tension and vulnerability. Keep the final exchange unscored — ambient room tone only. Add a subtle music swell (lo-fi, warm) ONLY under the end card text. Screen time: **2–3 minutes.**

### Editing Principles

- **The 3-Second Rule:** When someone says something vulnerable, hold the shot for at least 3 seconds of silence before cutting. Discomfort is content. [[H03:silence duration retention|Test whether 3-second vs. 1-second post-vulnerability holds affect average view duration]]
- **Reaction > Statement:** The funniest and most emotional moments are often in the *listener's* face, not the speaker's. Prioritize reaction shots during Act II comedy beats.
- **Jump Cuts Are Your Friend (in moderation):** Use jump cuts to compress dead air between questions, but NEVER within a vulnerable answer. The audience should feel the difference between "produced content" and "real moment."
- **Sound is 50% of the Edit:** Clean, present audio with audible room tone creates intimacy. The viewer should feel like they're sitting at the table. No reverb. No compression. No normalization that kills dynamic range.
- **Text/Graphics Philosophy:** Less is more. On-screen question text serves a functional purpose (accessibility, clarity). Meme-style pop-ups serve an engagement purpose — but overuse destroys authenticity. Cap at 4–5 total in the entire video.
- **Thumbnail Moment:** During the shoot, the director should actively flag 2–3 "thumbnail frames" — moments of extreme facial expression (shock, laughter, tears) that will be pulled as stills for the YouTube thumbnail. Frame these consciously on CAM B.
- **Vertical Cuts:** Simultaneously produce a 60–90 second vertical cut (9:16) optimized for TikTok/Reels/Shorts from the best single moment in Act II or III. This is the discovery funnel — it must work independently of the full video.
- **Color Grade:** Warm, slightly desaturated, skin-tone-forward. Not overly stylized. The grade should feel like "reality, but prettier." Reference: CUT's existing library for consistency.
- **Export Specs:** 4K master (3840×2160), H.265, 40 Mbps+ for YouTube upload. 1080p proxy for internal review.

### Editing Principles

- **The 4-Second Rule:** No single shot holds longer than 4 seconds unless the moment *demands* it (genuine tears, stunned silence, uncontrollable laughter). Attention is earned, not assumed.
- **Jump Cuts Are the Grammar:** This isn't a documentary. Embrace visible jump cuts within singles — they signal authenticity and keep pace. Smooth transitions feel produced; jump cuts feel real.
- **Audio-First Editing:** The editor should build the first assembly with eyes closed. If the audio story works without picture, the video will be exceptional. Cut to the voice, not the other way around.
- **Reaction > Action:** The person *not* speaking is often the better shot. Train the edit eye to prioritize the listener's face — widened eyes, suppressed smiles, the slow blink of recognition.
- **Music as Seasoning, Not Structure:** Underscore with lo-fi, ambient, or minimal beats. Music should never tell the audience how to feel — it should amplify what they're already feeling. Pull music entirely during the most vulnerable moments. [[H03:music removal during peak vulnerability vs. continuous underscore on emotional engagement metrics|Test no-music windows against continuous bed in peak emotional beats]]
- **Sound Design Matters:** The clink of glass, the glug of a pour, the table slap of a laugh — these are the ASMR layer that makes the video *tactile*. Mix them forward.
- **Vertical-First Clips:** The social media editor identifies the top 8–12 moments during assembly and cuts 9:16 versions immediately. These clips should be able to stand alone with zero context — if a clip needs explanation, it's not the right clip.
- **Pacing Breath:** After every high-intensity moment, give the audience 3–5 seconds of recovery (a pour, a laugh trail-off, a "wow"). Relentless intensity causes fatigue; rhythm creates addiction.
- **Text Overlays (Sparingly):** Gen Z expects some on-screen text but rejects overuse. Use text only for: the question being asked (lower-third, clean sans-serif), participant names/ages on first appearance, and one or two comedic captions if a moment is too good not to emphasize.
- **Thumbnail Bait Identification:** Editor flags the single most expressive freeze-frame from the shoot for thumbnail testing. Ideal: one person mid-gasp, mid-laugh, or covering their face, with the other person visibly reacting. Emotion must be readable at 1-inch scale on a phone screen.

## Final Production Notes

- **Shoot day is sacred.** No visitors, no extra bodies, no distractions. The set should feel like a private conversation that cameras happen to be capturing — not a production.
- **The director's most important job is silence.** Don't fill pauses. Don't redirect too quickly. The best moments in Truth or Drink come from the 3–5 seconds *after* someone finishes speaking, when the weight of what they said lands.
- **Gen Z can smell inauthenticity instantly.** Every creative decision — from casting to question writing to editing pace — must serve genuine human connection. The moment this feels like a formula, the audience is gone.
- **This is a repeatable format.** Document everything. What worked, what didn't, which questions landed, which fell flat, what the talent said in their post-shoot debrief. The first edition builds the playbook. The fifth edition perfects it.
