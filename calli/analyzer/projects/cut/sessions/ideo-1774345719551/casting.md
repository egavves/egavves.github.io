## Demographics & Energy

| Parameter | Target |
|---|---|
| **Age Range** | 19–26 (born 1999–2005 sweet spot) |
| **Personality Types** | High-openness, emotionally literate, comedically reactive — people who process discomfort through humor but aren't afraid to sit in a feeling |
| **Diversity** | Non-negotiable multi-axis diversity: race, ethnicity, gender expression, sexuality, body type, neurodivergence, socioeconomic background, regional accent. Not performative — structurally built into the casting pool so every pairing feels *real*, not assembled |
| **Energy Baseline** | 7/10 at rest. These are people who walk into a room and the room subtly reorients. Not "loud" energy — *magnetic* energy. They make strangers want to confess things. |

---

## Character Archetypes Needed

- **Hero** — The emotionally generous one. Answers first, answers honestly, makes the other person feel safe enough to follow. Probably has a therapy vocabulary but wears it lightly. [[H01: Test whether a "Hero" who leads with vulnerability produces 2x more reciprocal disclosure from their partner|emotional generosity as contagion]].
- **Villain** — Not actually villainous. The one who asks the follow-up question the producers didn't write. Charismatic enough to make discomfort entertaining. Smirks before answering. Drinks strategically — not out of fear, but for *drama*.
- **Underdog** — Visibly nervous in the first 30 seconds, then delivers the most devastating honest answer of the episode. The audience surrogate. Their arc IS the video.
- **Overconfident** — Walks in saying "I'm an open book." Gets cracked open by question 4. The humbling is the content. Ideally someone who's extremely online and thinks they've seen every format — until they're *in* one.
- **Wildcard** — Genuinely unpredictable. Not performing chaos — actually operates on a different emotional logic. Could cry, could make the other person cry laughing, could pivot the entire energy of the room in one sentence. [[H02: Test whether Wildcard archetypes drive disproportionate comment section engagement (replies, not just likes)|unpredictability as engagement engine]].
- **Super Fun** — The person who turns drinking into a celebration rather than a deflection. Their laugh is the kind that makes *other people* laugh. They keep the energy from tipping into therapy session territory. Essential for pacing.

---

## What We Value (In Order)

1. **Comfort on camera under pressure** — Can they hold a pause? Can they sit in silence after a devastating question without reaching for a bit? The camera finds fraud instantly. We need people whose "on-camera self" and "real self" have a gap of < 5%.
2. **Experience with vulnerability formats** — Not necessarily on-camera. Could be: group therapy, AA, spoken word, improv (longform > shortform), support group facilitation, podcast guesting. We want people who have *practiced* being honest in front of others.
3. **Social native fluency** — They understand the rhythm of content intuitively. They know when a moment is "a moment." They won't look at the camera and explain the joke. They speak in the cadence Gen Z actually speaks in — not the SNL parody of it.
4. **Group chemistry instinct** — Even in a two-person format, we need people who *read the room* and adjust. They know when to push, when to pull back, when to laugh, when to let it land.

---

## Red Flags to Avoid

- ❌ **Follower count as a qualifier** — Vanity metric. A person with 800 followers who makes their group chat cry-laugh is worth more than a 200K creator who's never been genuinely surprised on camera.
- ❌ **Traditional acting credits** — We are not looking for people who can *perform* authenticity. We're looking for people who *are* authentic and happen to be watchable. Trained actors default to "scene work" under pressure — we need people who default to *themselves*.
- ❌ **Brand deal history / influencer résumé** — If their reflex is to protect a personal brand, they will pull punches. We need people with nothing to protect and everything to say.
- ❌ **"Content house" alumni** — The collaborative performance habits are almost impossible to unlearn for this format.
- ❌ **Anyone who describes themselves as "unfiltered" in their bio** — Truly unfiltered people never need to advertise it.

---

## Gender Mix & Regional Voice

**Gender Mix:**
- Target a **non-binary-inclusive mix** across the full casting pool. For any given episode pairing, avoid defaulting to M/F. Prioritize pairings that feel *unexpected*: two people who wouldn't normally be in the same room but discover immediate, electric chemistry.
- Across a season: aim for roughly **40% women / 30% men / 30% nonbinary & gender-expansive** — but let chemistry dictate final pairings, not quotas.

**Regional Voice:**
- Actively cast **outside LA/NYC**. Gen Z's cultural centers are decentralized: Atlanta, Houston, Chicago's South Side, Detroit, the Bay, the Pacific Northwest, the rural South, border towns, college towns, military towns.
- Accents, dialects, and code-switching are *assets*, not things to be smoothed out. A person from Brownsville, TX who switches between English and Spanish mid-confession is *gold*.
- At least **30% of the cast pool** should come from non-coastal cities. [[H03: Test whether non-coastal talent produces higher "relatability" scores in post-view audience surveys vs. LA/NYC-based talent|geographic relatability hypothesis]].

---

## Casting North Star

> **The ideal cast member is someone your friend would describe as "you HAVE to meet this person" — not because they're famous, not because they're beautiful (though they might be), but because five minutes with them makes you feel like you've known them your whole life and also like you know nothing about them.** They are emotionally courageous without being performative. They make vulnerability look like the most natural thing in the world — because for them, it is. Cast the person who, when they take the shot instead of answering, makes you lean forward and whisper *"what was the answer?"*

# TRUTH OR DRINK: GEN Z EDITION — FULL VIDEO SCRIPT

**Runtime:** ~12–14 minutes | **Format:** Two strangers, one bottle, zero filter

---

## ACT I — COLD OPEN HOOK (0:00–0:15)

*[COLD OPEN — HARD CUT IN. No intro slate. We're mid-action.]*

*[TIGHT CLOSE-UP on a shot glass being slammed down on the table. Tequila splashes over the rim. The sound is crisp — almost ASMR-level mic work.]*

*[WHIP PAN to PERSON A's face. They're mid-exhale. Eyes wide. Half-laughing, half-horrified.]*

**PERSON A:**
*(breathless, to no one in particular)*
Bro. You can NOT just *ask* that.

*[SMASH CUT TO: PERSON B, arms crossed, completely calm, the faintest smile.]*

**PERSON B:**
I didn't ask it. The card did.

*[BEAT. Then Person A reaches — not for the shot glass. For the card. They read it again. Their face changes.]*

**PERSON A:**
*(quietly, almost to themselves)*
…Okay. Fine. Yeah. Fine.

*[CUT TO BLACK. Title card — white text, no frills:]*

> **TRUTH OR DRINK**
> **gen z edition.**

*[Half-second of silence. Then —]*

---

## ACT II — THE MEET (0:15–1:30)

*[WIDE SHOT. The set is minimal: a table, two chairs, one bottle of tequila, two shot glasses, a stack of cards face-down. Warm lighting. Nothing sterile — it should feel like a kitchen table at 1 AM.]*

*[PERSON A and PERSON B walk in from opposite sides. They've never met. The energy is the specific electricity of two strangers who are about to know too much about each other.]*

**PERSON A:**
*(extending hand, then pivoting to a hug mid-reach — the classic Gen Z greeting negotiation)*
Oh — are we — okay, we're hugging. Cool. Hi.

**PERSON B:**
*(laughing)*
Hi. I'm [Name]. I'm a hugger. Sorry. Not sorry.

**PERSON A:**
[Name]. No, I love it. I just — I was not prepared to be touched yet.

**PERSON B:**
Yet? So there's a timeline?

**PERSON A:**
*(sitting down, already grinning)*
There's always a timeline.

*[They settle. Eye the bottle. Eye each other.]*

**PERSON B:**
So… do we just…?

**PERSON A:**
I think we just start pulling cards and ruining each other's lives.

**PERSON B:**
Beautiful. You first.

---

## ACT III — ROUND 1: SURFACE LEVEL (1:30–4:00)

*[GRAPHIC: "ROUND 1 — WARM-UP (but make it personal)"]*

*[Person A draws a card. Reads it aloud.]*

**CARD 1:**
> *"What's the most recent lie you told — and who did you tell it to?"*

**PERSON A:**
*(immediately)*
Oh, this morning. I told my mom I was "on my way" and I was literally still in bed. Like, horizontal. Pillow crease on my face.

**PERSON B:**
That's not a lie, that's a lifestyle.

**PERSON A:**
It's generational trauma, actually.

*[Both laugh. The ice isn't broken — it was never really there.]*

*[Person B draws.]*

**CARD 2:**
> *"What's something you pretend to like because everyone else does?"*

**PERSON B:**
*(long pause. looks at the ceiling. then:)*
…Matcha.

**PERSON A:**
NO.

**PERSON B:**
It tastes like lawn. I'm sorry. It tastes like if grass had ambitions.

**PERSON A:**
You just ended a $4 billion industry.

**PERSON B:**
I've been holding that in for three years. Three years of smiling through green foam.

*[Person A draws.]*

**CARD 3:**
> *"Have you ever stayed in a relationship longer than you should have? Why?"*

*[The energy shifts. Just slightly. Person A reads the card, and their smile doesn't disappear — it just becomes more honest.]*

**PERSON A:**
*(beat)*
Yeah. I have. I stayed because… I think I was more afraid of being alone than I was of being unhappy. And I didn't realize those were different things until way later.

*[Silence. Person B doesn't fill it. This is where casting matters — the right Person B knows that silence IS the response.]*

**PERSON B:**
*(softly)*
How much later?

**PERSON A:**
*(small laugh)*
Like… embarrassingly later. But I got there.

**PERSON B:**
You got there. That's the part that matters.

*[Person A nods. Pours a shot — not because they need to dodge the question. They already answered. They pour it because the moment needs a punctuation mark.]*

**PERSON A:**
Cheers to getting there.

*[They clink. Drink. Wince. Laugh.]*

---

## ACT IV — ROUND 2: GOING DEEPER (4:00–8:00)

*[GRAPHIC: "ROUND 2 — okay, it's getting real"]*

*[The bottle is noticeably lower. The postures have loosened. Person B is sitting cross-legged in their chair now. Person A is leaning forward, elbows on the table.]*

**CARD 4 (Person B draws):**
> *"What's something you've never said out loud but think about all the time?"*

**PERSON B:**
*(reads it. re-reads it. sets the card down. picks up the shot glass. puts it back down.)*

I think about whether my parents actually like who I turned out to be. Not love — I know they love me. But *like*. Like, if I weren't their kid, would they want to hang out with me?

*[Person A's face — and this is why we cast for emotional intelligence — registers the weight of this without performing shock or pity.]*

**PERSON A:**
Have you ever asked them?

**PERSON B:**
*(laughs, but it's the kind of laugh that's one degree away from something else)*
No. Because what if the answer is complicated?

**PERSON A:**
What if it's not, though?

**PERSON B:**
*(pause)*
…Yeah. What if it's not.

*[HOLD on this moment. No cutaway. No reaction shot. Just two people sitting in something true.]*

---

**CARD 5 (Person A draws):**
> *"Drink or answer: What's the worst thing you've done to someone who didn't deserve it?"*

*[Person A reads it. Exhales. Reaches for the bottle — then stops.]*

**PERSON A:**
No. I'll answer this. I… ghosted someone who was really good to me. Not because they did anything wrong. Because they were *so* good to me that I didn't know what to do with it. I didn't feel like I deserved it, so I just… disappeared.

**PERSON B:**
Did you ever go back?

**PERSON A:**
I tried. They'd moved on. Which — honestly? Good for them. But I still think about it.

**PERSON B:**
You should tell them that.

**PERSON A:**
*(gestures around)*
I kind of just did.

---

**CARD 6 (Person B draws):**
> *"What's a version of yourself that you've killed off — and do you miss them?"*

**PERSON B:**
Oh, this is a GREAT question. Okay. The version of me that people-pleased to survive. The one who would laugh at jokes that weren't funny and agree with opinions I didn't hold just to keep the room comfortable. I killed her off junior year of college.

**PERSON A:**
Do you miss her?

**PERSON B:**
*(without hesitation)*
No. But I'm grateful to her. She kept me safe when I needed to be safe. She just… overstayed.

**PERSON A:**
That's the most mature thing anyone has ever said at a table with tequila on it.

---

## ACT V — ROUND 3: THE DEEP END (8:00–11:30)

*[GRAPHIC: "ROUND 3 — no going back"]*

*[The energy is different now. They're not strangers anymore. They're two people who have decided to trust each other for reasons neither could fully articulate.]*

**CARD 7 (Person A draws):**
> *"Drink or answer: Are you the person you said you'd never become?"*

*[Long silence. Person A reads it twice.]*

**PERSON A:**
*(voice quieter now)*
In some ways? Yeah. I told myself I'd never be the person who prioritizes work over people. And then I looked up one day and realized I hadn't called my best friend in two months. Not because I didn't care. Because I'd gotten so good at being "productive" that I forgot being present is the whole point.

**PERSON B:**
Are you calling them after this?

**PERSON A:**
I'm calling them *during* this if they'll pick up.

*[Both laugh — real, full, the kind that resets a room.]*

---

**CARD 8 (Person B draws):**
> *"What do you want someone to say to you that no one has said yet?"*

*[Person B doesn't hesitate. Which is somehow more devastating than if they had.]*

**PERSON B:**
"I see how hard you're trying, and it's enough."

*[Person A's eyes visibly change.]*

**PERSON A:**
*(leaning forward)*
Hey. I see how hard you're trying. And it's enough.

*[Person B laughs — and it cracks, just for a second. They wave their hand in front of their face in the universal "I am NOT going to cry on camera" gesture.]*

**PERSON B:**
Okay, STOP. We've known each other for eleven minutes.

**PERSON A:**
Eleven really efficient minutes.

*[Person B pours two shots. Slides one across.]*

**PERSON B:**
This one's not because I'm dodging. This one's because we earned it.

*[Clink. Drink. The wince is smaller now — or maybe they've just gotten used to it.]*

---

**CARD 9 (Person A draws — the final card):**
> *"Last card. Say one true thing to the person across from you."*

*[Person A looks at Person B. Really looks at them.]*

**PERSON A:**
I think you're braver than you realize. And I think the people in your life are lucky — even if you haven't heard that enough.

*[Beat.]*

**PERSON B:**
*(nodding slowly)*
I think you're going to be okay. Whatever the thing is that you're carrying — you're going to be okay. And I think you already know that, you just needed someone to say it.

*[They hold eye contact. It's not performed. It's not directed. It's two people who walked in as strangers and are walking out as something that doesn't have a word yet.]*

---

## ACT VI — THE CLOSE (11:30–12:30)

*[WIDE SHOT. They stand. The hug this time is different from the one at the start — longer, tighter, real.]*

**PERSON B:**
Can I get your number? Like, genuinely. Not for content.

**PERSON A:**
Already in my phone. I stole it from the call sheet.

**PERSON B:**
*(laughing)*
You're UNHINGED.

*[They walk off set together, still talking, voices fading. The camera holds on the table: the bottle, two-thirds empty. The cards, scattered. The shot glasses, still wet.]*

## POST-SCRIPT: PRODUCTION & CASTING NOTES

- **Do not over-direct.** The script above is a *skeleton*. The actual magic is in the unscripted moments between the written beats. Give talent the cards. Give them the bottle. Get out of the way.
- **Card order matters.** The escalation from surface → personal → existential is the entire architecture. Don't shuffle.
- **The bottle is a character.** Every pour, every clink, every wince is a micro-story. Capture it all.
- **Two-camera minimum.** One wide, one tight. The tight camera lives on the *listener's* face, not the speaker's. The reaction is the content.
- **Music: almost none.** A low ambient tone under Round 1. By Round 3, it should be voice and silence only. Trust the silence.
- **Runtime discipline.** If it's running long, cut Round 1 questions — never Round 3. The depth is the product.
