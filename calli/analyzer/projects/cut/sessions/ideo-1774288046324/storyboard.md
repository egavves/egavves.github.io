# CUT Analytics — Storyboard v2.1

**"ALMOST SAID IT" — Exes Play Truth or Drink: But They're Still in Love**

Format: Two exes who everyone around them knows are still in love sit across from each other. Neither has admitted it since the breakup. Questions escalate from playful nostalgia into territory that forces one of them to either confess or drink their way through the most obvious feelings in the room.

Duration Target: 5:30–7:00 · Scenes: 5

---

## SCENE 1 — "THE SIT-DOWN"

**Timestamp: 0:00–1:15**

**Visual Description:**
Black screen. Two seconds of silence. Then a smash cut to Jess's face mid-reaction — eyes wide, mouth half-open, caught in something she wasn't ready to feel. Audio hits a half-beat late: "—you can't just *say* that." Hard cut to title card. We rewind.

Wide two-shot on a minimal set — matte black table, two chairs, warm overhead pendant light creating a pool of intimacy against a dark, slightly out-of-focus background. Camera is locked on a 50mm at eye level. Bottles (tequila, vodka, mystery mixer) are arranged between the participants like a barrier. Lighting is soft-key with amber tones — cozy but slightly interrogation-room. A second camera (handheld B-cam) captures tight singles over-the-shoulder.

**On-Screen Action & Key Dialogue:**
Participants introduce themselves with the careful, overly-casual energy of two people who've rehearsed how to seem fine around each other. "This is Jalen." "This is Jess." Beat. "And we… used to be together." Jess adds, almost correcting herself: "Like, *together* together." They share a look that holds one second too long. A title card smashes in: **ALMOST SAID IT — TRUTH OR DRINK.** Jess pours the first shot preemptively — "I already know I'm gonna need this." Jalen watches her pour and says nothing, but the B-cam catches him almost smile.

**Mood:** 😬 — Nervous energy, performative casualness masking something neither will name yet. The audience can already feel the gravity between them. The cold open has already told us where this is going — now we want to watch how they get there.

---

## SCENE 2 — "WARM-UP ROUNDS"

**Timestamp: 1:15–2:45**

**Visual Description:**
Same setup but now we're cutting faster between the A-cam wide and B-cam singles. Insert shots: fingers tapping the table, a shot glass being slid across, eyes darting away then drifting back. Lighting unchanged — the warmth feels deceptive now, like a living room where a hard conversation is about to happen. We test whether more frequent micro-cutaway inserts in this section improve 30-second retention. New insert priority: catch every moment one of them looks at the other when they think they're not being watched.

**On-Screen Action & Key Dialogue:**
Questions are playful but laced with nostalgia — the kind exes ask when they're testing whether it's safe to remember. Card reads: "Have you ever lied to me about who you were texting?" Jalen laughs too hard, deflects — "I plead the fifth… or the tequila." He drinks. Jess watches him drink and says quietly, "That's interesting." Second card: "Do you ever re-read our old messages?" Jess reaches for the bottle immediately — then stops. "Wait. I'll answer." She pauses. "Sometimes. When I can't sleep." Jalen's face does something he can't control. The B-cam catches it. He changes the subject fast.

**Mood:** 🫣 — Still in the safe zone, but the nostalgia is doing its work. Every "harmless" question is a door they keep almost walking through. The audience starts clocking the micro-expressions — the real game isn't Truth or Drink, it's *how long can they pretend they don't still feel this.*

---

## SCENE 3 — "THE TURN"

**Timestamp: 2:45–4:15**

**Visual Description:**
Lighting shifts subtly — the amber warms another half-stop, shadows deepen. The set feels smaller now, more private. A-cam pushes in slightly from the wide to a medium two-shot, compressing the space between them. B-cam goes tighter: jaw muscles, the way a hand grips a glass, a swallow before speaking. We're in close-up territory now. Insert: the question card being flipped, held in frame long enough for the audience to read it before they do.

**On-Screen Action & Key Dialogue:**
The cards stop being playful. Card reads: "What's something you wanted to say during the breakup but didn't?" Long silence. Jess looks at the bottle, looks at Jalen, looks at the bottle again. "I'll drink." She takes the shot. Jalen, quieter now: "I wouldn't have." "Wouldn't have what?" "Drunk. I would've answered that one." Jess: "Then answer it now." Jalen: "…That's not what the card asked me." The tension is a wall. Next card: "Do your friends think we should get back together?" Jalen laughs — a real laugh, almost helpless. "Bro. My mom asks about you every Sunday." Jess covers her face. "Stop." She's smiling but it's the kind of smile that's one degree from something else.

**Mood:** 💔🔥 — The game is now a vehicle and everyone knows it. They're not performing anymore. The drinks are becoming a shield — every shot taken is a confession dodged, and the audience feels each one.

---

## SCENE 4 — "ALMOST SAID IT"

**Timestamp: 4:15–5:45**

**Visual Description:**
B-cam is now the primary coverage — we live in the singles. The wide shot becomes the cutaway, used sparingly to remind us of the physical distance between them (which now feels almost absurd given the emotional proximity). One overhead insert shot: two hands on the table, inches apart, not touching. The lighting hasn't changed but feels completely different because *they* have changed — the warmth now reads as tender, not deceptive. The shot glasses are mostly full. They've stopped drinking. That's the tell.

**On-Screen Action & Key Dialogue:**
Final card territory. Card reads: "Are you still in love with me?" The room goes vacuum-sealed. Jess reads it aloud and her voice catches on "love." Jalen stares at the card, not at her. Five seconds of silence that will feel like thirty. Jess pushes the bottle toward him — giving him the out. He pushes it back. "I'm not drinking that one." Jess, barely above a whisper: "Then answer it." Jalen: "You already know." Jess: "I need to hear you say it." Jalen opens his mouth — and the edit hangs. This is the moment from the cold open. We've arrived. He says: "I never stopped. You know that. Everybody knows that." Jess's reaction — the one we smash-cut to at 0:00 — plays in full context now. "You can't just *say* that." "You literally asked me to." They both laugh — wet, overwhelmed, real. Neither reaches for a glass.

**Mood:** 🥹 — This is the peak. The title pays off — they've been *almost saying it* the entire video, and when it finally lands, it doesn't feel produced. It feels like two people who ran out of ways to hide. The audience has been holding their breath and this is the exhale.

---

## SCENE 5 — "THE AFTERMATH"

**Timestamp: 5:45–6:30 (+ optional tag to 7:00)**

**Visual Description:**
Pull back to the wide A-cam shot — same frame as Scene 1, but everything reads differently now. The bottles between them look absurd, a barrier that failed. Warm overhead light. They're leaning in closer than they were at the start — the geometry of the shot has changed because *they* moved, not the camera. B-cam catches a final insert: one of them sliding their hand across the table. Whether the other takes it — we cut before we see.

**On-Screen Action & Key Dialogue:**
Cool-down. The formal game is over. They're just talking now — half-laughing, half-processing. Jess: "So what do we… do with that?" Jalen: "I don't know. But I'm not taking it back." Jess looks at the camera for the first time — a tiny, almost conspiratorial glance, like she forgot it was there and is suddenly, sweetly embarrassed. Jalen, to camera: "Y'all got what you wanted." Smash to end card: **ALMOST SAID IT.** Beat. Final title: *"What would you confess?"* — CTA to comment.

**Optional tag (6:30–7:00):** Behind-the-scenes moment, unscripted. Crew audio leaks in. One of them says something we can't quite hear. The other laughs. It feels real because it is. Fade to black.

**Mood:** 🫶 — Warm landing. The tension has broken but what's left isn't resolution — it's possibility. The audience leaves wanting more, not because we withheld, but because the story is genuinely unfinished. Comment section becomes the sixth scene.
