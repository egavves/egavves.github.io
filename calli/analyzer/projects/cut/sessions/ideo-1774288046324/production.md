## 1. SHOOTING SETUP & LOCATION

### Location
**Primary:** Rented studio loft or blacked-out warehouse space (LA or equivalent). We want a void — the world disappears and all that exists is these two people and the bottle between them. No visible walls, no horizon line. Think CUT's signature look but moodier.

**Fallback:** Any space with minimum 20×20 ft clearable area, high ceilings (10ft+), and controllable ambient light (no windows, or fully blackout-able).

### Set Design
| Element | Spec | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Table** | Small round bistro table, matte black or raw dark wood, 28–30" diameter | Intimacy is mandatory — they should almost be able to touch each other's hands. Too big = emotional distance on camera. |
| **Chairs** | Two matching low-back stools or minimal chairs, black | No armrests. We want open body language to be a *choice*, not a default. |
| **Backdrop** | Seamless black (muslin or painted cyc) | We may add a single practical light (Edison bulb, warm) hanging slightly out of focus in the BG for depth, but the default is negative space. |
| **Floor** | Black seamless paper or matte black vinyl underfoot | Catches no reflections, disappears. |
| **Table Surface Dressing** | Bottle(s), two glasses, question cards (face down in a stack) | That's it. Nothing else. The minimalism is the point — nowhere to hide. |

### Lighting Mood & Feel
**Reference Mood:** Intimate, confessional, slightly dangerous. Think A24 dialogue scene meets late-night kitchen conversation at 2 AM.

| Light | Type | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Key** | Single soft overhead or ¾ front key (large book light or 4x4 frame w/ diffusion), slightly warm (3800K–4200K) | Wraps faces, keeps skin tones gorgeous across all complexions. Not flat — we want some gentle shadow on the far side of each face. |
| **Edge/Separation** | Two subtle backlights or kickers (Astera tubes or small LEDs w/ grid), cool-leaning (5200K) | Just enough to separate shoulders/hair from the black void. Creates a cinematic "floating in darkness" feel. |
| **Practical** | One warm Edison bulb hung slightly above and behind, visible but soft-focused | Gives the background a single point of life. Subconsciously signals "warmth" even as the conversation gets cold. |
| **Fill** | Negative fill (black flags on both sides) | We *want* shadow. Flat lighting = flat emotion. The shadows do half the storytelling. |

**Lighting Shift (Optional, Planned):** As questions escalate in Act 3, the gaffer subtly dims the key by 15–20% and the practical bulb warms slightly. [[H12: emotional lighting shift increases watch-through rate in final act|The audience may not consciously notice the room getting darker, but their body will]]. This is a testable variable — we shoot one pair with the shift, one without, and compare retention curves.

## 2. PROPS & WARDROBE

### Props

| Prop | Spec | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Liquor Bottle(s)** | 1 hero bottle (premium-looking tequila or whiskey, clear/amber liquid reads well on camera) + 1 backup | Label facing camera at top of shoot; after first pour, we don't care. NO brand deals unless cleared — use a clean/minimal label or decant into a beautiful unmarked bottle. |
| **Glasses** | 2x short rocks glasses, clear, heavy-bottomed | The *weight* of the glass matters — the sound of it hitting the table is a punctuation mark. No plastic. No stemware. |
| **Question Cards** | Custom printed, matte black card stock with white/off-white sans-serif type (our brand font) | Face down in a neat stack. Back of card: CUT Analytics logo, subtle. Front: question text, large enough to read on camera if a participant holds one up. **Print 30 cards even though we'll use 12–18.** |
| **Napkins/Tissues** | Small stack, hidden just off-camera on a low stool | If someone cries, we don't want a PA running in. Talent can reach for them naturally. |
| **Water** | Two water bottles, tucked behind/under chairs, out of frame | Non-negotiable. People need to hydrate between shots of liquor. |
| **Phone Basket** | Small basket placed off-set before shooting | All phones go in. No checking between rounds. Keeps them present. |

### Wardrobe Notes

**Philosophy:** These are real people, not characters. Wardrobe should feel like "what they'd actually wear to meet this person for a loaded conversation" — not costume-y, not overly styled.

| Guideline | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Color Palette** | Muted, warm tones preferred: olive, cream, rust, soft black, navy. Avoid: pure white (blooms on camera against black BG), busy patterns (moiré on camera), loud logos. |
| **Texture** | Encouraged. Knits, denim, cotton. Texture catches light beautifully and makes people look *real*. |
| **Fit** | Whatever makes them feel confident. We're not policing bodies. If someone shows up in a crop top and that's their armor, great. If someone shows up in an oversized hoodie and that's their armor, also great. The wardrobe IS the character work. |
| **Jewelry/Accessories** | Minimal. One or two personal pieces max. Dangly earrings or bangles create audio nightmares with lapel mics. Flag this in the pre-shoot talent call. |
| **Coordination Between Pairs** | They should NOT match. Subtle visual contrast reinforces that these are two separate people with two separate inner worlds. Wardrobe supervisor should see both outfits before they sit down and adjust if needed. |

## 3. CREW TABLE

| Role | HC | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Director** | 1 | Runs the room. Sits OFF-camera but within eyeline of both participants. Manages energy, calls card pulls, decides when to push and when to breathe. Must have strong emotional intelligence — this is 70% directing humans, 30% directing cameras. |
| **Producer / PM** | 1 | Manages schedule, talent logistics, releases, call sheets, catering. On-set: keeps time, tracks which cards have been pulled, flags if we're running long or short. |
| **Director of Photography (DP)** | 1 | Operates Camera A (hero two-shot / mid-shot). Calls framing adjustments to B and C ops. Owns the look. |
| **Camera Operator B** | 1 | Dedicated close-up, Participant 1 (e.g., JESS). Stays locked on face. Must be comfortable holding a static shot and making micro-adjustments silently. |
| **Camera Operator C** | 1 | Dedicated close-up, Participant 2. Same brief as B Op. |
| **Gaffer** | 1 | Sets and maintains lighting. Executes the subtle Act 3 dim if we're testing that hypothesis. Should be able to adjust silently mid-take. |
| **Grip/Electric Assist** | 1 | Supports gaffer, manages flags, stands, practical rigging. Also handles any set adjustments between pairs. |
| **Sound Mixer** | 1 | Runs a sound cart or bag. Two lav mics (wired preferred for reliability, wireless if wardrobe demands it) + one overhead boom as safety/room tone. Monitors for clothing rustle, stomach gurgles (real issue with alcohol on set), and emotional moments where talent drops to a whisper. |
| **Wardrobe/Grooming** | 1 | Light touch-ups between pairs. Checks for mic visibility, sweat, smudged makeup. On standby, not hovering. |
| **Production Assistant(s)** | 2 | PA 1: Set PA — resets table, refills glasses, swaps cards between pairs, manages talent flow (green room → set → wrap-out). PA 2: Talent PA — stays in green room with upcoming pair, keeps them comfortable and OFF their phones, manages release forms. |
| **Post-Production Editor** | 1 (not on set) | Receives organized footage + selects notes within 24 hrs. Should be briefed on the storyboard and pacing targets before the shoot so they can attend video village remotely or review dailies same-day. |
| **Data Analyst** | 1 (not on set) | Pre-shoot: provides benchmark data on Truth or Drink retention curves, optimal video length, and thumbnail click-through patterns. Post-launch: runs the hypothesis tests. |

**Total On-Set Crew: 12**

## 4. CAMERA STRATEGY

### Camera Count: 3 (minimum) | 4 (ideal)

| Camera | Assignment | Lens / Framing | Movement | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **A — Hero** | Wide-ish two-shot showing both participants, table, bottle, and negative space | 35–50mm (FF equiv), shot at T2.8–4 for moderate depth | **Locked on sticks.** Micro-reframes only between rounds. | This is our "geography" shot — the audience's home base. It establishes spatial relationship and lets us see body language (leaning in, pulling back, crossed arms, reaching for the bottle). |
| **B — CU Participant 1** | Tight close-up, JESS or equivalent. Eyes to chin, with breathing room above. | 85–105mm, T2 or wider for shallow DOF | **Locked on sticks or fluid head.** Operator makes subtle reframes to follow micro-expressions. | The money shot. When someone's eyes glass over, when they bite their lip before answering, when they look away — this camera catches it. Shallow DOF melts the black background into pure void. |
| **C — CU Participant 2** | Same as B, mirrored for the other participant. | Same as B | Same as B | Matching focal length and stop to B is critical — visual parity signals equal narrative weight. |
| **D — Roamer / Insert (IDEAL, NOT MANDATORY)** | Handheld or gimbal. Catches inserts: the pour, hands fidgeting, the card being flipped, a glass being slammed down. Also grabs over-the-shoulder angles for editorial variety. | 35–50mm, T2 | **Handheld, slow and deliberate.** Not run-and-gun. Think "curious observer." | This camera elevates the edit from "talking heads" to "short film." If budget is tight, cut this camera and have PA shoot inserts on a locked-off A7S between pairs. |

### Recording Specs
- **Codec:** ProRes 422 HQ or BRAW (depending on camera system)
- **Resolution:** 4K minimum (allows punch-in for additional CU options in post)
- **Frame Rate:** 24fps base. Camera B and C also roll select moments at 48fps for subtle slow-motion on key reactions (the moment before someone drinks instead of answering — [[H07: slow-motion drink moments increase share rate on short-form clips|stretching that decision beat could be the most shareable micro-moment in the video]]).
- **Color:** Shoot flat/log. Grade in post for the warm, contrasty, slightly desaturated look.
- **Sync:** Timecode sync across all cameras + scratch audio on each body. Slate each pair.
