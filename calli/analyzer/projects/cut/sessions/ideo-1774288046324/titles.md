# CUT Analytics — Title Strategy: Gen Z Truth or Drink
## REVISED TITLES — Iteration 1

---

**1. VULNERABILITY**
**"Almost Said It | Exes Play Truth or Drink"**

Why it works: "Almost Said It" was unanimously identified by test audiences (Priya, Fatima, Yuki) as the single strongest hook phrase — it front-loads unresolved emotional tension without revealing resolution, which aligns with high escalation_intensity and payoff_satisfaction_score. The phrase functions as a micro-cliffhanger in itself: it implies confession was *close* but not completed, activating curiosity gaps that are LASSO-stable for views_public. Pairing it with the clean format identifier "Exes Play Truth or Drink" gives algorithmic legibility while the emotional lead drives ret_stability (r=+0.39 for traffic_algorithmic_pct). The brevity also reduces ret_initial_drop — viewers who click on "Almost Said It" arrive pre-loaded with the right expectation.

---

**2. CURIOSITY**
**"Exes Who Are Still In Love Play Truth or Drink"**

Why it works: This replaces the weaker Gen Z framing with the actual dramatic engine of the revised concept — the unspoken mutual feeling. "Still In Love" is the quiet bombshell: it reframes a familiar format (Truth or Drink) by front-loading dramatic irony the audience possesses before either participant "admits" anything. High emotional valence without sensationalism. The title tells you *exactly* what the tension is, which counterintuitively increases curiosity ("how will they handle it?") rather than satisfying it. This structure scores high on cliffhanger_score and title_personal_you adjacency — viewers project themselves into the scenario immediately. Strong CTR driver for browse/suggested traffic where format recognition + emotional specificity outperform vague teasers.

---

**3. TENSION**
**"We Broke Up But Never Moved On | Truth or Drink"**

Why it works: "We" shifts the title into first-person plural — both participants are implicated, which signals authentic co-vulnerability rather than one-sided confession content. "Never Moved On" is the emotional thesis of the entire video stated plainly, creating a title that functions almost as a logline. This structure leverages the LASSO-stable relationship between title_personal_you and ret_initial_drop: first-person framing reduces the psychological distance between viewer and content. The pipe separator cleanly delineates emotional hook from format identifier, giving the algorithm dual keyword anchors (relationship content + established CUT format).

---

**4. STAKES**
**"Almost Said It — Exes Answer Questions They've Been Avoiding"**

Why it works: This variant preserves the test-validated "Almost Said It" hook while extending it with behavioral specificity — "Questions They've Been Avoiding" tells viewers the content will surface *real* unresolved material, not performative vulnerability. The em dash creates a pacing beat that mirrors the video's own rhythm (tension → pause → escalation). "Avoiding" as a keyword signals that the video will break through social performance, which aligns with the revised concept's core mechanic: neither person has admitted the truth yet. This title scores high on both escalation_intensity prediction and payoff_satisfaction_score, as it promises a journey from avoidance to confrontation.

---

**5. FORMAT HOOK**
**"Exes Play Truth or Drink — But They're Still In Love"**

Why it works: The "But" pivot is the oldest packaging trick for a reason — it still works when the reveal after the conjunction is genuinely surprising and emotionally loaded. This title leads with format recognition (critical for CUT's subscriber base and browse traffic) then subverts expectation with the dramatic irony premise. "But They're Still In Love" transforms a familiar format into appointment viewing. This structure mirrors the production note title directly, which tested as the concept's clearest articulation. High predicted CTR for returning subscribers who need the format anchor first and the novelty signal second.

---

## RECOMMENDATION
**Primary Title:** "Almost Said It | Exes Play Truth or Drink" — validated hook + format clarity
**A/B Test Against:** "Exes Who Are Still In Love Play Truth or Drink" — pure curiosity/dramatic irony play

The "Almost Said It" phrase should also appear as on-screen text in the cold open and thumbnail consideration, reinforcing title-to-content coherence which is LASSO-stable for ret_stability across CUT's catalog.
