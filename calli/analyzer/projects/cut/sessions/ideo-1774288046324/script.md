## SCENE 1 — "THE SIT-DOWN"

**[0:00–0:15 | COLD OPEN HOOK]:**

[COLD OPEN — No music. Black screen. We hear a voice before we see anyone. SMASH CUT to a tight close-up of JESS's face mid-reaction — eyes wide, mouth slightly open, caught completely off guard. The question card is visible but blurred in the foreground. A half-second of stunned silence.]

**JESS:** *(barely breathing)* "...You actually want me to answer that? With you sitting right there?"

[CUT TO: MARCUS across the table, leaning back, arms crossed, jaw tight — but his eyes are soft. There's something unresolved living in the space between them. He says nothing. He just gestures toward the bottle between them.]

**MARCUS:** *(quiet, almost a dare)* "That's the game."

[SMASH CUT TO BLACK. Title card slams on screen: **"ALMOST SAID IT"** — dripping text, the "I" replaced by a shot glass. Subtitle fades in: *Truth or Drink: Gen Z Edition.* A low bass tone hums underneath.]

**[0:15–1:15 | THE SIT-DOWN]:**

[WIDE TWO-SHOT. Minimal set — matte black table, two chairs, warm overhead pendant light creating a pool of intimacy against a dark, slightly out-of-focus background. Camera locked on a 50mm at eye level. Both JESS (24, oversized vintage tee, silver rings, nervous energy masked by humor) and MARCUS (25, clean hoodie, fresh cut, performatively calm) settle into their chairs. A bottle of tequila and two shot glasses sit between them like a loaded weapon. They avoid sustained eye contact — but keep almost making it. Little gravitational pulls they both pretend aren't happening.]

**JESS:** *(adjusting in her chair, half-laughing)* "Okay so like... hi."

**MARCUS:** *(small nod, tight smile)* "Hi."

[Beat. Awkward silence. They both look at the bottle. MARCUS's hand moves a centimeter toward the table, then stops — like he almost reached for something that isn't his anymore.]

**JESS:** "This is so weird. Why did I agree to this?"

**MARCUS:** *(dry)* "Because you love chaos."

**JESS:** *(pointing at him)* "Don't do that. Don't act like you know me."

**MARCUS:** "I mean—"

**JESS:** "You *used* to know me. There's a difference."

[Marcus exhales. Doesn't argue. But his eyes stay on her a beat too long — the kind of look that says *I still do.* He catches himself. Looks down. The tension is already here, and it's not the tension of two people who are over it. It's the tension of two people who never actually said the thing. A PRODUCER'S HAND enters frame and places a stack of question cards face-down on the table.]

**PRODUCER** *(off-camera)*: "Quick intros. Tell us who you are and what your... situation is."

[They glance at each other, silently negotiating who goes first. The glance lasts one second longer than it should.]

**JESS:** *(to camera)* "I'm Jess. Um. We were... a thing? For like eight months? And then we were not a thing. And now we're... this." *(gestures vaguely at the table)*

**MARCUS:** *(to camera)* "I'm Marcus. Yeah. What she said." *(pause)* "We haven't really talked in like... four months."

**JESS:** "Five."

**MARCUS:** *(looks at her)* "...Five."

[Beat. The correction lands. Not as a flex — as proof she's been counting. MARCUS registers it. His jaw works like he wants to say something. He doesn't. JESS looks away first, but her hand briefly touches the edge of the table closest to him — unconscious, withdrawn immediately.]

**JESS:** *(quieter, to no one in particular)* "Five months. Yeah."

[Neither of them reaches for the tequila yet. They don't need it — the game hasn't started and they're already exposed.]
