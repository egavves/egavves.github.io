# PRODUCTION NOTE: "Exes Play Truth or Drink… But On — Script

# CUT ANALYTICS — TRUTH OR DRINK: EXES EDITION

**FULL VIDEO SCRIPT | Runtime: 5–7 min | Participants: SIX PLAYERS — including JESS (22, she/her) & MARCO (21, he/him), who are secretly each other's ex. Neither knows the other was cast.**

---

## SCENE 1: COLD OPEN HOOK (0:00–0:15)

**[STAGE DIRECTION: COLD OPEN — No music. Hard cut to a TIGHT CLOSE-UP on MARCO's face, mid-reaction. His eyes go wide. A half-second of silence, then he exhales sharply. SMASH CUT to a WIDE SHOT: six people seated around a long table. Tequila bottle and shot glasses between them. Desaturated sage-green backdrop. Soft overhead key light (3200K), subtle halo edge light behind each participant. The CUT logo is small, bottom-right corner. JESS and MARCO are seated directly across from each other — but we don't call attention to it yet.]**

MARCO: [leaning back, hand over mouth] "Bro… you can NOT ask me that on camera."

JESS: [grinning, holding up the question card] "I didn't write it! I'm just the messenger."

**[TITLE CARD SLAMS ON SCREEN: TRUTH OR DRINK — BUT ONE COUPLE DOESN'T KNOW THEY'RE BOTH HERE — accompanied by a single punchy bass hit.]**

---

## SCENE 1B: THE SECRET — PRE-GAME CONFESSIONALS (0:15–0:35)

**[STAGE DIRECTION: INTERCUT — Separate confessional setups, shot in a different room. Tight frames on JESS and MARCO individually, recorded BEFORE they enter the main room. These are intercut rapidly, building tension.]**

JESS: [confessional, adjusting her hair] "They told me it's Truth or Drink with strangers. I'm freshly single, so — honestly? I could use a tequila and some chaos."

MARCO: [confessional, rubbing his hands together] "I just got out of something like a month ago, so this is either gonna be therapeutic or a disaster."

**[STAGE DIRECTION: GRAPHIC — clean white text on black screen: "Jess and Marco dated for 8 months. They broke up 5 weeks ago. Neither knows the other is here." Hold for 2.5 seconds. Let it land.]**

*The audience now holds information that both participants lack. This asymmetry is the engine of the entire video — every "normal" interaction between them is now laced with dramatic irony.*

---

## SCENE 2: THE SETUP (0:35–1:15)

**[STAGE DIRECTION: WIDE SHOT, 50mm lens, slow dolly-in. Six participants are finding their seats around the table. The energy is warm but awkward — strangers-meeting tension. JESS sits down, looks up, and sees MARCO across the table. A micro-beat. She blinks. He hasn't looked up yet. She recovers fast — but the camera catches it. A PRODUCER's voice comes from off-camera, casual and warm.]**

**[STAGE DIRECTION: CUT TO — TIGHT CLOSE-UP on JESS. Her smile freezes for exactly one frame. She pulls it back together. To anyone at the table, nothing happened. To the audience — everything just happened.]**

PRODUCER (O.S.): "Alright. Go around the table — name, age, and your biggest ick."

**[STAGE DIRECTION: We hear other participants introduce themselves first — quick cuts, friendly energy, table laughter — but the edit moves through them briskly, landing on JESS and MARCO.]**

JESS: [small wave, composed] "I'm Jess, I'm twenty-two, and my biggest ick is when someone sends a voice memo over two minutes long. Like, call me or don't."

MARCO: [laughs — a beat too quick] "Okay, valid. I'm Marco, twenty-one, and my ick is people who clap when the plane lands."

JESS: "Oh, that's universal."

MARCO: "It should be."

**[STAGE DIRECTION: MICRO-BEAT. They hold eye contact one second longer than strangers would. Neither acknowledges it. The other four players notice nothing.]**

PRODUCER (O.S.): "None of you have met before today. The rules are simple: you pull a card, read it to the person across from you. They either answer honestly… or they drink."

**[STAGE DIRECTION: CLOSE-UP on the tequila bottle. A hand reaches in and sets it center-table with a deliberate thunk.]**

JESS: [eyeing the bottle] "That's tequila."

MARCO: "Yeah, this is gonna go sideways fast."

*The cold open reaction beat is designed to stop the scroll before context is established. The confessional intercuts and the "neither knows" title card create an information gap the audience is desperate to see closed.*

---

## SCENE 3: ROUND 1 — "THE WARM-UP" (1:15–2:00)

**[STAGE DIRECTION: MEDIUM TWO-SHOT. The first few card pulls come from OTHER players at the table — quick, fun, establishes group chemistry. Then JESS draws a card. The edit tightens. She reads it silently, smirks, then reads aloud. She's directing it to MARCO — because he's across from her. Because that's the rule. Because the producers made sure of it.]**

CARD: "What's the most unhinged thing in your screen time report?"

JESS: [reading card aloud] "What's the most unhinged thing in your screen time report?"

MARCO: [immediate groan] "Truth. Okay. Last week I had… eleven hours on TikTok. In one day."

JESS: "ELEVEN?"

MARCO: "

**[STAGE DIRECTION: The table erupts. But JESS's reaction is a fraction bigger than it should be — she knows his screen time habits. She knows he doom-scrolls after midnight. The audience knows she knows. MARCO doesn't know she knows. The dramatic irony compounds silently.]**

---

*[Script continues — subsequent rounds escalate question intimacy. The questions are designed by producers to feel generic but land personally for Jess and Marco: "What's something you never told your last ex?" / "Have you ever been the toxic one?" / "What would you say to your most recent ex right now?" The table treats these as fun party prompts. For two of the six players, they are landmines.]*

---

**PRODUCTION NOTES:**

- The "neither knows the other is here" asymmetry is the **core mechanic**. It is never explained away, never softened, never broken by a reveal until the producers choose to break it (late in the video or in a Part 2).
- All six players are real. Four are true strangers. Two are the secret exes.
- Questions are rigged to escalate emotional proximity — but framed as standard Truth or Drink fare so the table doesn't suspect production interference.
- Every JESS/MARCO interaction is shot with **dual coverage** — their faces must always be capturable in the same moment.
- The audience holds the secret. That's the scroll-stop. That's the rewatch. That's the comment section.
