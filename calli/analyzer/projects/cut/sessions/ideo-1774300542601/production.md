# CUT Analytics — Production Plan
## "Truth or Drink: Gen Z Edition"

**Format:** Truth or Drink · **Target:** Gen Z (18–25) · **Runtime:** 5–7 min
**Participants:** 2 strangers, both Gen Z (JESS, 22, she/her & MARCO, 21, he/him)

---

## 1. SHOOTING SETUP & LOCATION

### Location
**Primary:** CUT's in-house studio (or rented cyclorama stage, minimum 20×20 ft shooting area). The entire video lives in one contained space — the table becomes the world.

### Set Design
- **Backdrop:** Matte black seamless paper or painted flat wall. Zero distractions. The CUT signature look — people are the set.
- **The Table:** A small, round bistro-height table (30" diameter, 36" tall) — intentionally intimate, forces proximity. Finished in matte black or dark walnut. The table should feel like a confessional, not a bar.
- **The Drinks:** Positioned center-table on a minimal black tray. We use a mid-shelf tequila (recognizable bottle shape, good on camera) and two shot glasses. A small carafe of water sits to the side — the "coward's option" we never explicitly offer.
- **Floor:** Black felt or dark concrete. No reflections competing with faces.

### Lighting Mood & Feel
- **Key Concept:** "Podcast booth meets interrogation room." Warm but directional. Intimate, not moody.
- **Key Light:** Large soft source (4x4 Kino Flo or equivalent LED panel through ½ grid cloth) positioned at ~45° camera-left, slightly above eye line. Soft wrap on faces, gentle falloff.
- **Fill:** Negative fill (black flag) on the opposite side to create a subtle shadow side — adds dimension without going dramatic. Fill ratio approximately 3:1.
- **Hair/Edge Light:** Subtle warm-toned backlight (Aputure 300d with CTO gel through a small softbox) to separate subjects from the black background. Should feel like a practical glow, not a "studio light."
- **Accent:** One small practical element — a single Edison bulb on a dimmer, visible in the wide shot hanging slightly above and behind the table. Gives the frame a focal warm point and a hint of "bar energy" without building a full bar set.
- **Color Temperature:** 3400K–3800K overall. Warm, flattering, skin-friendly. Grade in post leans slightly desaturated with lifted blacks — the current CUT aesthetic.

> **Feel in three words:** Warm. Bare. Magnetic.

## 2. PROPS & WARDROBE

### Props List

| Prop | Spec Notes |
|------|-----------|
| Tequila bottle | Mid-shelf, clear glass (Espolòn or Casamigos — label visible but not featured as sponsor unless paid). Must be real; authenticity matters for Gen Z trust. |
| Shot glasses (×4) | 2 hero + 2 backup. Clear glass, standard 1.5 oz. No branding. |
| Water carafe + 2 glasses | Clear glass. On-set hydration that also reads as "palate cleanser." |
| Question cards | Matte black cardstock, white sans-serif type (GT Walsheim or Helvetica Neue). Numbered. Slightly oversized (5×7) so they read on the wide shot. |
| Card holder/stack | Small black acrylic stand or just a neat face-down stack. |
| Napkins | Black cloth, not paper. Tucked to the side — practical but invisible. |
| Spit bucket (hidden) | Below table, out of frame. Legal/safety requirement. |

### Wardrobe Notes

**JESS (22, she/her):**
- Solid-tone top — think sage green, burnt orange, or cream. NO logos, no micro-patterns (moiré risk on camera).
- Texture is welcome: ribbed knit, cropped cardigan, linen. Should feel "her real closet" not "costume."
- Minimal jewelry. One or two small pieces max (stud earrings, thin chain). Nothing that catches key light or creates audio jingle.

**MARCO (21, he/him):**
- Solid dark tee (black, navy, charcoal) or an open button-down with a plain tee underneath. Slightly oversized fit — relaxed, not sloppy.
- No hats (shadows kill close-ups on black backgrounds). If he's a hat person, we negotiate on-set — could work for the wide but comes off for tights.
- One personal accessory allowed (ring, bracelet, chain) — these become fidget objects the cameras can catch during tense moments.

**General Wardrobe Rules:**
- No white (blooms on black BG), no busy prints, no branded clothing.
- Both participants should look like they *chose* their outfit, not like we dressed them. [[H14:authentic wardrobe vs. styled wardrobe increases perceived relatability and share rate|Authenticity in wardrobe choices may directly impact shareability]].
- Backup tops for both in complementary colors (in case of spills or color clashing on monitor).

## 4. CAMERA STRATEGY

### Three-Camera Setup

```
        [CAMERA C - Detail/Insert]
              (locked, top-down or 45°)
                    ↓
    ┌──────────────────────────────┐
    │          THE TABLE           │
    │   JESS ●          ● MARCO   │
    └──────────────────────────────┘
         ↗                    ↖
   CAMERA A                 CAMERA B
 (Hero / Wide-to-Mid)   (Reaction / Tight)
```

| Camera | Lens | Coverage | Movement | Notes |
|--------|------|----------|----------|-------|
| **A — Hero** | 50mm (FF equiv.) on Sony FX6 or similar | Starts wide two-shot (establishing), transitions to medium singles as conversation deepens. Primary editorial camera. | Dolly-in on opening shot (slider or dana dolly). Otherwise locked or subtle reframes on fluid head. | This is the "safe" camera — always has a usable frame. Shoots at 4K/24fps. |
| **B — Reaction** | 85mm (FF equiv.) on Sony FX6 or similar | Tight close-ups. Primarily covers the *listener* — the person NOT speaking. Catches micro-expressions, eye darts, suppressed laughs, the moment before a drink. | Handheld on monopod or easy rig for organic micro-movement. Operator has freedom to drift between subjects. | This camera creates the viral moments. The 85mm compression on black BG is gorgeous and isolating. Shoots 4K/24fps with option to overcrank to 60fps for slow-mo reactions. |
| **C — Detail** | 35mm (FF equiv.) on Sony A7IV or mirrorless | Top-down or 45° insert cam. Covers: hands, the pour, the shot glass lift, card reveals, fidgeting fingers, table energy. | Locked on tripod or magic arm. Repositioned between rounds if needed. | Shoots continuously. Editor pulls inserts as needed. Also serves as a safety wide if A goes down. 4K/24fps. |

### Technical Standards
- **Codec:** 4K ProRes 422 (or 10-bit 4:2:2 internal minimum)
- **Frame Rate:** 24fps primary, 60fps selectively on Camera B
- **Color:** S-Log3 / S-Gamut3.Cine (graded in post to CUT's house LUT)
- **Audio Sync:** Timecode jam-synced across all cameras + scratch audio on each body

## 5. PACING & EDITING NOTES

### Overall Rhythm
The edit should feel like a **conversation you're eavesdropping on**, not a game show. Pacing follows emotional arcs, not a metronome.

### Scene-by-Scene Pacing

| Scene | Timestamp | Pacing Notes |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| **1 — Cold Open Hook** | 0:00–0:15 | HARD CUT IN. No title card, no music. We drop the viewer into Marco's reaction mid-moment (pulled from Scene 3 or 4). 2–3 quick cuts: reaction → pour → drink → SMASH to title card. This is the "scroll-stopper." Silence or diegetic sound only. |
| **2 — The Setup / Intros** | 0:15–0:45 | Slow dolly-in on wide shot. Participants introduce themselves. Light music bed fades in (lo-fi, warm, non-intrusive). Pacing is relaxed — let the audience settle in. First impressions matter: we need viewers to *like* these two within 15 seconds. |
| **3 — Round 1: Warm-Up Qs** | 0:45–2:00 | Faster cuts. Ping-pong between speakers. Questions are light ("What's the most unhinged thing in your search history?"). Energy is giggly, exploratory. Use Camera C inserts of card reveals to punctuate transitions between questions. |
| **4 — Round 2: The Escalation** | 2:00–4:00 | Cuts slow down. Hold on faces longer. Let pauses breathe. This is where questions get real ("Have you ever been in love with someone who didn't love you back?"). When someone chooses to drink instead of answer — HOLD on the pour. Slow-mo optional. The drink IS the answer. Music drops out during the heaviest moments. |
| **5 — Round 3: The Gut Punch** | 4:00–5:30 | Minimal cuts. Let Camera B live on the listener's face. If someone tears up or goes quiet, we DO NOT cut away — we sit in it. This is the emotional peak. One question should land as the "moment" — the clip that gets pulled for socials. [[H07:emotional peak placement in final third drives higher completion rates|Placing the emotional climax in the final third is hypothesized to maximize watch-through]]. |
| **6 — The Cooldown / Goodbye** | 5:30–6:30 | Return to the wide shot. Energy lifts — they laugh, reflect, maybe hug. A brief "what did you learn?" moment. Music returns, warmer now. End on a genuine human beat, not a punchline. |
| **7 — End Card** | 6:30–6:45 | CUT logo. Subscribe CTA. "More Truth or Drink" playlist link. Clean, fast, no lingering. |

### Editing Style Rules
- **Jump cuts are fine** — they're native to the format and Gen Z's visual language. But use them for energy, not laziness.
- **Text overlays:** Minimal. Participant names + ages on first appearance (lower-third, clean sans-serif). Question text on screen when the card is read — helps accessibility and silent viewing.
- **Subtitles:** Full open captions, always. 68% of Gen Z watches with sound off first. White text, black outline or subtle drop shadow. Positioned lower-third, not center.
- **Music:** Licensed lo-fi / bedroom pop instrumentals. Never overpowering. Drops to silence for emotional peaks. No trending TikTok songs (licensing nightmare + dates the content).
- **Color Grade:** Warm, slightly desaturated, lifted blacks. Skin tones are sacred — never sacrifice skin for aesthetic.
- **Sound Design:** Subtle. The glass-on-table *clink* should be crisp. Liquid pouring should be audible. Room tone should feel present, not dead — a slight room ambience makes it feel real.

### Deliverables from Edit
1. **Hero cut** (5–7 min, YouTube)
2. **3× vertical clips** (30–60 sec each, TikTok/Reels/Shorts) — pulled from Rounds 2 & 3
3. **1× cold open teaser** (15 sec, all platforms)
4. **Thumbnail selects** (3 options, pulled from Camera B reaction frames)

## 6. KEY RISKS & CONTINGENCIES

| # | Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Contingency |
|---|------|-----------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | **Talent clams up / low chemistry** | Medium | High — kills the video | Director has 3 "chemistry unlock" warm-up exercises prepped (off-camera improv games, shared snack moment, "tell me something nobody knows about you" before rolling). If chemistry is dead after Round 1, we have a **backup participant on standby** (pre-cast, on-call within 30 min of studio). |
| 2 | **Over-intoxication on camera** | Low | Critical — legal, ethical, brand risk | **Hard limit: 4 shots max per participant.** PA tracks count on a tally sheet. Shots are poured at ¾ volume (not full 1.5 oz). Water and snacks available between every round. Producer has authority to call cut if anyone shows signs of impairment. Signed alcohol waiver includes acknowledgment of limits. Rideshare arranged for both participants post-shoot. |
| 3 | **Participant says something problematic / offensive** | Medium | High — post-production headache, potential brand damage | Director gently redirects in the moment ("Let's reframe that..."). In post, the editor flags any moment that could be misread out of context (especially for short-form clips). Producer reviews all cuts before publish. We do NOT air anything a participant asks us to remove — consent is ongoing, not one-time. |
| 4 | **Technical failure (camera, audio, media)** | Low | High | Camera C acts as emergency safety wide. All cameras record to dual card slots (simultaneous backup). Sound mixer runs 3 sources (2 lavs + boom) — any single failure still leaves us covered. DIT checks media integrity every 30 minutes. |
| 5 | **Participant requests removal after filming** | Low | Medium — lost content + reshoot cost | Consent form includes a **48-hour grace period** where participants can withdraw entirely, no questions asked. This protects the brand long-term — a forced publish that goes viral for the wrong reasons is worse than a reshoot. Budget includes contingency for one reshoot day. |
| 6 | **Content underperforms / doesn't resonate with Gen Z** | Medium | Medium | [[H22:two-stranger pairings with visible demographic contrast outperform same-demo pairings in Gen Z engagement|We're testing whether demographic contrast between strangers drives higher Gen Z engagement]] — if this hypothesis fails, we pivot casting for the next episode toward friends or situationships. First 24-hour metrics (CTR, avg. view duration, comment sentiment) are reviewed in a post-mortem within 72 hours of publish. |
| 7 | **Alcohol visibility triggers platform demonetization** | Medium | Medium — revenue impact | Thumbnail and title avoid explicit alcohol reference (use "Truth or Drink" as established brand, not "Getting Drunk With Strangers"). First 30 seconds of video contain no visible drinking (cold open uses the *reaction*, not the pour). Vertical clips for TikTok are reviewed against current community guidelines before posting. |
