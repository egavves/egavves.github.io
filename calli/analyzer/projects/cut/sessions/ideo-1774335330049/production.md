## Pacing & Editing Notes

**Act-by-Act Structure:**

| Act | Timecode | Duration | Content | Energy Level | Editing Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **ACT I — Cold Open Hook** | 0:00–0:15 | 15 sec | Mid-conversation bombshell moment pulled from later in the shoot; text overlay: "TRUTH OR DRINK: GEN Z EDITION" | 🔥 High (shock/laugh) | Jump straight into the most unhinged moment. No intro slate. Smash cut to title. This is the "stop scrolling" moment. |
| **ACT II — Meet the Strangers** | 0:15–0:55 | 40 sec | Participants introduce themselves; first impressions; light banter; rules explained organically ("You either answer honestly… or you take a shot") | 😊 Warm/Curious | Let awkwardness breathe. Use B-Cam singles. Subtle lower-third name/age graphics. Keep it quick — audience already knows the format. |
| **ACT III — Warm-Up Questions** | 0:55–2:30 | ~95 sec | Easy-ish questions to build rapport: "What's your body count?" / "Have you ever cried after sex?" / "Read your last thirst trap DM out loud" | 📈 Rising | 2-3 questions. Quick cuts. Reaction shots are king. Use C-Cam inserts of card reveals. First drink happens here — make it an EVENT (slow-mo pour option). |
| **ACT IV — The Deep Cut** | 2:30–5:30 | ~3 min | Escalating vulnerability: "What's something you've never told anyone?" / "What's the worst thing you've done to someone who loved you?" / "Are you actually happy?" | 💔 Emotional Peak | This is the heart of the video. Let silences land — DO NOT over-cut. Hold on faces. [[H02:holding reaction shots 3+ seconds before cutting increases comment engagement by 20-30%|Longer holds drive emotional resonance]]. Music dips to near-silence. |
| **ACT V — Chaos Round** | 5:30–7:00 | ~90 sec | Unhinged rapid-fire: "Who at this table would you hook up with?" / "Show me your screen time" / "Call your ex right now" | 🎉 Chaotic/Peak Fun | Fast cuts, jump cuts, sound effects (subtle — record scratch, vine boom used SPARINGLY). Energy release after emotional valley. |
| **ACT VI — The Closer** | 7:00–7:45 | ~45 sec | Final question + genuine moment of connection; participants reflect; potential hug/handshake/exchange socials | 🥹 Warm Resolution | Wide shot callback to opening frame. Let the moment be real. End on a laugh or a genuine human beat — NOT a manufactured cliffhanger. |
| **End Card / CTA** | 7:45–8:00 | 15 sec | CUT subscribe animation + "Comment which question YOU would've taken the shot for" | 📢 CTA | Standard CUT end card; overlay clickable elements for YouTube |

**Editing Principles:**

- **The 3-Second Rule:** Never let a shot linger past its emotional utility UNLESS it's a genuine reaction beat (see H02 above)
- **Sound Design:** Minimal music bed (lo-fi, soft piano, or ambient); music DROPS OUT entirely during vulnerable answers — silence = power
- **Text/Graphics:** Gen Z-native text overlays (lowercase, sans-serif, slight shake animation) for emphasis on key quotes — but use sparingly (max 5-6 per video). No cringe "EMOTIONAL" or "AWKWARD" labels
- **Jump Cuts:** Embrace them for comedic pacing in Acts III and V; avoid in Act IV (emotional continuity matters)
- **Thumbnail Frame:** Capture a genuine "mouth open" shock reaction during shoot — DP flags potential thumbnail moments in real-time
- **Vertical Cuts:** Editor produces 3-5 vertical clips (30-90 sec) optimized for TikTok/Reels/Shorts simultaneously; these should be self-contained moments, not just crops
- **Color Grade:** Warm, slightly desaturated, skin-tone-first grading. Reference: CUT's existing palette but push 10% warmer for this series
- **Pacing North Star:** If you're bored in the edit, the audience left 10 seconds ago. Cut faster than feels comfortable — then watch it back

## Key Risks & Contingencies

| Risk | Impact | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Participant freezes / low energy | Unusable footage; flat video; poor retention metrics | Medium | Cast 3 pairs, plan to use best 1-2; director has "energy reset" prompts ready; casting pre-interviews screen for on-camera comfort |
| Participant gets too intoxicated | Ethical/legal liability; unusable slurred footage; brand damage | Medium | Hard cap: 4 shots per person per session; PA tracks count on clipboard; water + snacks between rounds; no one drives home (rideshare vouchers provided) |
| Alcohol compliance issue (underage participant) | Legal catastrophe; channel shutdown risk | Low | Double-verify government ID at check-in; compliance officer present; signed age verification + liability waiver before ANY cameras roll |
| Participant crosses a line (offensive/harmful content) | Requires heavy editing or full reshoot; potential PR issue | Medium | Director has pre-established "redirect" phrases; participants sign content guidelines acknowledging CUT's right to cut any answer; editor flags in assembly |
| Participants have existing relationship / recognize each other | Destroys "strangers" premise; audience calls it out immediately | Low | Casting coordinator confirms no social overlap; separate green rooms; participants don't meet until cameras are rolling |
| Audio bleed / HVAC noise | Unusable audio on key moments | Low | Sound check 30 min pre-shoot; HVAC killed during takes; boom mic as backup track; room tone recorded for post patching |
| Set spill / glass breakage | Continuity break; minor safety hazard | Medium | 4 backup shot glasses; PA has towels staged; white vinyl floor wipes clean in <30 sec; C-Cam operator steps back during pours |
| Low YouTube CTR / retention drop-off | Video underperforms; series doesn't get greenlit | Medium | [[H03:testing 3 thumbnail variants and 2 title options in first 24hrs optimizes CTR above 8%|A/B test thumbnails and titles at launch]]; cold open hook designed to survive the first 5-second retention cliff; vertical clips seed audience on TikTok pre-YouTube publish |
| Emotional distress during vulnerable questions | Participant becomes visibly upset; ethical duty of care | Low-Medium | Director trained in de-escalation; any participant can pause or stop at any time (stated explicitly pre-shoot); quiet cool-down space available off-set |

## Scene 1: The Setup — "So… This Is Gonna Get Messy"

**Visual Description:**
Wide establishing shot (A-Cam) of two strangers — **JALEN** (22) and **MIKA** (21) — seated across from each other at the minimalist white round table. CUT's signature matte white seamless paper fills the background. The subtle pink neon "sip or spill" sign glows softly behind them. A bottle of Casamigos Blanco sits between two empty shot glasses on an acrylic tray. A stack of matte black question cards rests to the side. The lighting is soft and warm — flattering, confessional. B-Cam holds a medium close-up on Mika; C-Cam catches a detail shot of Jalen's ringed fingers drumming nervously on the table.

**Mood:** Nervous excitement. The energy of two people who know they're about to say things they shouldn't.

**Storyboard Frame:** Two figures facing each other, table between them, white void around them. Negative space emphasizes intimacy. The bottle is the visual anchor at center-frame.

---

## Scene 2: The First Drink — "Nah, I Can't Say That Out Loud"

**Visual Description:**
B-Cam tight on Jalen's face as he reads a card aloud: *"What's the most unhinged thing in your search history?"* Cut to Mika's face — eyes widen, mouth opens, she immediately reaches for the bottle. C-Cam catches the pour in a low-angle detail shot: tequila catching the warm light as it fills the glass. She throws it back. Jalen erupts laughing. A-Cam holds the wide two-shot as they both dissolve into laughter. Text overlay appears in lowercase white sans-serif with a subtle shake: *"that bad??"*

**Mood:** The ice breaks. The audience now understands the stakes — these questions are REAL.

**Storyboard Frame:** Split-frame composition — left panel: card being read (C-Cam detail); center panel: Mika's "oh no" face (B-Cam); right panel: the pour and shot (C-Cam slow-mo insert).

---

## Scene 3: The Vulnerable Moment — "I've Never Actually Said This Before"

**Visual Description:**
B-Cam slow push-in on Jalen. The music bed has dropped to silence. He's just read the card: *"What's the worst thing you've done to someone who loved you?"* He pauses. Looks down at his rings. The shot holds — 4 full seconds of silence. C-Cam catches his hands going still. He looks up at Mika and says something real — something the audience won't expect. Mika's reaction (B-Cam quick cut) is genuine: she reaches across the table and touches his hand. A-Cam holds the wide to show the full body language shift — they've leaned in, the distance between them has halved.

**Mood:** This is the moment the video earns its emotional core. Raw. Unscripted. The reason people share CUT videos. [[H02:holding reaction shots 3+ seconds before cutting increases comment engagement by 20-30%|The long hold is the edit choice that makes this scene]].

**Storyboard Frame:** Single frame — B-Cam close-up of Jalen's face, eyes slightly glassy, mouth half-open mid-sentence. Shallow depth of field. The background is pure soft white. Nothing else in frame. Just a human being being honest.

---

## Scene 4: The Chaos Closer — "Wait, You're Actually Calling?!"

**Visual Description:**
Energy spike. Mika draws a card: *"Call your most recent ex and tell them one honest thing."* Jalen's jaw drops (B-Cam). Mika laughs, shakes her head, then — to everyone's surprise — pulls out her phone. C-Cam captures the phone screen (blurred for privacy) as it rings. A-Cam holds the wide as both of them lean over the phone, Jalen covering his mouth. The call connects. Mika says one sentence. Hangs up. They both SCREAM. The energy is pure chaos. Quick cut to both of them standing, hugging, laughing. Final wide shot: they're exchanging Instagrams. Jalen says to camera: *"Yo, this was actually healing?"* Mika: *"Therapy could never."* Smash cut to black. CUT logo. End card.

**Mood:** Catharsis through chaos. The audience leaves on a high — emotionally satisfied AND entertained.

**Storyboard Frame:** Dynamic split — left: the phone ringing (C-Cam detail); right: both faces in frame, mouths open, pure shock (A-Cam wide). Energy lines. Movement. The visual opposite of Scene 3's stillness.

- **Sobriety option always available.** If a participant doesn't drink, substitute with a dare, a hot sauce shot, or a "call someone and confess" penalty. Never pressure alcohol consumption.
- **Informed consent is sacred.** Participants review all possible question topics (not exact questions) before signing. They can veto any category. They can stop filming at any time. They review their footage before publish and can request removal of any moment.

### Act-by-Act Pacing Table

| **Act** | **Timecode** | **Duration** | **Content** | **Energy Level** | **Editing Style** |

| **Act VII — Outro & Tag** | 9:00–9:45 | 45 sec | Participants rate each other, exchange Instagrams, final one-liner; CTA — "Subscribe and comment which question YOU would've drunk on" | 😄 (7/10) — warm, fun | Upbeat music returns; end card with subscribe animation; social handles pop up; tease next episode |

### Editing Principles

- **First 3 seconds are everything.** Open on the most shocking/funny moment — no logos, no intros, no preamble. [[H03:test cold open with text-only question card vs. mid-reaction clip for higher 3-second retention|Hook format testing is critical.]]
- **Jump cuts > smooth transitions.** Gen Z editing language is rhythmic, punchy, and unapologetic about discontinuity. Every cut should either advance the joke, heighten the tension, or reveal a reaction.
- **Text overlays are dialogue, not decoration.** Use them to: (a) emphasize punchlines, (b) add internal monologue commentary ("the way she paused 💀"), (c) surface the question being asked so viewers scrolling with sound off still engage.
- **Sound design is a character.** Vinyl scratch on awkward moments. Bass drop on bombshells. Gentle piano on vulnerable beats. TikTok-native SFX (iPhone notification, "oh no" sound) used sparingly but strategically.
- **Pacing rule of thumb:** No single shot holds longer than 8 seconds in Acts III–V. Acts I and VI are exceptions — silence and stillness earn their keep there.
- **Music beds:** Lo-fi/chill-hop for warm-up; trending instrumental (cleared) for escalation; strip music entirely for the vulnerability beat; upbeat/fun for outro. All tracks must be YouTube Content ID-safe.
- **Vertical-first clips:** Editor and Social Media Clipper co-identify the 3–5 strongest 15–60 second moments during assembly. These are cut simultaneously with the long-form edit — not as an afterthought. Vertical clips should feel native to TikTok (fast captions, zooms, trending audio layering).
- **Color grade:** Warm, slightly desaturated, skin-tone-flattering. Not overly stylized — CUT's look is "real people in a clean space," not cinematic drama.
- **Runtime target:** 7:30–9:45 final cut. Under 7 minutes feels thin; over 10 loses Gen Z attention curves.

## Key Risks & Contingencies

| **Risk** | **Impact** | **Mitigation** |
|---|---|---|
| **Talent no-show or last-minute cancel** | High — delays entire shoot day; wastes crew call | Cast 3 pairs minimum for each shoot day; maintain a "hot list" of 5 backup participants who can arrive within 90 minutes; confirm attendance 48hr, 24hr, and 2hr before call time |
| **Participant freezes or gives flat answers** | Medium — unusable footage; boring content | Producer/segment producer warms talent up 20 min before cameras roll with casual conversation; question cards are ordered easy→hard to build comfort; producer feeds follow-up prompts via earpiece or hand signals to off-camera interviewer |
| **Participant becomes genuinely distressed or triggered** | High — ethical/legal liability; bad optics; talent harm | Consent coordinator briefs all participants on "tap out" protocol before shoot; any participant can skip any question without explanation; on-set PA trained in de-escalation; therapist referral card provided in departure packet; footage of distress is NEVER used without explicit post-shoot written consent |
| **Alcohol-related issues (over-intoxication, underage)** | Critical — legal, brand, platform risk | Verify government-issued ID for all participants (21+ only for alcohol shoots); shots are 0.5 oz pours max; water and snacks available throughout; producer monitors intake and caps at 4 shots per person; non-alcoholic alternative always available (and normalized — "drink" can mean the NA option) |
| **Audio bleed or technical failure** | Medium — unusable takes; costly reshoots | Dual-system audio (lav + boom); backup recorder running independently; sound mixer monitors live and calls "cut" on technical issues immediately; timecode sync verified at top of each act |
| **Controversial or platform-violating content** | High — demonetization, age-restriction, or removal on YouTube/TikTok | Producer reviews question cards pre-shoot for platform policy compliance; editor flags any content touching YouTube's "sensitive topics" guidelines; legal reviews final cut before publish; two versions cut if needed (full + "clean") |
| **Low on-camera chemistry between participants** | Medium — content feels forced; poor retention | Cast for contrast (e.g., introvert + extrovert, different backgrounds); producer can pivot question order or introduce a "wild card" question mid-shoot to shake energy; worst case, footage becomes B-content and isn't published as hero video |
| **Post-production bottleneck / missed publish window** | Medium — loses trending relevance; disrupts content calendar | Editor begins rough assembly same day as shoot using proxy files; 48-hour rough cut turnaround is contractual; social clips are cut and ready within 24 hours to capitalize on freshness |
| **Negative audience reception / backlash** | Medium — brand damage; comment section toxicity | Community manager monitors comments for first 72 hours post-publish; pre-written response templates for common concerns; if significant backlash, prepared holding statement and option to unlist (not delete) video while assessing |

## Scene 1: The Setup — "So… This Is Gonna Get Messy"

**Visual Description:**
Wide establishing shot of two strangers seated across from each other at a minimalist white table. The set is CUT's signature clean backdrop — matte white seamless paper — with a single bottle of Casamigos tequila centered between them, flanked by two shot glasses. Soft warm key light from camera-left wraps their faces; a subtle hair light separates them from the background. A stack of question cards sits face-down on the table. The framing is symmetrical — almost confrontational — emphasizing that these two people have never met. Both participants fidget slightly. JALEN (22) adjusts his silver rings. MIKA (21) tucks her micro bangs behind her ear, then untucks them. The energy is nervous electricity.

**Camera Directions:**
- **A-Cam:** Locked-off wide master, both in frame, symmetrical composition. This is the "album cover" shot — the thumbnail lives here.
- **B-Cam:** Slow push-in on MIKA's face as she scans JALEN. Capture the micro-assessment.
- **C-Cam:** Overhead insert of the table — bottle, glasses, cards. Clean, almost ASMR-level detail.

**Audio/Music:**
Ambient room tone for 2 beats of silence. Then a soft lo-fi beat fades in (think Idealism or Kupla — warm, slightly melancholy, unhurried). The quiet says: *something is about to happen.*

**Text Overlays:**
- Lower third: **"JALEN, 22"** and **"MIKA, 21"** — clean sans-serif, CUT house style.
- Subtitle pop: *"they've never met before."*

**Duration:** ~45 seconds (0:15–1:00 in final edit, following the cold open hook)

## Scene 2: The Warm-Up — "Okay Wait, That's Actually Embarrassing"

**Visual Description:**
Energy shifts from nervous to playful. MIKA reads the first card aloud: *"What's your daily screen time?"* JALEN winces, pulls out his phone, and turns the screen toward camera — C-Cam catches the close-up: **11 hours 47 minutes.** MIKA gasps theatrically. She shows hers: **9 hours 12 minutes.** They both laugh — the ice cracks. Next card: *"Who was the last person you Instagram-stalked?"* JALEN immediately reaches for the tequila bottle instead of answering. MIKA screams. He pours a careful shot, clinks the glass against nothing, and drinks. MIKA leans forward: *"It was an EX, wasn't it."* JALEN says nothing. His face says everything.

**Camera Directions:**
- **A-Cam:** Holds the wide two-shot; captures the full-body language shift as they warm up.
- **B-Cam:** Rapid alternation — tight on whoever is reacting (not speaking). The reaction is the content.
- **C-Cam:** Handheld inserts — phone screen reveal (to be replaced with motion graphic in post), the pour, the shot glass lift, JALEN's hand covering his face.

**Audio/Music:**
Lo-fi beat continues but with slightly more energy (add a subtle hi-hat pattern). SFX: iPhone "lock screen" sound when phones are shown; a comedic vinyl scratch when JALEN grabs the bottle; a soft crowd "ooooh" (subtle, not overproduced).

**Text Overlays:**
- Question card appears as full-screen graphic between shots: **"What's your screen time? 📱"**
- Pop-up text over JALEN reaching for the bottle: **"DRINK. 🥃"**
- Commentary text: **"the silence is TELLING 💀"**

**Duration:** ~90 seconds (1:00–2:30)

## Scene 3: The Escalation — "You Did NOT Just Say That"

**Visual Description:**
The table now shows evidence of play — a few used shot glasses, cards scattered. The body language has completely transformed: both participants are leaning in, elbows on the table, grinning with the reckless energy of people who've decided to stop being careful. MIKA draws a card and reads it silently first — her eyes go wide. She looks at JALEN. Looks at the camera. Looks back at the card. *"Have you ever… emotionally manipulated someone and KNOWN you were doing it?"* Long pause. JALEN exhales. He doesn't reach for the bottle. *"Yeah. I have."* Beat. *"I love-bombed someone because I was bored and lonely and I knew exactly what I was doing."* MIKA's jaw drops — not performatively, genuinely. She whispers: *"That's the most honest thing anyone's ever said on one of these."* Then JALEN flips it: *"Your turn. Read your last DM slide attempt. Out loud."* MIKA IMMEDIATELY grabs the bottle. The room erupts.

**Camera Directions:**
- **A-Cam:** Holds wide but operator subtly tightens framing (slow zoom) during JALEN's confession — the walls close in.
- **B-Cam:** Locked on JALEN's face during confession — single unbroken take, no cuts. This is the emotional anchor. When the energy flips to comedy (MIKA grabbing the bottle), B-Cam whip-pans to her.
- **C-Cam:** During confession: slow push-in on MIKA's listening face. During comedy beat: handheld chaos — bottle grab, pour, her hiding behind the card.
- **60fps on C-Cam** for the bottle grab — this is the slow-mo money shot for TikTok clips.

**Audio/Music:**
Music drops OUT completely during JALEN's confession. Room tone only. The absence of music is the loudest choice. When MIKA grabs the bottle, a bass-drop SFX hits and the beat returns at higher energy. This is the audio "roller coaster" — the contrast between silence and sound makes both moments hit harder.

**Text Overlays:**
- Question card: **"Have you ever emotionally manipulated someone? 🎭"**
- During JALEN's pause (before he answers): a ticking clock graphic or subtle **"⏳"** — building tension.
- After his confession: **"…respectfully, therapy is working 🫡"**
- When MIKA grabs the bottle: **"DRINK DRINK DRINK"** in rapid-fire stacked text.

**Duration:** ~150 seconds (2:30–5:00, representing the heart of Act IV into Act V)

---

## Scene 4: The Vulnerable Close — "I Didn't Expect to Feel Things"

**Visual Description:**
The energy has settled. The bottle is noticeably lower. Both participants are slightly flushed, looser in their chairs, but there's a new quality in the room — intimacy. Two strangers who now know real things about each other. The producer's voice comes from off-camera (first time we hear it): *"Last question. This one's different."* MIKA picks up the final card. It's printed on a different color — gold instead of white. She reads: *"What are you most afraid people will find out about you?"* Silence. MIKA goes first: *"That I'm not actually over it. Any of it. I perform 'healed' online but I still cry in my car like three times a week."* Her eyes are wet. JALEN reaches across the table and puts his hand on hers — a gesture neither of them expected. He answers: *"I'm afraid people will find out I'm lonely. Like, actually lonely. I have a million friends and I'm the loneliest person I know."* Neither of them drinks. The bottle sits untouched. The game is over; something real replaced it.

**Camera Directions:**
- **A-Cam:** Wide master — DO NOT CUT AWAY. This scene lives in the unbroken two-shot. The space between them is the story. Operator may execute one glacially slow push-in over the full 90 seconds.
- **B-Cam:** Soft singles, but the editor should use them sparingly — only to catch a tear, a swallow, the hand reach. The wide shot is king.
- **C-Cam:** One insert only — the untouched bottle and two full shot glasses. No one drank. That image IS the thesis of the video.

**Audio/Music:**
Complete silence for MIKA's answer. As JALEN responds, a single piano note fades in — not a melody, just a tone. A resonance. After both have spoken, a gentle, sparse piano piece builds underneath (think Ólafur Arnalds or Nils Frahm — permission/license secured in pre-production). The music doesn't manipulate — it holds space.

**Text Overlays:**
- Minimal. The question card appears once: **"What are you most afraid people will find out about you? 💛"**
- No commentary text. No jokes. No emojis. The restraint IS the editorial choice.
- After a beat of silence post-answers, a simple white-on-black card: **"Neither of them drank."**

**Transition to Outro:**

## Character Archetypes (Mix & Match for Pairings)

| **Archetype** | **Description** | **Energy** | **Example Reference** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The Overthinker** | Journals daily, has a Notes app full of unsent texts, says "I've been doing a lot of inner work" | Warm, slightly nervous, accidentally profound | Jalen in this script |
| **The Chaos Agent** | Says the quiet part loud, zero filter, somehow still lovable | High-energy, reactive, meme-able | The friend who gets you kicked out of brunch |
| **The Old Soul** | 22 going on 45, uses phrases like "in this economy?" unironically | Dry humor, grounded, surprising depth | Your friend who has a retirement plan already |
| **The Softie** | Cries at commercials, carries emotional weight gracefully, disarming vulnerability | Gentle, empathetic, scene-stealing in quiet moments | Mika in this script |
| **The Performer** | Theater kid energy, every answer is a bit, but the mask slips in the deep rounds | Big reactions, quotable, unexpectedly raw when pressed | The one who goes viral |
| **The Wildcard** | Unreadable at first — could go any direction — keeps everyone guessing | Unpredictable, magnetic, high rewatch value | The person comments argue about |

---

## Casting Priorities

- **Priority 1:** Emotional range. They need to go from "ELEVEN HOURS?!" to "my dad missed my graduation" within the same shoot. If they can only do one register, pass.
- **Priority 2:** Conversational chemistry in the first 30 seconds of meeting. We're not scripting banter — we're casting for people who generate it naturally.
- **Priority 3:** Comfort with silence. The deep rounds require people who won't fill every pause with nervous laughter. [[H07:casting for comfort with silence increases emotional payoff in final act|Silence is content.]]
- **Priority 4:** Relatability over polish. We want the person your audience DMs to their group chat, not the person who looks like they're auditioning for a Netflix show.

---

## Casting Process

| **Stage** | **Format** | **Duration** | **What We're Evaluating** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Open Call / Submission** | Self-tape: answer 3 personal questions on camera (alone, phone quality fine) | 60–90 sec | Authenticity, camera comfort, voice |
| **2. Chemistry Read** | Zoom or in-person: paired with a stranger, given 5 min of unstructured conversation | 5 min | Listening skills, curiosity, natural humor |
| **3. Vulnerability Screen** | 1-on-1 with producer: asked one "deep cut" level question | 3–5 min | Emotional access, boundary awareness, willingness |
| **4. Final Pairing** | Producer + creative director select pairings based on contrast and complementary energy | N/A | Maximum dynamic range per pair |

---

## Self-Tape Prompt Questions (Stage 1)

1. "What's something you're weirdly proud of that nobody else would be impressed by?"
2. "Describe the last time you were embarrassed in public."
3. "If you could say one honest thing to your younger self, what would it be?"

---

## Red Flags in Casting

| **Red Flag** | **Why It's a Problem** |
|---|---|
| Only performs for camera, never listens | Kills chemistry; the other person becomes a prop |
| Can't access vulnerability even slightly | Deep rounds will fall flat; audience disengages |
| Tries too hard to be "the funny one" | Reads as inauthentic; Gen Z detects this instantly |
| Uncomfortable with any silence | Will steamroll emotional beats that need space |
| Gives rehearsed or "influencer" answers | Breaks the fourth wall of authenticity CUT depends on |
| Can't hold eye contact with their partner | Signals discomfort that translates as disinterest on screen |

---

## Pairing Philosophy

The best pairings are **asymmetric but balanced**:

- One person who leads with humor + one who leads with sincerity
- One person who drinks freely + one who answers everything
- One person who's loud in the early rounds + one who's quiet but devastating in the deep rounds

**The goal:** By the final question, the audience should be genuinely surprised by who made them cry.
