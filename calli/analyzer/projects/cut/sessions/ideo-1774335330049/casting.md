## Demographics & Energy

| **Parameter** | **Target** |
|---|---|
| **Age Range** | 19–25 (born 1999–2005) |
| **Personality Types** | Anxious-overthinkers, chaotic extroverts, "I'm healed" optimists, soft-spoken deep-feelers, unhinged comedians |
| **Diversity** | Non-negotiable multi-axis: race, gender identity, body type, neurodivergence, socioeconomic background, regional accent. No "diversity by default = two light-skinned people from LA" |
| **Energy Signature** | The room should feel like a group chat that accidentally got too real at 1 AM. Equal parts nervous laughter and genuine revelation. |

---

## Character Archetypes Needed

- **Hero** — The emotionally intelligent one who goes first, sets the vulnerability ceiling. Probably journals. Will say "honestly?" before every answer and mean it.
- **Villain** — Not actually villainous — just unapologetically blunt. Says what everyone's thinking. Will choose truth every time because "I'm not a coward."
- **Underdog** — Quiet at first, then drops the most devastating answer of the episode. Comment section will rally around them. [[H01:test whether the underdog's breakout moment drives the highest retention spike|Their reveal moment is the video's emotional climax.]]
- **Overconfident** — Thinks they can handle every question. Cannot. The first to reach for the bottle when the question hits too close. Their crumble is the comedic engine.
- **Wildcard** — Genuinely unpredictable. Might answer a question with a question. Might drink when the question is easy and answer when it's brutal. Keeps the edit alive.
- **Super Fun** — Pure serotonin. Reacts big, laughs loud, hypes everyone else up. The connective tissue of the group. Not the main character — the best friend energy that makes the main characters shine.

---

## What We Value (In Order)

1. **Comfort on camera under pressure** — Can they hold a pause? Can they sit in silence after a hard question without performing discomfort? We need people who are *present*, not performing.
2. **Experience with vulnerability formats** — Have they done a podcast, a confessional TikTok series, a storytelling open mic? We want people who understand the contract of emotional exchange. [[H02:test whether casting from vulnerability-native creators vs. general influencers produces higher average view duration|Vulnerability-native creators may outperform general influencers on retention.]]
3. **Social native fluency** — They understand the grammar of short-form content intuitively. They know what a "moment" is. They won't over-explain a joke or under-sell a confession.
4. **Group chemistry instinct** — Can they read a room, volley energy, make space for someone else's moment? This is ensemble work, not a solo reel.

---

## Red Flags to Avoid

- ❌ **Follower count as a casting metric** — A person with 1,200 followers who cries on camera authentically will outperform a 500K creator who performs vulnerability. Every time.
- ❌ **Traditional acting credits** — We're not looking for trained emotional beats. We need real reactions. Actors default to "scene work" under pressure; we need people who default to *themselves*.
- ❌ **Brand deal history / management teams** — If their manager sends a "pre-approved topic list," they're wrong for this. Truth or Drink requires surrender of editorial control.
- ❌ **"Content house" alumni** — The energy is too produced, too aware of camera placement. We need people who forget the camera is there.

---

## Gender Mix & Regional Voice

| **Gender Identity** | **Target Mix** |
|---|---|
| Women (cis & trans) | 2–3 |
| Men (cis & trans) | 2–3 |
| Non-binary / genderqueer | 1–2 |

**Regional Voice:**
- At least **one person from the South or Midwest** — not everyone should sound like coastal internet
- At least **one immigrant or first-gen American perspective** — code-switching is a lived experience that produces incredible Truth or Drink answers
- At least **one person from a small town or rural background** — the cultural friction is content gold
- **No more than two people from LA or NYC** — oversaturation kills specificity

---

## Casting North Star

> **We are casting for the moment *after* the laugh — the two-second pause where someone's eyes change and the audience realizes this person just told the truth for the first time out loud.** The ideal cast is six strangers who each carry a secret they've only told one person, who find it easier to be honest with someone they'll never see again, and whose chemistry makes the audience feel like they're eavesdropping on something they shouldn't hear. [[H03:test whether stranger pairings outperform friend pairings on share rate for Gen Z audiences|Stranger pairings may drive higher share rates because the vulnerability feels riskier and therefore more authentic.]]

## ACT I — COLD OPEN HOOK (0:00–0:15)

**[WIDE SHOT: Two strangers — JALEN (22, oversized vintage tee, silver rings) and MIKA (21, baby tee, micro bangs) — sit across from each other at a minimalist white table. Bottle of Casamigos and two shot glasses between them. Stark white background. No music. Just ambient room tone.]**

**JALEN:**
*(looking at the first card, then slowly looking up at Mika)*
Bro... they're not playing.

**MIKA:**
*(laughing nervously, pulling the bottle closer)*
How strong is this?

**[SMASH CUT TO: Title card — "TRUTH OR DRINK: GEN Z EDITION" — bold sans-serif, slight glitch effect. One beat of bass. Cut back.]**

---

## ACT II — THE WARM-UP: SURFACE TRUTHS (0:15–2:30)

**[The energy is light. These questions are designed to build comfort, establish chemistry, and give the audience a reason to like both people before the floor drops out.]**

**CARD 1 — MIKA reads:**
"What's the most unhinged thing in your screen time report?"

**JALEN:**
*(immediately)* Seven hours of TikTok on a Tuesday. And I was *at work*.

**MIKA:**
*(dying)* At WORK?

**JALEN:**
I work from home! It's giving... barely employed.

---

**CARD 2 — JALEN reads:**
"What's a lie you've told in the last 48 hours?"

**MIKA:**
*(thinking, then smiling guiltily)*
I told my mom I was at the library. I was at this guy's house.

**JALEN:**
The LIBRARY? That's so specific. That's how you know it's a lie.

**[Both laugh. The chemistry is landing. Audience is in.]**

---

**CARD 3 — MIKA reads:**
"What's something you pretend to like because the internet told you to?"

**JALEN:**
*(long pause)*
...Matcha.

**MIKA:**
NO.

**JALEN:**
It tastes like lawn. I'm sorry. I said it.

**MIKA:**
You are so brave and so wrong.

---

## ACT III — THE TURN: MEDIUM TRUTHS (2:30–5:30)

**[The questions escalate. The laughs get shorter. The pauses get longer. This is where the edit starts to breathe — we hold on faces, let the silence work.]**

**CARD 4 — JALEN reads:**
"What's something you've never told your closest friend?"

**[Beat. MIKA's smile fades. She looks at the bottle. Looks at the card. Looks at Jalen.]**

**MIKA:**
*(quietly)*
That I was jealous of her for like... two years. Not in a cute way. In a way that made me a worse friend.

**JALEN:**
*(softly)*
Did it pass?

**MIKA:**
Yeah. Therapy. *(small laugh)* Expensive but effective.

---

**CARD 5 — MIKA reads:**
"When's the last time you cried, and why?"

**JALEN:**
*(reaches for the bottle, then stops. Puts it down.)*
Last week. My dad called me for the first time in like... four months. And it was just to ask about my sister.

**[Silence. MIKA doesn't fill it. This is the moment. The camera holds on JALEN's face for three full seconds.]**

**JALEN:**
*(recovering, half-smiling)*
Anyway. Next card.

**MIKA:**
*(gently)*
You good?

**JALEN:**
Yeah. That just... came out different than I expected.

---

**CARD 6 — JALEN reads:**
"Have you ever been someone's second choice and known it?"

**MIKA:**
*(immediately reaches for the bottle, pours, drinks)*

**JALEN:**
Wow.

**MIKA:**
Some things you drink to. That's a drink question.

**JALEN:**
I respect it.

**[This moment is critical — it establishes that drinking is NOT a cop-out. It's a boundary. It's a choice. The audience respects the refusal as much as the confession.]**

---

## ACT IV — THE DEEP END: HARD TRUTHS (5:30–8:30)

**[The table feels different now. They're leaning in. The bottle is half empty. The energy is quiet, conspiratorial, almost sacred.]**

**CARD 7 — MIKA reads:**
"What's the version of yourself you show the internet, and what's the version you are when you're alone?"

**JALEN:**
*(long exhale)*
Online I'm... funny. I'm the guy who's always good. Always got a take, always got energy. Alone I'm... really quiet. I don't talk to anyone for days sometimes. And I don't think that's healthy but I also don't know how to stop.

**MIKA:**
*(nodding slowly)*
That's the most relatable thing anyone's ever said to me.

---

**CARD 8 — JALEN reads:**
"What are you most afraid your generation will be remembered for?"

**MIKA:**
Being the most informed generation that still couldn't fix anything. Like... we *know* everything is broken. We have the data. We have the language. And I'm scared we'll just... post about it and call it activism and nothing will actually change.

**[Beat.]**

**JALEN:**
That's heavy.

**MIKA:**
It keeps me up.

---

**CARD 9 — MIKA reads:**
"Is there someone you owe an apology to that you'll probably never give?"

**[JALEN looks at the bottle. Looks at the camera. Looks at MIKA.]**

**JALEN:**
Yeah. My little brother. I was supposed to be the example and I just... left. I went to college and I didn't look back enough. He needed me and I was busy becoming a person and I forgot he was still becoming one too.

**[MIKA's eyes are wet. She doesn't wipe them. The camera catches it.]**

**MIKA:**
You should call him after this.

**JALEN:**
*(nodding)*
Yeah. I think I will.

---

## ACT V — THE FINAL CARD & CLOSE (8:30–9:45)

**[WIDE SHOT. Both of them are looser now — emotionally open, slightly buzzed, fully connected.]**

**JALEN:**
Last one. You read it.

**MIKA:**
*(picks up the card, reads it silently first, then laughs — a real laugh, not a content laugh)*

"If you could send one anonymous text to anyone in your life right now, what would it say?"

**JALEN:**
You first.

**MIKA:**
*(looking directly into camera)*
"I forgive you. But I'm not coming back."

**[Three-second hold.]**

**JALEN:**
*(looking down, then up)*
"I'm proud of who I'm becoming. I just wish you could see it."

**[Silence. Then MIKA reaches across the table and squeezes JALEN's hand. He squeezes back.]**

**MIKA:**
This was really intense.

**JALEN:**
*(laughing, wiping his eye)*
I came here thinking I wouldn't say anything real. And then I said everything.

## POST-SCRIPT: PRODUCTION NOTES

| **Element** | **Direction** |
|---|---|
| **Camera** | Two-camera setup minimum. A-cam wide, B-cam tight on faces. The tight shots are where the video lives. |
| **Audio** | Lav mics on both. Room tone is part of the aesthetic — don't kill it in post. Silence is a feature, not a bug. |
| **Music** | Minimal. Lo-fi ambient pads ONLY during transitions. Never under confessions. The audience needs to hear the breath before the answer. |
| **Edit Rhythm** | ACT I cuts fast (comedy pacing). ACT III slows down. ACT IV barely cuts at all. Let the discomfort sit. |
| **Color** | Slightly desaturated, warm. Not clinical. The white background should feel like a confessional, not a doctor's office. |
| **Run Time** | Target **9:00–10:00** for YouTube. Cut a **0:45 vertical trailer** for TikTok/Reels that uses CARD 5 and CARD 9 as the hooks. |
| **Thumbnail** | Split frame: JALEN reaching for the bottle on one side, MIKA's face mid-realization on the other. Text: **"I've never told anyone this."** |
