# Truth or Drink: Gen Z Edition — 4-Scene Storyboard

---

## Scene 1: The Setup — "So… This Is Gonna Get Messy"

**Visual Description:**
Wide establishing shot of two strangers seated across from each other at a minimalist white table. The set is CUT's signature clean backdrop — matte white seamless paper — with soft, diffused overhead lighting and a subtle warm key light at 45° to flatter skin tones. A bottle of tequila and two shot glasses sit between them like a dare. The camera slowly pushes in from a wide two-shot to a medium, building quiet tension. Mood is bright but charged with nervous anticipation.

**On-Screen Action & Key Dialogue:**
The two participants introduce themselves awkwardly — names, ages, zodiac signs (a must for Gen Z). One says, *"I already know I'm gonna regret this,"* and the other laughs nervously, reaching for the bottle preemptively. A title card smash-cuts in: **TRUTH OR DRINK: STRANGERS EDITION** with a bold, sans-serif typeface and a subtle liquid animation.

**Mood:** 😬 Nervous excitement — the calm before the chaos.

---

## Scene 2: The Warm-Up Round — "Wait, That's Actually Deep"

**Visual Description:**
Camera alternates between tight over-the-shoulder shots and individual close-ups (50mm lens, shallow depth of field) to capture micro-expressions. Lighting remains warm and inviting. [[H01:test warm vs cool lighting on perceived vulnerability|The warm lighting palette is designed to make participants feel safer opening up sooner]]. A small card deck sits on the table; participants draw questions one at a time. The edit rhythm here is snappy — quick cuts on reactions, held beats on silences.

**On-Screen Action & Key Dialogue:**
Questions escalate from playful (*"What's your body count… of streaming subscriptions you mooch off someone else?"*) to genuinely probing (*"What's a lie you tell yourself to get through the day?"*). One participant pauses, visibly weighing the shot glass against honesty, then says, *"Okay fine — I pretend I have a five-year plan. I literally do not."* The other nods hard in recognition. [[H02:test relatable confession moments vs shocking confessions for shareability|This beat of quiet relatability is hypothesized to outperform shock-value moments in Gen Z shares]].

**Mood:** 😂➡️🥺 Laughter dissolving into unexpected sincerity — the tonal pivot that hooks viewers.

---

## Scene 3: The Escalation — "Pour Me a Double"

**Visual Description:**
The energy shifts. Camera work gets slightly handheld, introducing a documentary-feel wobble to mirror the loosening atmosphere. Insert shots punctuate the scene: tequila pouring in slow motion (120fps), a grimace after a shot, fingers tapping nervously on the table. The lighting hasn't changed, but the *feeling* has — participants are leaning in closer, the physical space between them shrinking. A second camera captures a wide profile two-shot to emphasize their body language mirroring.

**On-Screen Action & Key Dialogue:**
The questions hit harder: *"Have you ever been in love with someone who didn't love you back?"* One participant immediately grabs the bottle — *"Nope. Drinking. Next."* — but then stops mid-pour and quietly admits, *"Yeah. And I think they knew."* The other responds with genuine empathy, not performance. This is the **clip moment** — the raw, unscripted beat where avoidance collapses into truth. [[H03:test whether the "almost drank but chose truth" reversal drives higher completion rates|The dramatic reversal from avoidance to vulnerability is the core clip hypothesis]].

**Mood:** 💔🥃 Vulnerable and electric — the emotional peak of the video.

---

## Scene 4: The Cool Down — "Okay, We're Definitely Friends Now"

**Visual Description:**
Return to the wide two-shot from Scene 1, but the frame feels entirely different now — the table is cluttered with used shot glasses, crumpled question cards, and the half-empty bottle. Lighting is unchanged but participants are visibly softer — slouched, smiling, guards fully down. The camera holds steady, giving the moment room to breathe. A slow push-in mirrors Scene 1's opening move, creating a visual bookend.

**On-Screen Action & Key Dialogue:**
The final question is light: *"Would you hang out with this person again?"* Both laugh and say yes without hesitation. One says, *"You literally know more about me than my therapist now."* They cheers with their last shot. The screen cuts to black with the CUT logo and a text prompt: **"Send this to someone you'd never play this with."** — a CTA engineered for DM sharing.

**Mood:** 🫶☀️ Warm, earned intimacy — the emotional payoff that makes viewers want to rewatch and share.

## Director's Note

**Pacing:** The video follows a classic emotional arc — tension (Scene 1), playfulness (Scene 2), vulnerability peak (Scene 3), resolution (Scene 4). Scenes 1 and 4 should be the shortest (~30–45 sec each). Scene 2 runs ~1.5–2 min. Scene 3 is the longest and most elastic (~2–3 min), because this is where authentic moments live or die — **do not rush the silences**. Total runtime target: **6–7 minutes**.

**The Clip:** Scene 3 — specifically the "almost drank but chose truth" reversal — is the hero clip for TikTok/Reels/Shorts. Cut it as a 45–60 second vertical excerpt with a cold open on the question, the pour, the pause, and the confession. No context needed; the emotion is self-contained.

**Key Production Decisions to Test:**
- [[H01:test warm vs cool lighting on perceived vulnerability|Warm lighting hypothesis]] — run A/B with a cool-toned episode to measure comment sentiment.
- [[H02:test relatable confession moments vs shocking confessions for shareability|Relatability vs. shock hypothesis]] — track share-to-view ratio across episode variants.
- [[H03:test whether the "almost drank but chose truth" reversal drives higher completion rates|Reversal moment hypothesis]] — measure retention curve drop-off at the clip beat across multiple episodes.
