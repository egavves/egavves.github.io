## II. CHARACTER ARCHETYPES NEEDED

We don't cast "characters" — we cast **real people who naturally occupy archetypal space.** The edit will find the story, but we need the raw signal:

| Archetype | Who Fills It | What We're Looking For in Casting Tape |
|---|---|---|
| **Hero** | JORDAN | Takes accountability mid-game. Says "yeah, that was on me" without being prompted. Audience roots for their growth. |
| **Villain** | No one — *and everyone, briefly.* | The format itself creates micro-villain moments (dodging a question, a revealed lie). We **never** pre-cast a villain. That's a red flag for manufactured content. |
| **Underdog** | DEV | Entered the situation with the most to lose emotionally (watching a partner's ex in real time). Audience protective instinct kicks in. |
| **Overconfident** | SAM (early game) | Starts the monitor room saying "I'm not worried at all" — the edit lives for the moment that confidence cracks. |
| **Wildcard** | MAYA | Unpredictable honesty. She might drink when you expect her to answer — or answer when everyone expects her to drink. Keeps the algorithm guessing. |
| **Super Fun** | DEV + SAM dynamic | Their commentary must feel like a group chat come to life. If their reactions aren't GIF-able, we miscast. |

## III. WHAT WE VALUE (RANKED)

### 1) 🎯 Comfort on Camera Under Pressure
This is non-negotiable and **un-coachable at speed.** In the casting tape, we ask: *"Tell me something you've never said out loud."* We're not listening to the answer. We're watching:
- Do they freeze or flow?
- Do their eyes go to the lens or stay human?
- Does their body language close or open?

**Test:** 60-second hot seat. Three rapid personal questions. No prep.

### 2) 🫣 Experience with Vulnerability Formats
Has this person ever been emotionally exposed in a public or semi-public context — podcast, support group facilitation, open mic storytelling, therapy TikTok, even a brutally honest Instagram caption with real engagement? We want **scar tissue, not fresh wounds.** People who have processed their story, not people about to unravel on our set.

**Screening question:** *"What's the most vulnerable thing you've done in front of strangers, and how did it feel after?"*

### 3) 📱 Social Native Fluency
They don't need followers. They need to **understand the rhythm of content.** They know what a "moment" feels like because they've consumed thousands of them. They instinctively pause before a punchline. They know the difference between a TikTok beat and a YouTube beat.

**Test:** Show them a CUT clip with the sound off. Ask them to narrate what's happening and predict the comment section. Accuracy = fluency.

### 4) 🧪 Group Chemistry Instinct
Can they volley? Do they listen to respond or listen to *connect*? In a 4-person chemistry read, watch for:
- Who asks follow-up questions?
- Who builds on someone else's joke?
- Who makes the quietest person in the room speak?

That person gets cast.

## IV. RED FLAGS TO AVOID

| ❌ Red Flag | Why It Kills the Video |
|---|---|
| **High follower count as primary qualification** | Vanity metric. A 500K creator who's never been unscripted will freeze or perform. We need humans, not brands. |
| **Traditional acting credits (film/TV/theater)** | Trained performers default to "scene work" under pressure. They project instead of reveal. The audience clocks it in 3 seconds and scrolls. |
| **Brand deal history / management representation** | They'll self-censor to protect partnerships. Every answer runs through an internal PR filter. Authenticity dies. |
| **"I'm an open book" self-description** | Almost always means they've rehearsed a curated version of openness. Real vulnerability doesn't announce itself. |
| **Recent breakup (< 6 months)** | Ethical concern + emotional volatility ≠ good content. We want resolved tension, not active trauma. |

## V. GENDER MIX & REGIONAL VOICE

**Gender Mix:**
- This episode's table pair (JORDAN & MAYA): **gender-flexible casting.** The brief works across any combination — M/F, M/M, F/F, NB/F, etc. We cast the *relationship dynamic*, not the gender configuration.
- Monitor room pair (SAM & DEV): Same principle. Chemistry and contrast matter more than representation checkboxes, but we **do not default to all-cis-hetero** without intentional creative justification.
- Across the full season slate: track cumulative gender representation to ensure no identity is tokenized in a single episode and then absent for ten.

**Regional Voice:**
We are actively countering CUT's historical LA/PNW casting gravity.

| Priority Regions | Why |
|---|---|
| **South (Atlanta, Houston, Memphis)** | Underrepresented in vulnerability formats; enormous Gen Z cultural influence; distinct storytelling cadence |
| **Midwest (Chicago, Detroit, Minneapolis)** | Grounded energy, less performative social conditioning, strong community-narrative instinct |
| **Northeast (Philly, BK, Boston)** | Direct communication style that reads as authentic; high comfort with confrontation |
| **Southwest / Border communities** | Bilingual code-switching is inherently compelling on camera; cultural specificity deepens the content |

We will **fly people in** if necessary. Regional voice is not a nice-to-have — it's a texture that separates a 2M-view video from a 10M-view video.

## VI. FULL VIDEO SCRIPT v2.0

### CUT ANALYTICS — "EXES: Truth or Drink"

**Format:** Two exes (**JORDAN**, 24 & **MAYA**, 23) sit across from each other at a small round table. Bottle of whiskey between them. Shot glasses pre-poured. Their current partners (**SAM**, 25 & **DEV**, 24) watch from a hidden monitor room with split-screen cutaways.

**Duration Target:** 5–7 min edited / ~45 min raw shoot

---

### COLD OPEN (0:00–0:25)

*[WIDE SHOT: Empty table. Two chairs. Bottle. Silence.]*

**TITLE CARD:** *They dated for two years. They haven't spoken in eight months.*

*[CUT TO: Monitor room. SAM and DEV sit side by side on a couch, headphones on, watching a live feed.]*

**SAM:** *(to DEV, nervous laugh)* I told Jordan I was fine with this. I am... mostly fine with this.

**DEV:** *(deadpan)* I told Maya if she cries, I'm walking in there.

*[SMASH CUT TO: JORDAN and MAYA sitting down at the table. Awkward beat. Eye contact. Half-smiles.]*

**JORDAN:** ...Hi.

**MAYA:** Hi.

*[TITLE CARD: TRUTH OR DRINK]*

---

### ROUND 1 — THE WARM-UP (0:25–1:30)

*[Card draw. JORDAN reads first.]*

**JORDAN:** *(reading)* "On a scale of 1 to 10, how would you rate our relationship?"

**MAYA:** *(immediately)* You first.

**JORDAN:** That's not how the game—

**MAYA:** You first.

*[MONITOR ROOM — SAM leans forward.]*

**JORDAN:** ...Seven. It was a seven.

**MAYA:** *(beat)* That's generous.

*[DEV in monitor room, under breath: "Damn."]*

**MAYA:** *(draws card, reads)* "What's one thing you faked during our relationship?"

*[JORDAN reaches for the shot glass. Pauses. Puts it back down.]*

**JORDAN:** I faked being okay with how close you were with your coworker. I wasn't okay with it. I just didn't want to be *that* person.

*[MONITOR ROOM — SAM, quietly: "Oh. That's... yeah."]*

**MAYA:** *(nods slowly)* I knew.

---

### ROUND 2 — THE ESCALATION (1:30–3:15)

**JORDAN:** *(reads)* "Did you ever think about getting back together after we broke up?"

*[Maya picks up the shot glass. Holds it. Looks at Jordan. Drinks.]*

*[MONITOR ROOM — DEV stiffens. Says nothing. Camera holds on their face for three full seconds.]*

**MAYA:** *(reads)* "What's something your current partner does better than I did?"

**JORDAN:** *(long exhale)* Sam listens differently. Like... Sam asks me what I actually mean instead of just reacting to what I said.

*[MONITOR ROOM — SAM puts hand over mouth. Eyes wet. Whispers: "I didn't know he noticed that."]*

**MAYA:** *(quiet)* That's fair. I was reactive. I know that now.

---

### ROUND 3 — THE GUT PUNCH (3:15–4:45)

**JORDAN:** *(reads, then stops. Re-reads silently. Looks up.)* I'm not reading this one.

**PRODUCER (O.S.):** You can drink instead.

*[Jordan drinks. Fast. Doesn't explain.]*

*[MONITOR ROOM — SAM: "What was on that card?" DEV: "I don't think we want to know."]*

**MAYA:** *(reads)* "Do you think your ex has changed, or are they the same person you broke up with?"

*[Silence. Five seconds. The longest five seconds in the video.]*

**MAYA:** *(looking directly at Jordan)* I think you're trying. And I think trying is new for you. So... yeah. You've changed. A little.

*[JORDAN's jaw tightens. Nods once. Doesn't speak.]*

*[MONITOR ROOM — DEV, softly: "She still knows him." SAM doesn't respond.]*

---

### ROUND 4 — THE RELEASE (4:45–5:45)

**JORDAN:** Last card. *(reads)* "Is there anything you never apologized for that you should have?"

**MAYA:** *(without hesitation)* Yes. I'm sorry I told your sister about our problems. That wasn't mine to share, and it changed how she looked at you. I've thought about that a lot.

**JORDAN:** *(surprised)* ...Thank you. I— yeah. That one stayed with me.

**MAYA:** I know.

**JORDAN:** *(beat)* I'm sorry I made you feel like you were hard to love. You weren't. I was just bad at it.

*[MONITOR ROOM — SAM wipes eyes. DEV reaches over and squeezes SAM's hand. No words.]*

---

### CLOSE (5:45–6:15)

*[WIDE SHOT: Table. Both lean back. Tension has dissolved into something quieter.]*

**MAYA:** *(small laugh)* That was terrible.

**JORDAN:** *(laughing)* The worst.

**MAYA:** You good?

**JORDAN:** I'm good. You good?

**MAYA:** Yeah.

*[They stand. Brief, genuine hug. Not performative. Not long. Just enough.]*

*[CUT TO: Monitor room door opens. JORDAN walks in. SAM stands. They look at each other.]*

**SAM:** A seven?

**JORDAN:** *(smiling)* You're a ten. Come on.

*[MAYA enters. DEV stands, arms crossed, mock-serious.]*

**DEV:** You *drank* on the "getting back together" question.

**MAYA:** *(laughing)* I panicked!

**DEV:** Mhm.

**MAYA:** *(grabs DEV's face)* You're stuck with me.

**DEV:** *(trying not to smile)* ...Fine.

*[WIDE SHOT: All four, laughing, tension broken. Real.]*

**END CARD:** *Truth or Drink. New episodes every Thursday.*

*[FADE TO BLACK.]*

### POST-SCRIPT: PRODUCTION NOTES FOR CASTING TEAM

- Pair SAM/DEV candidates on a couch with a fake "live feed" (use old footage). Watch their instinct: Do they narrate? React? Go silent? All valid — but we need *complementary* instincts, not matching ones.
