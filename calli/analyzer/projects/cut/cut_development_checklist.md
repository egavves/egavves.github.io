# Cut Development Document — Validation Checklist

Use this checklist as a chain-of-thought framework when generating new video concepts.
For each section, explicitly answer every question before proceeding to the next.

---

## 1. Clear Loop
- **Which type of core loop is it?**
  Must have a repeatable mechanic: Prompt→Response→Reaction→Next, or similar.
  Loop types: Gatekeeper, Truth/Pressure, Reaction, Mystery, Social Elimination, Gamble, Compatibility/Pairing, Transaction, Challenge Trial, Shock Truth, Transformation, Commit/Reveal.
- **What are the psychological drivers of this loop?**
  Psych drivers: Status Sensitivity, Belonging vs Rejection, Loss Aversion, Curiosity/Information Gap, Dopamine/Anticipation, Emotional Contagion, Moral Judgment, Fairness/Inequity Aversion, Schadenfreude, Competence/Mastery, Contrast/Visible Change, Narrative Closure/Zeigarnik, Prediction Error.
- **Loop structure: Hook → Tension → Resolution → Output?**
  Each cycle must have all four beats. The loop creates small cycles of tension and resolution, over and over.
- **Does each single instance of the hook drive delight?**
  Every iteration should be independently enjoyable — not just setup for the next one.
- **How do the loops escalate?**
  Light tension → deeper tension → risky moment → outcome. Escalation increases emotional investment.

## 2. Strong Packaging
- **What are the clear stakes (social or personal)?**
  The packaging should make the emotional risk instantly legible from the first frame.
- **Clear one-sentence title?**
  The concept should be understandable in one sentence and one image. E.g. "Press the button to reject your date."
- **What is the curiosity gap?**
  What is the thing the audience HAS to find out? Curiosity creates cognitive tension that drives the click.
- **Why is it clearly delightful?**
  Even when stakes are high, the show should feel playful and enjoyable.
- **Why is it clearly provocative?**
  Cut pushes boundaries in ways that surprise. Every episode has instantly legible emotional risk.

## 3. Reason to Watch to The End
- **Is there a reveal, decision, rejection, or final outcome?**
  Each episode should feel like a small emotional story with a destination.
- **Does something emotionally change?**
  Attraction grows/weakens, confidence collapses/grows, someone gets exposed, someone changes their mind.
- **Are there surprise moments?**
  Brutal honesty, unexpected reactions, sudden emotional turns. Surprise resets attention and creates shareable moments.

## 4. Strong Characters
- **All unfiltered?**
  Cast people who are emotionally open and fearless, willing to say the quiet parts out loud.
- **Specific character archetypes present?**
  Hero, Villain, Underdog, Overconfident Player, Chaotic Wildcard, Super Fun, Heartwarming.
- **Multiple reveals built in?**
  Hidden truths, identity surprises, or emotional reveals that punctuate the episode.

## 5. Early Retention (First 3 Minutes)
- **How are you dropped into the action?**
  Start with tension, not explanation. Bold question, risky moment, or surprising reaction.
- **How do we capture curiosity in first 30s?**
  Within the first moments: what is happening, what could go wrong, why they should keep watching.
- **What is the 1-minute escalation?**
  By 60 seconds the stakes must have visibly increased from the opening.
- **What drives early spikes and investment?**
  Preview of payoff, personality clash, first uncomfortable moment — something that locks commitment.

## 6. Social & Personal Stakes
- **Something meaningful is at risk?**
  Rejection, Embarrassment, Judgment, Exposure, Relationship, Personal.
- **Emotional courage is visible?**
  Cut formats work best when vulnerability is on display — people choosing courage without knowing the reward.

## 7. Escalation
- **Episode builds intensity over time?**
  Structure moves from light tension → deeper tension → risky moment → outcome.
- **Multiple escalation dimensions?**
  Combine emotional + social + physical. Not just one axis.

## 8. Fun, Delight & Authenticity
- **Does it feel playful despite high stakes?**
  Cut is fun, lighthearted, delightful. Game-like mechanics that apply pressure to revelation and emotional truth.
- **Does it feel real and intimate?**
  No posturing, no performative polish. Just honest, unfiltered, imperfect expression.

## 9. The Cut Formula: Topic + Loop + Setup
- **Topic identified?**
  Dating & Attraction | Secrets, Sex & Taboo | Relationship Drama with History | Ranking & Social Perception.
- **Loop type identified?**
  Gatekeeper, Truth/Pressure, Mystery, Compatibility, Gamble, Elimination, etc.
- **Setup amplifier?**
  In the dark, trapped together, meeting for the first time, live audience, time limit, physical proximity.

## 10. Brand Alignment
- **Provocative?** Pushes boundaries, surprises. Instantly legible emotional risk from the first frame.
- **Playful?** Fun, lighthearted. Loops guarantee delight through game-like mechanics.
- **Heartfelt?** Leaves you with something heartwarming. People choosing courage creates depth.
- **Thoughtful?** Kindness, intention, depth for participants. Never exploits just for clicks.
- **Real?** No posturing. Intimate, honest, unfiltered. Cast real, unaffected people.
